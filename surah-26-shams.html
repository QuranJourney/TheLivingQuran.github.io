Surah Ash-Shams

The Sun • Makkah • 15 Verses
The Ultimate Thesis of the Soul. Allah swears the longest chain of consecutive oaths in the Quran—by the cosmos and its opposites—to deliver one absolute truth: Success is entirely dependent on purifying the soul, and failure is entirely due to corrupting it.
Verse 1
وَٱلشَّمْسِ وَضُحَىٰهَا
"By the sun and its brightness,"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the magnificent sun and the radiant, life-giving light it emits in the early morning.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah begins a series of 11 oaths (the longest sequence in the Quran). To swear by the sun is to point to the most obvious, undeniable, and universally necessary physical reality in human existence.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Just as the physical sun illuminates the physical world, the "Sun" of divine revelation (the Quran/Prophethood) illuminates the spiritual world. Without the sun, the earth dies in freezing darkness; without revelation, the soul dies in the cold darkness of ignorance.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The cosmos is all darkness. It is illumined only by the manifestation of God in it." The sun has no light of its own; it is merely a mirror reflecting the creative power of its Maker.

Verse 2
وَٱلْقَمَرِ إِذَا تَلَىٰهَا
"And [by] the moon when it follows it,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the moon as it rises and follows the sun, providing light in the darkness.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The sequence of oaths is establishing the concept of duality and pairs. The sun dominates the day, and the moon faithfully takes over its shift in the night. They are bound by an unbreakable cosmic discipline.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
If the Sun is the Prophet ﷺ (the source of light), the Moon is the spiritually purified scholar or saint who "follows it." The moon has no light of its own; it merely reflects the light of the sun into the dark night. A true guide only reflects Prophetic light.

Verse 3
وَٱلنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلَّىٰهَا
"And [by] the day when it displays it,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the bright daytime when it fully reveals the sun and exposes the earth.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Sometimes He gives to you and deprives you; and sometimes He deprives you and gives to you." The day represents the state of spiritual expansion (Bast) and clarity, where good deeds feel easy and faith feels brilliant.

Verse 4
وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَىٰهَا
"And [by] the night when it covers it,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the night when its darkness covers the sun, plunging the world into shadows.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The constant alternation of day and night proves that change is the only constant in creation. Nothing in this world is permanent; only the Creator of the day and night remains unchanged.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The night represents the state of spiritual contraction (Qabd) or trials, where the light of faith feels covered or hidden. The true seeker worships the Lord of the Sun just as faithfully during the dark Night of the soul.

Verse 5
وَٱلسَّمَآءِ وَمَا بَنَىٰهَا
"And [by] the sky and He who constructed it,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the vast canopy of the sky, and by the incredible power of the God who built it without pillars.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Notice the shift. First, Allah swore by the created objects (Sun, Moon). Now, He swears by the object *and* the active divine force that made it. It elevates the listener's mind from admiring the art to awing the Artist.

Verse 6
وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَمَا طَحَىٰهَا
"And [by] the earth and He who spread it,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the earth, and by the majestic power of the God who flattened and smoothed it out to be habitable.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The sky represents elevation and the spiritual realm; the earth represents humility and the physical realm. You are a bridge between the two: made of the dirt of the earth, but carrying the breath of the sky.

Verse 7
وَنَفْسٍۢ وَمَا سَوَّىٰهَا
"And [by] the soul and He who proportioned it"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the human soul, and by the God who designed it with perfect balance and intricate proportion.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This is the pinnacle of the oaths. Allah equates the complexity and majesty of the human soul to the complexity of the sun, moon, sky, and earth. The inner universe of the human mind is just as vast as the outer cosmos.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Sawwaha" (proportioned it) means God gave the soul a perfect baseline balance (Fitrah). It is naturally inclined to equilibrium. When you sin, you violate your own internal physics, causing the soul to fall out of proportion and suffer anxiety.

Verse 8
فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَىٰهَا
"And inspired it [with discernment of] its wickedness and its righteousness,"
Plain Understanding
God fundamentally wired into the human soul the intuitive ability to recognize what is deeply wicked (sin) and what is deeply righteous (God-consciousness).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This destroys the excuse of ignorance. Even before a Prophet arrives, every human being has a conscience. You intuitively feel a knot in your chest when you steal (Fujur), and you feel expansion when you give charity (Taqwa).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
You contain both the capacity of Pharaoh and the capacity of Abu Bakr. The ego leans toward Fujur (bursting boundaries); the spirit leans toward Taqwa (guarding boundaries). Your life is the battlefield between these two inspired forces.

Verse 9
قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن زَكَّىٰهَا
"He has succeeded who purifies it,"
Plain Understanding
This is the central thesis of the entire Surah: True, ultimate, and absolute success belongs exclusively to the person who actively purifies and cleanses their soul.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This is the definition of Sufism (Tazkiyat al-Nafs). The Arabic word "Zakka" means both to "purify" (remove the weeds of envy, anger, and lust) and to "grow" (plant the seeds of mercy, patience, and love). You cannot grow the flower until you pull the weed.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Success (Falah) in the Dunya is measured by what you accumulate (wealth). Success in the Akhirah is measured by what you eliminate (sins from the heart). "The day when neither wealth nor sons will avail, except him who brings to Allah a sound heart."

Verse 10
وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّىٰهَا
"And he has failed who instills it [with corruption]."
Plain Understanding
And total ruin, failure, and despair belong to the person who corrupts, buries, and pollutes their soul with sin.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Dassaha" means to bury something deep in the dirt to hide it. The sinner buries their pure, illuminated soul under layers of dark, filthy sins until the conscience is entirely smothered and can no longer breathe or see the light.

Verse 11
كَذَّبَتْ ثَمُودُ بِطَغْوَىٰهَآ
"Thamud denied [their prophet] by reason of their transgression,"
Plain Understanding
A historical case study of a buried soul: The ancient tribe of Thamud called the truth a lie because their arrogance and rebellion (Tughyan) completely took over.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Denial of truth is rarely an intellectual problem; it is almost always a disease of the ego. They didn't reject the Prophet Salih because he lacked evidence; they rejected him because accepting his authority would crush their "Tughyan" (their arrogant desire to be their own gods).

Verse 12
إِذِ ٱنبَعَثَ أَشْقَىٰهَا
"When the most wretched of them was sent forth."
Plain Understanding
This happened when the most evil, miserable, and corrupt man among them stepped forward to do the unthinkable.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
His name was Qudar ibn Salif. He was the most wicked man in the tribe. The entire tribe cheered him on as he volunteered to execute their treacherous plan to kill the miraculous she-camel that God had sent as a test.

Verse 13
فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ نَاقَةَ ٱللَّهِ وَسُقْيَـٰهَا
"And the messenger of Allah [Salih] said to them, '[Do not harm] the she-camel of Allah or [prevent her from] her drink.'"
Plain Understanding
The Prophet Salih desperately warned them: "Beware! Leave God's camel alone, and do not block her from her turn to drink water!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The she-camel is a symbol of God's sacred boundaries (Hurumat). Every person has a "she-camel" in their life—a clear, undeniable command from God that they are tested with. Do not strike the boundary, or you invite the consequence.

Verse 14
فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا فَدَمْدَمَ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُم بِذَنۢبِهِمْ فَسَوَّىٰهَا
"But they denied him and hamstrung her. So their Lord brought down upon them destruction for their sin and made it equal [upon all of them]."
Plain Understanding
But they ignored the Prophet, violently sliced the legs of the camel, and killed her. As a direct result, their Master unleashed a crushing, flattening destruction upon them because of their sin, wiping them all out equally.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Even though only one man (Qudar) killed the camel, the entire tribe was destroyed. Why? Because they all supported, cheered, and enabled him. Silence in the face of violent injustice makes you complicit in the crime. "Damdama" implies a punishment that completely envelops and crushes, leaving nothing standing.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Notice the word "Sawwaha" (made it equal). In Verse 7, God "proportioned/equalized" (Sawwaha) the human soul for greatness. Because they destroyed that inner equilibrium with sin, God sent an external punishment that "equalized" (Sawwaha) them with the dirt. The external punishment matches the internal crime.

Verse 15
وَلَا يَخَافُ عُقْبَـٰهَا
"And He does not fear the consequence thereof."
Plain Understanding
And unlike human kings, God does not fear any backlash, revenge, or consequence for destroying them.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
When a human king destroys a powerful tribe, he lives in constant fear that their allies will seek revenge. God acts with absolute, sovereign impunity. He annihilated a superpower, and there is no power in the cosmos that can question Him or retaliate.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Thamud did not fear the consequence of killing the camel, so God destroyed them with a power that fears no consequence. The height of delusion is acting recklessly in the kingdom of a King who cannot be challenged.

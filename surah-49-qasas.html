Surah Al-Qasas

The Narrative • Makkah • 88 Verses
The Anatomy of Divine Decree. A profound, chronological biography of Moses, demonstrating how God's delicate, invisible orchestration effortlessly shatters the loudest, most violent machinations of worldly tyrants.
Verse 1
طسٓمٓ
"Ta, Seen, Meem."
Plain Understanding
The Surah opens with three disconnected Arabic letters, establishing the miraculous, divine origin of the text.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Like Surah Ash-Shu'ara, this chapter begins with "Ta, Seen, Meem," signaling to the Quraysh that the ensuing narrative is constructed from their own alphabet, yet impossible for their greatest poets to replicate.

These following verses belong to the Book that makes everything exceptionally clear and distinct.

"Al-Mubeen" (The Clarifying). Human history is often a chaotic, confusing web of politics and violence. The Qur'an acts as the ultimate clarifying lens, peeling back the geopolitical layers of ancient Egypt to reveal the pristine, underlying engine of Divine Justice operating beneath it all.

Verse 3
نَتْلُوا۟ عَلَيْكَ مِن نَّبَإِ مُوسَىٰ وَفِرْعَوْنَ بِٱلْحَقِّ لِقَوْمٍۢ يُؤْمِنُونَ
"We recite to you from the news of Moses and Pharaoh in truth for a people who believe."
Plain Understanding
God tells the Prophet ﷺ: "We are going to accurately recite to you the true, factual history of Moses and Pharaoh, specifically as a lesson and guidance for people who have faith."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Bil-Haqq" (In truth). The stories of Moses had been altered and mythologized by the Israelites over centuries. God asserts that this recitation is the raw, uncorrupted, objective truth. It is delivered "for a people who believe" because only believers extract spiritual medicine from history; disbelievers merely read it as an entertaining ancient fable.

Verse 4
إِنَّ فِرْعَوْنَ عَلَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَجَعَلَ أَهْلَهَا شِيَعًۭا يَسْتَضْعِفُ طَآئِفَةًۭ مِّنْهُمْ يُذَبِّحُ أَبْنَآءَهُمْ وَيَسْتَحْىِۦ نِسَآءَهُمْ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ كَانَ مِنَ ٱلْمُفْسِدِينَ
"Indeed, Pharaoh exalted himself in the land and made its people into factions, oppressing a sector among them, slaughtering their [newborn] sons and keeping their females alive. Indeed, he was of the corrupters."
Plain Understanding
The setting is established: Pharaoh became a wildly arrogant, violent tyrant in Egypt. He divided the population into classes to control them, severely oppressing the Israelites. He initiated a genocide, massacring their baby boys and keeping the women alive for slavery. He was the ultimate source of systemic corruption.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The anatomy of tyranny. "Ala fil-ard" (exalted himself/acted arrogantly) is the spiritual root. "Shiya'an" (factions) is the political strategy: divide and conquer. By violently fracturing society into ethnic hierarchies, the ego of the tyrant maintains absolute control. The slaughter of infants is the ultimate manifestation of "Fasad" (corruption)—destroying the future of a people out of paranoid fear for one's own throne.

Verse 5
وَنُرِيدُ أَن نَّمُنَّ عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ ٱسْتُضْعِفُوا۟ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَنَجْعَلَهُمْ أَئِمَّةًۭ وَنَجْعَلَهُمُ ٱلْوَٰرِثِينَ
"And We wanted to confer favor upon those who were oppressed in the land and make them leaders and make them inheritors"
Plain Understanding
But God possessed a vastly different plan: "We desired to grant a massive blessing to the very people who were being tortured and oppressed. We decided to transform them from helpless slaves into the elite leaders and the inheritors of the earth."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The clash of wills. Pharaoh willed ("Yudhabihu" - he slaughters) to extinguish them; God willed ("Nureedu" - We wanted) to elevate them. When the will of the creation collides with the will of the Creator, the physics of reality immediately bend to serve Heaven. The very condition of "Istid'af" (being utterly weakened and oppressed) is what activates the fierce, protective Grace ("Mann") of the Almighty.

Verse 6
وَنُمَكِّنَ لَهُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَنُرِىَ فِرْعَوْنَ وَهَـٰمَـٰنَ وَجُنُودَهُمَا مِنْهُم مَّا كَانُوا۟ يَحْذَرُونَ
"And establish them in the land and show Pharaoh and Haman and their soldiers through them that which they feared."
Plain Understanding
"And We intended to give these slaves absolute political power in the land, and to force Pharaoh, his chief minister Haman, and their entire military to personally witness the exact nightmare they had been terrified of all along."

Pharaoh slaughtered the infants because his astrologers prophesied that a boy from the Israelites would destroy his empire. God's poetic justice is flawless: He does not merely defeat Pharaoh; He ensures that Pharaoh's desperate attempts to prevent the prophecy become the very mechanism that fulfills it. "Ma kanu yahdharoon" (that which they feared) will strike them directly from within their own palace.

Verse 7
وَأَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَىٰٓ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰٓ أَنْ أَرْضِعِيهِ ۖ فَإِذَا خِفْتِ عَلَيْهِ فَأَلْقِيهِ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ وَلَا تَخَافِى وَلَا تَحْزَنِىٓ ۖ إِنَّا رَآدُّوهُ إِلَيْكِ وَجَاعِلُوهُ مِنَ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ
"And We inspired to the mother of Moses, 'Suckle him; but when you fear for him, cast him into the river and do not fear and do not grieve. Indeed, We will return him to you and will make him [one] of the messengers.'"
Plain Understanding
God initiated the plan by placing an inspiration into the heart of Moses' mother: "Nurse your baby. But the moment you hear the soldiers coming, put him in a basket and throw him into the Nile River! Do not be afraid of him drowning, and do not grieve over losing him. I promise you, We will bring him right back to you, and We will make him a great Prophet!"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ultimate test of Tawakkul (Trust). A mother's biological instinct is to hold her child tightest when danger approaches. God commands her to override her biology and cast her infant into the chaotic, deadly currents of the Nile ("Al-Yamm"). The verse contains two commands (nurse, cast), two prohibitions (do not fear, do not grieve), and two massive promises (return him, make him a Prophet). God secures the heart of the mother with absolute theological certainty before demanding the impossible physical sacrifice.

Verse 8
فَٱلْتَقَطَهُۥٓ ءَالُ فِرْعَوْنَ لِيَكُونَ لَهُمْ عَدُوًّۭا وَحَزَنًا ۗ إِنَّ فِرْعَوْنَ وَهَـٰمَـٰنَ وَجُنُودَهُمَا كَانُوا۟ خَـٰطِـِٔينَ
"And the family of Pharaoh picked him up [out of the river] so that he would become to them an enemy and a [cause of] grief. Indeed, Pharaoh and Haman and their soldiers were deliberate sinners."
Plain Understanding
The basket floated directly to the palace, where Pharaoh's own family picked the baby out of the water. They had no idea they were rescuing the very child destined to become their worst enemy and greatest source of sorrow. Truly, Pharaoh, Haman, and their armies were deeply arrogant and mistaken.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Liyakoona lahum 'aduwwan" (so that he would become to them an enemy). The "Lam" (letter L) here is the 'Lam of Consequence', not purpose. Pharaoh's family picked him up out of love, entirely unaware of the catastrophic consequence. This exposes the supreme blindness of the oppressor. Pharaoh spent his entire military budget searching the slums of Egypt for the prophesied boy, while the King of the Heavens delivered the boy directly into Pharaoh's lap, forcing Pharaoh to pay for his upbringing out of the royal treasury. The irony of Divine Decree is absolute.

Verse 9

Pharaoh's wife, Asiya, instantly fell in love with the baby and pleaded with her husband: "He will bring immense joy and comfort to my eyes and yours! Please do not execute him! Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Qurratu 'Ayn" (comfort/coolness of the eye). God answers the prayers of the oppressed by planting "Mahabbah" (intense love) in the heart of the queen. Asiya (a believer whose faith was later revealed) becomes the impenetrable human shield for Moses inside the palace. Pharaoh, the mass-murderer of infants, is psychologically disarmed by his wife's tears in his own bedroom.

Verse 10
وَأَصْبَحَ فُؤَادُ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ فَـٰرِغًا ۖ إِن كَادَتْ لَتُبْدِى بِهِۦ لَوْلَآ أَن رَّبَطْنَا عَلَىٰ قَلْبِهَا لِتَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ
"And the heart of Moses' mother became empty [of all else]. She was about to disclose [the matter concerning] him had We not bound fast her heart that she would be of the believers."
Plain Understanding
Meanwhile, the heart of Moses' mother became completely hollowed out by trauma and panic. She was so devastated she almost ran into the streets screaming that he was her baby. She would have exposed the secret if We had not stepped in and firmly tied her heart down with patience, keeping her faith strong.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Asbaha fu'adu ummi Musa farighan" (her heart became completely empty). The Qur'an brilliantly captures the raw psychology of a mother's trauma. Her intellect briefly collapsed under the biological agony of losing her child. "Rabatna 'ala qalbiha" (We bound/tied her heart). Faith is not just a human effort; it is a Divine intervention. When the trial exceeds human capacity, God physically "ties" the heart to prevent it from shattering, proving that true Sabr (patience) is a gift injected by the Creator.

The sister utilizes brilliant espionage ("'an junub" - from the side/distance), acting like an innocent bystander while meticulously tracking the royal guards to see where the baby was taken. Divine Decree requires human courage to facilitate it.

Verse 12
وَحَرَّمْنَا عَلَيْهِ ٱلْمَرَاضِعَ مِن قَبْلُ فَقَالَتْ هَلْ أَدُلُّكُمْ عَلَىٰٓ أَهْلِ بَيْتٍۢ يَكْفُلُونَهُۥ لَكُمْ وَهُمْ لَهُۥ نَـٰصِحُونَ
"And We had prevented from him [all] wet nurses before, so she said, 'Shall I direct you to a household that will be responsible for him for you while they are to him [for his upbringing] sincere?'"
Plain Understanding
Inside the palace, the baby refused to eat. God made Moses naturally reject the milk of every single Egyptian nurse they brought him. Seeing their desperation, his sister casually approached the guards and said: "Should I show you a good family who can successfully nurse and raise him for you with genuine care?"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Harramna 'alayhi" (We prohibited/prevented him). God engineered a temporary biological block in the infant. Moses's refusal to drink was not stubbornness; it was the invisible hand of God creating a crisis inside the palace that only the mother of Moses could solve. The Creator creates the problem specifically so He can deliver the perfect solution.

The mother sacrificed her son into the river for the sake of God, and God returned him to her the very same day, but now with royal protection and a state-sponsored salary from Pharaoh to nurse her own child! When you surrender your most precious attachment to the Creator, He returns it to you purified and protected.

Verse 14
وَلَمَّا بَلَغَ أَشُدَّهُۥ وَٱسْتَوَىٰٓ ءَاتَيْنَـٰهُ حُكْمًۭا وَعِلْمًۭا ۚ وَكَذَٰلِكَ نَجْزِى ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ
"And when he attained his full strength and was [mentally] mature, We bestowed upon him judgement and knowledge. And thus do We reward the doers of good."
Plain Understanding
Years passed. When Moses grew up, reaching peak physical strength and intellectual maturity, We rewarded him by granting him deep wisdom, sound judgment, and profound religious knowledge. This is exactly how We generously reward those who act with excellence.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Ashuddahu" (full physical strength, usually around age 18-30) and "Istawa" (mental and emotional stability/maturity, usually around age 40). Moses was raised in the epicenter of worldly knowledge (the Egyptian royal court), but God granted him "Hukm wa 'Ilm" (Divine Wisdom and Prophetic Knowledge), which supersedes secular education. He received this because he maintained his moral excellence ("Muhsineen") despite living in a palace of corruption.

Verse 15
وَدَخَلَ ٱلْمَدِينَةَ عَلَىٰ حِينِ غَفْلَةٍۢ مِّنْ أَهْلِهَا فَوَجَدَ فِيهَا رَجُلَيْنِ يَقْتَتِلَانِ هَـٰذَا مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ وَهَـٰذَا مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ ۖ فَٱسْتَغَٰثَهُ ٱلَّذِى مِن شِيعَتِهِۦ عَلَى ٱلَّذِى مِنْ عَدُوِّهِۦ فَوَكَزَهُۥ مُوسَىٰ فَقَضَىٰ عَلَيْهِ ۖ قَالَ هَـٰذَا مِنْ عَمَلِ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنِ ۖ إِنَّهُۥ عَدُوٌّۭ مُّضِلٌّۭ مُّبِينٌۭ
"And he entered the city at a time of inattention by its people and found therein two men fighting: one from his faction and one from among his enemy. And the one from his faction called for help to him against the one from his enemy, so Moses struck him and [unintentionally] killed him. [Moses] said, 'This is from the work of Satan. Indeed, he is a manifest, misleading enemy.'"
Plain Understanding
One day, Moses quietly entered the city during a time when people were resting. He stumbled upon two men fighting in the street: one was an Israelite (his own people), and the other was an Egyptian (the oppressors). The Israelite screamed for Moses's help. Moses stepped in and threw a single punch at the Egyptian, which accidentally killed him instantly. Horrified, Moses cried out: "I didn't mean to do this! This was the whispering of Satan! He is truly an evil enemy trying to lead me astray!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Fawakazahu" (so he struck him/punched him). This verse highlights Moses's terrifying physical strength. He did not use a weapon or intend to murder; a single, bare-handed strike to break up the fight was lethal. Moses's immediate reaction is profound. He does not justify the killing by saying "He was an oppressive Egyptian." He instantly recognizes the tragedy as a manipulation of anger, diagnosing it as "'Amal ash-Shaytan" (the work of Satan). The Prophet claims absolute accountability for his mistake.

Verse 16
قَالَ رَبِّ إِنِّى ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِى فَٱغْفِرْ لِى فَغَفَرَ لَهُۥٓ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلْغَفُورُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ
"He said, 'My Lord, indeed I have wronged myself, so forgive me,' and He forgave him. Indeed, He is the Forgiving, the Merciful."
Plain Understanding
Stricken with crushing guilt, Moses prayed: "My Master! I have committed a terrible wrong against my own soul. Please forgive me!" And God immediately forgave him. Truly, God is the Most Forgiving, the Most Merciful.

"Dhalamtu nafsi" (I have wronged myself). The exact same confession made by Adam in Paradise. Moses does not blame the Egyptian, the Israelite, or his own strength. He takes 100% ownership of the sin. Because the repentance was immediate, raw, and untainted by ego, the Divine response ("Faghafara lahu" - and He forgave him) was instantaneous. God does not demand perfection; He demands rapid, sincere accountability.

Verse 18

When he sees the same Israelite fighting again, Moses recognizes the man is an instigator. Moses refuses to enable "Asabiyyah" (blind tribal loyalty). Justice demands condemning the criminal behavior of your own people just as harshly as you condemn the enemy.

Verse 19
فَلَمَّآ أَنْ أَرَادَ أَن يَبْطِشَ بِٱلَّذِى هُوَ عَدُوٌّۭ لَّهُمَا قَالَ يَـٰمُوسَىٰٓ أَتُرِيدُ أَن تَقْتُلَنِى كَمَا قَتَلْتَ نَفْسًۢا بِٱلْأَمْسِ ۖ إِن تُرِيدُ إِلَّآ أَن تَكُونَ جَبَّارًۭا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَمَا تُرِيدُ أَن تَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلْمُصْلِحِينَ
"And when he wanted to strike the one who was an enemy to both of them, he said, 'O Moses, do you intend to kill me as you killed someone yesterday? You only want to be a tyrant in the land and do not want to be of the amenders.'"
Plain Understanding
Despite his frustration with the Israelite, Moses stepped forward to stop the Egyptian enemy. But the frightened Israelite, thinking Moses was going to hit *him* for being a troublemaker, panicked and exposed the secret: "Moses! Are you going to murder me just like you murdered that guy yesterday?! You're just trying to act like a violent, brutal tyrant in this city, not someone who wants to fix things!"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The devastating betrayal of the weak ego. The Israelite, in a moment of cowardice, publicly broadcasts the classified information of yesterday's manslaughter, inadvertently informing the Egyptian that Moses is a wanted killer. He weaponizes Moses's guilt against him, accusing the Prophet of being a "Jabbar" (tyrant). The secret is out, and Moses's life is immediately forfeit under Egyptian law.

"So he left it, fearful and anticipating [apprehension]. He said, 'My Lord, save me from the wrongdoing people.'"
Plain Understanding
Moses didn't hesitate. He fled the city immediately, terrified and constantly looking back to see if the police were chasing him. As he ran into the desert, he prayed desperately: "My Master! Please rescue me and save me from these oppressive people!"

Yet, his first instinct is not to complain, but to invoke the "Rabb" (Master). This moment of absolute vulnerability and exile marks the transition from worldly prince to divine Prophet. The breaking of the ego in the desert is the prerequisite for carrying the Torah.

God ends with "Afala ta'qiloon" (Will you not use reason?), proving that chasing the Dunya at the expense of the Akhirah is not just a moral failure; it is a failure of basic, logical intelligence.

وَقَالَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ وَيْلَكُمْ ثَوَابُ ٱللَّهِ خَيْرٌۭ لِّمَنْ ءَامَنَ وَعَمِلَ صَـٰلِحًۭا وَلَا يُلَقَّىٰهَآ إِلَّا ٱلصَّـٰبِرُونَ
"But those who were granted true spiritual knowledge said, 'Woe to you! The eternal reward of God is infinitely better for anyone who believes and does righteous deeds. Yet, no one will be granted this high station except those who are deeply patient.'"
Plain Understanding
But the deeply religious scholars in the crowd immediately rebuked them: "Shame on you! Stop drooling over his cheap gold! The reward God has waiting in Heaven is infinitely better and richer than this! But only the people who are patient enough to avoid greed will ever get to see it."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The confrontation of two different types of "Knowledge" ('Ilm). Qarun claimed he had the 'Ilm of economics (Verse 78). The righteous believers possess the 'Ilm of the Hereafter. The scholars diagnose the optical illusion of the Dunya: Qarun's parade is merely a temporary mirage. They inject "Sabr" (patience) as the absolute antidote to envy. You must be patient enough to endure the poverty of the Dunya to inherit the palaces of the Akhirah.

Verse 81
فَخَسَفْنَا بِهِۦ وَبِدَارِهِ ٱلْأَرْضَ فَمَا كَانَ لَهُۥ مِن فِئَةٍۢ يَنصُرُونَهُۥ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَمَا كَانَ مِنَ ٱلْمُنتَصِرِينَ
"Consequently, We caused the earth to violently split open and swallow him and his entire mansion. He had absolutely no army or faction to protect him from God, nor was he capable of saving himself."
Plain Understanding
Suddenly, God executed His justice. The ground violently ripped open right beneath Qarun's feet, and the earth swallowed him, his gold, and his massive mansion completely alive! None of his bodyguards or wealth could save him, and he could not protect himself.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Fakhasafna" implies a sudden, terrifying sinking or collapsing of the earth. Qarun boasted that his wealth made him heavy and immovable on the earth. God turns that very heaviness against him. The earth, which Qarun arrogantly marched upon in his parade, opens its mouth and consumes the billionaire. Gravity becomes the instrument of Divine Execution. All the gold returned to the dirt from which it was mined.

Verse 82
وَأَصْبَحَ ٱلَّذِينَ تَمَنَّوْا۟ مَكَانَهُۥ بِٱلْأَمْسِ يَقُولُونَ وَيْكَأَنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَبْسُطُ ٱلرِّزْقَ لِمَن يَشَآءُ مِنْ عِبَادِهِۦ وَيَقْدِرُ ۖ لَوْلَآ أَن مَّنَّ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْنَا لَخَسَفَ بِنَا ۖ وَيْكَأَنَّهُۥ لَا يُفْلِحُ ٱلْكَـٰفِرُونَ
"And by the next morning, the very people who had been wishing to be in his position the day before were saying, 'Ah! It is God alone who expands the provision for whomever He wills among His servants, and He restricts it. If God had not shown us immense grace, He could have easily made the earth swallow us too! Ah, it is undeniably true that the disbelievers will never succeed.'"
Plain Understanding
The very next morning, the exact same people who were crying out of jealousy yesterday stared at the massive sinkhole in horror. They realized their mistake and said: "Wow. God gives wealth to whoever He wants, and He holds it back. Thank God He didn't answer our prayers yesterday! If He had given us Qarun's wealth, the earth would have swallowed us too! It is so obvious now: arrogant, ungrateful people never actually win in the end."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The immediate cure of the envious heart. The masses realized that what they perceived as "deprivation" (being poor) was actually "protection" (Divine Grace). God restricted their wealth ("Yaqdiru") to prevent them from developing the arrogance that would trigger their destruction. Sometimes, the greatest mercy God can bestow upon a servant is to firmly deny their worldly prayers.

Verse 83
تِلْكَ ٱلدَّارُ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةُ نَجْعَلُهَا لِلَّذِينَ لَا يُرِيدُونَ عُلُوًّۭا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَا فَسَادًۭا ۚ وَٱلْعَـٰقِبَةُ لِلْمُتَّقِينَ
"That magnificent home of the Hereafter - We exclusively reserve it for those who do not seek arrogant dominance on the earth, nor do they spread corruption. And the ultimate, beautiful conclusion belongs entirely to those who are mindful of God."
Plain Understanding
"That beautiful, eternal mansion in Paradise—We only give it to people who do not use their lives trying to dominate others, show off, or cause corruption on earth. The final victory always belongs to the humble, God-fearing people."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The absolute, defining thesis of Surah Al-Qasas. Pharaoh sought "Uluwwan" (political arrogance/dominance). Qarun sought "Fasadan" (economic corruption). Both were utterly destroyed. God explicitly closes the door of Jannah to anyone who harbors the desire to forcefully elevate their ego over others. True elevation is only achieved through absolute prostration to the King.

Verse 84
مَن جَآءَ بِٱلْحَسَنَةِ فَلَهُۥ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْهَا ۖ وَمَن جَآءَ بِٱلسَّيِّئَةِ فَلَا يُجْزَى ٱلَّذِينَ عَمِلُوا۟ ٱلسَّيِّـَٔاتِ إِلَّا مَا كَانُوا۟ يَعْمَلُونَ
"Whoever comes forward with a beautiful, good deed will receive a reward far better than it. But whoever comes forward with evil deeds - those who commit evil will not be punished with anything more than exactly what they used to do."
Plain Understanding
"God's justice is perfect: If you do a good deed, God will reward you with something mathematically much greater than what you did. But if you commit a sin, God will only punish you exactly in proportion to what you did—He will never exaggerate the punishment."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The staggering generosity of the Divine Court. "Khayrun minha" (better than it) guarantees that a single unit of good is met with a multiplied, superior return (often a minimum of ten times its value). Conversely, the punishment for a sin is capped precisely at its face value. Mercy is an explosive multiplier; Justice is a strict limitation.

Verse 85
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِى فَرَضَ عَلَيْكَ ٱلْقُرْءَانَ لَرَآدُّكَ إِلَىٰ مَعَادٍۢ ۚ قُل رَّبِّىٓ أَعْلَمُ مَن جَآءَ بِٱلْهُدَىٰ وَمَنْ هُوَ فِى ضَلَـٰلٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ
"Indeed, the One who explicitly obligated you to recite and follow the Qur'an will undoubtedly bring you back to an ultimate place of return. Say to them, 'My Lord knows perfectly well who is bringing true guidance, and who is completely lost in obvious error.'"
Plain Understanding
God comforts Prophet Muhammad ﷺ directly: "I promise you, the exact same God who commanded you to preach this Qur'an will definitely bring you back to your home (Mecca) in victory. Tell your enemies: 'My Master knows exactly who is guided and who is completely lost!'"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse was physically revealed while the Prophet ﷺ was in the midst of Hijrah (the terrifying migration from Mecca to Medina), hiding in the Cave of Thawr or traveling the desert road. He was heartbroken to leave his beloved hometown. God draws a parallel with Moses. Just as Moses fled Egypt in fear (Verse 21) but returned as a triumphant Prophet, Muhammad ﷺ is guaranteed a victorious "Ma'ad" (place of return) to Mecca. Eight years later, the promise was fulfilled.

Verse 86
وَمَا كُنتَ تَرْجُوٓا۟ أَن يُلْقَىٰٓ إِلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبُ إِلَّا رَحْمَةًۭ مِّن رَّبِّكَ ۖ فَلَا تَكُونَنَّ ظَهِيرًۭا لِّلْكَـٰفِرِينَ
"And you never expected or imagined that this Book would be cast down into your heart; it was granted only as a pure mercy from your Master. Therefore, never act as a supporter or backer for those who reject the truth."
Plain Understanding
"You were just an ordinary man; you never expected or asked to become a Prophet receiving a holy Book! God gave this to you purely out of His love and mercy. Because of this massive favor, you must never compromise or assist the disbelievers in their corruption."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Wa ma kunta tarju" (And you did not expect). Prophethood is not an academic degree that can be earned through excessive studying or meditation; it is a violent, unilateral election by Heaven. Because the Prophet received this ultimate "Rahmah" (Mercy) freely, he is commanded to exhibit the same uncompromising loyalty that Moses exhibited in Verse 17: "I will never be an assistant to the criminals."

Verse 87
وَلَا يَصُدُّنَّكَ عَنْ ءَايَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ بَعْدَ إِذْ أُنزِلَتْ إِلَيْكَ ۖ وَٱدْعُ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ ۖ وَلَا تَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْمُشْرِكِينَ
"And absolutely do not let them block you or distract you from the verses of God after they have been revealed to you. Keep constantly inviting people to your Lord, and never become one of those who associate partners with Him."
Plain Understanding
"Do not let their insults, threats, or psychological warfare stop you from preaching God's verses. Keep calling the people to your Master, and guard your heart fiercely against ever compromising with idolaters."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Wa la yasuddunnaka" (Do not let them avert/block you). The enemies of truth will use every tool in their arsenal—mockery (like Pharaoh), bribery (like Qarun), or violence—to halt the transmission of light. The Prophet ﷺ is commanded to anchor himself in the "Da'wah" (calling to the Lord), treating the opposition's noise as irrelevant static.

Verse 88
وَلَا تَدْعُ مَعَ ٱللَّهِ إِلَـٰهًا ءَاخَرَ ۘ لَآ إِلَـٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۚ كُلُّ شَىْءٍ هَالِكٌ إِلَّا وَجْهَهُۥ ۚ لَهُ ٱلْحُكْمُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ
"And do not call upon or pray to any other supposed god alongside Allah. There is absolutely no deity except Him. Every single thing in existence will perish and be destroyed except His Face. The ultimate Judgment belongs entirely to Him, and to Him you will all be violently returned."
Plain Understanding
"Never worship anything alongside God. There is no other god! Look around you: absolutely every single thing you see—every empire, every planet, every human—will inevitably die and turn to dust. Only the majestic Face of God will remain forever. He is the ultimate Judge, and you will all be dragged back to stand in front of Him."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The breathtaking, absolute conclusion of the Surah. "Kullu shay'in halikun illa Wajhah" (Everything is perishing except His Face). The Surah has documented the death of Pharaoh, the death of Haman, the death of Qarun, and the ruin of their massive castles. The final verse acts as a cosmic scythe, mowing down the entire Dunya. Because the universe is fundamentally programmed for expiration ("Halik"), building your identity upon it is madness. The only logical anchor for the human heart is the Eternal Face of the Creator.

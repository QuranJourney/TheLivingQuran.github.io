Surah An-Naml

The Ant • Makkah • 93 Verses
The Dominion of Knowledge. This Surah explores the intersection of absolute worldly power and profound spiritual humility, featuring the legendary empire of Solomon, the wisdom of a tiny ant, and the diplomatic clash with the Queen of Sheba.
Verse 1
طسٓ ۚ تِلْكَ ءَايَـٰتُ ٱلْقُرْءَانِ وَكِتَابٍۢ مُّبِينٍ
"Ta, Seen. These are the verses of the Qur'an and a clear Book"
Plain Understanding
The Surah opens with two disconnected letters, declaring immediately that what follows are the verses of the Qur'an, a Book that makes all truths perfectly clear.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pairing of "Qur'an" (that which is recited aloud) and "Kitab" (that which is written down) establishes the dual nature of its preservation. It is protected perfectly in the oral tradition of the reciters and simultaneously secured in the physical text of the manuscript.

Verse 2
هُدًۭى وَبُشْرَىٰ لِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ
"As guidance and good tidings for the believers"
Plain Understanding
This Book functions as a precise map of guidance and a source of joyous, beautiful news specifically for those who choose to believe.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Hudan" (Guidance) protects the intellect from wandering in darkness; "Bushra" (Good tidings) protects the emotional heart from sinking into despair. The Book addresses both the mind and the soul. However, this dual benefit is strictly conditional: it only activates "lil-Mu'mineen" (for the believers). To the arrogant, it appears as a threat.

Verse 3
ٱلَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ
"Who establish prayer and give zakah, and of the Hereafter they are certain [in faith]."
Plain Understanding
These true believers are defined by three things: they consistently perform their daily prayers, they pay the obligatory charity to the poor, and they possess absolute, unshakeable certainty that the Day of Judgment is coming.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The golden triad of Islamic identity. "Salah" repairs the vertical relationship between the servant and the Creator. "Zakah" repairs the horizontal relationship between the wealthy and the poor. But "Yooqinoon" (absolute certainty in the Hereafter) is the invisible engine that powers both. Without the certainty of an eternal audit, prayer becomes a chore and charity becomes a tax.

Verse 4
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ زَيَّنَّا لَهُمْ أَعْمَـٰلَهُمْ فَهُمْ يَعْمَهُونَ
"Indeed, for those who do not believe in the Hereafter, We have made pleasing to them their deeds, so they wander blindly."
Plain Understanding
On the other hand, for those who entirely reject the Hereafter, God allows their evil and selfish actions to look beautiful and justified in their own eyes, leaving them to wander through life completely blind to the truth.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Zayyanna" (We have made pleasing/adorned). This is the terrifying punishment of "Istidraj" (gradual ruin). When a soul repeatedly rejects the accountability of the Hereafter, God removes the internal alarm system of guilt. The sinner begins to view their own corruption as brilliance and their own greed as success. "Ya'mahoon" describes the aimless, chaotic wandering of a blind animal in the desert.

Verse 5
أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَهُمْ سُوٓءُ ٱلْعَذَابِ وَهُمْ فِى ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ هُمُ ٱلْأَخْسَرُونَ
"Those are the ones for whom there will be the worst of punishment, and in the Hereafter they are the greatest losers."
Plain Understanding
Because they chose to wander blindly, they will face the most severe punishment, and when the next life begins, they will realize they have lost absolutely everything.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Al-Akhsaroon" (the greatest losers). True loss is not bankruptcy in the Dunya; it is arriving in the Akhirah having spent the entirety of your biological life investing in an illusion. They thought they were winning the world (because their deeds were "made pleasing" to them), but they were actively purchasing their own damnation.

Verse 6
وَإِنَّكَ لَتُلَقَّى ٱلْقُرْءَانَ مِن لَّدُنْ حَكِيمٍ عَلِيمٍ
"And indeed, [O Muhammad], you receive the Qur'an from one Wise and Knowing."
Plain Understanding
God reassures the Prophet ﷺ directly: "Rest assured, you are receiving this Qur'an directly from the absolute presence of the Most Wise, the All-Knowing God."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Min ladun" (from the immediate, intimate presence). The Qur'an is not merely an inspired philosophy; it is a direct download from the Source. "Hakeem" guarantees that every command in the Book is perfectly balanced for human flourishing; "Aleem" guarantees that the Book perfectly encompasses the knowledge of the past, present, and future.

Verse 7
إِذْ قَالَ مُوسَىٰ لِأَهْلِهِۦٓ إِنِّىٓ ءَانَسْتُ نَارًۭا سَـَٔاتِيكُم مِّنْهَا بِخَبَرٍ أَوْ ءَاتِيكُم بِشِهَابٍ قَبَسٍۢ لَّعَلَّكُمْ تَصْطَلُونَ
"[Mention] when Moses said to his family, 'Indeed, I have perceived a fire. I will bring you from there information or will bring you a burning torch that you may warm yourselves.'"
Plain Understanding
The Surah introduces Moses, but with a specific focus on his search for knowledge. Lost in the cold desert, he saw a fire and told his family: "I see a fire! Let me go. I will either bring back information on which way to travel, or at least bring a flaming branch so we can get warm."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Surah An-Naml is the "Surah of Knowledge." Notice Moses's priorities here compared to other Surahs: he asks for "Khabar" (information/news) *before* he asks for "Qabas" (a torch for warmth). His intellectual desperation (being lost) superseded his biological desperation (being cold). Because he sought direction, God granted him Prophethood.

Verse 8
فَلَمَّا جَآءَهَا نُودِىَ أَنۢ بُورِكَ مَن فِى ٱلنَّارِ وَمَنْ حَوْلَهَا وَسُبْحَـٰنَ ٱللَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَـٰلَمِينَ
"But when he came to it, he was called, 'Blessed is whoever is at the fire and whoever is around it. And exalted is Allah, Lord of the worlds."
Plain Understanding
When Moses approached the glowing bush, a majestic voice called out: "Blessed and holy is the one inside this fire, and blessed are those around it! And absolute glory belongs to God, the Master of the universe!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Boorika man fin-nar" (Blessed is whoever is at the fire). The light Moses saw was not an earthly combustion; it was the "Noor" (Light) of Divine manifestation. The one "around it" refers to Moses himself, and the angels attending the perimeter. God instantly transitions Moses from seeking physical warmth to witnessing the unapproachable, terrifying glory ("SubhanAllah") of the Creator.

Verse 9
يَـٰمُوسَىٰٓ إِنَّهُۥٓ أَنَا ٱللَّهُ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ
"O Moses, indeed it is I - Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Wise."
Plain Understanding
"O Moses! Understand this clearly: It is I, Allah, the Almighty who cannot be defeated, and the infinitely Wise!"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The Divine introduction. God uses the Attributes "Al-'Aziz" (Mighty) and "Al-Hakeem" (Wise). Moses is a wanted fugitive facing the greatest military empire on earth. God establishes that He possesses the Power ('Aziz) to crush Pharaoh, and the Wisdom (Hakeem) to orchestrate the exact method of liberation. The terrified fugitive is instantly heavily armed by these two Names.

Verse 10
وَأَلْقِ عَصَاكَ ۚ فَلَمَّا رَءَاهَا تَهْتَزُّ كَأَنَّهَا جَآنٌّۭ وَلَّىٰ مُدْبِرًۭا وَلَمْ يُعَقِّبْ ۚ يَـٰمُوسَىٰ لَا تَخَفْ إِنِّى لَا يَخَافُ لَدَىَّ ٱلْمُرْسَلُونَ
"And [he was told], 'Throw down your staff.' But when he saw it writhing as if it were a snake, he turned in flight and did not return. [Allah said], 'O Moses, fear not. Indeed, in My presence the messengers do not fear."
Plain Understanding
"Throw down your stick!" When Moses threw it, it turned into a fast-moving, terrifying snake. Moses panicked, turned around, and ran away as fast as he could without looking back. God called out: "Moses! Do not be afraid! True Messengers have absolutely nothing to fear when they are standing in My presence."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"La yakhafu ladayya al-mursaloon" (In My presence the messengers do not fear). Fear is generated by the possibility of harm. When you are "Ladayya" (intimately in My presence), you are entirely enveloped by the Sovereign who controls the thing you fear. To fear the snake while standing before the Creator of the snake is a temporary lapse in awareness that God immediately corrects.

Verse 11
إِلَّا مَن ظَلَمَ ثُمَّ بَدَّلَ حُسْنًۢا بَعْدَ سُوٓءٍۢ فَإِنِّى غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ
"Otherwise, he who wrongs, then substitutes good after evil - indeed, I am Forgiving and Merciful."
Plain Understanding
"The only one who should feel fear is the one who commits a sin. But even then, if that person replaces their bad deed with sincere good deeds, they will find that I am intensely Forgiving and Merciful."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This is a profound, subtle comfort to Moses. Moses was terrified of returning to Egypt because he had committed manslaughter (a "wrong"). God indirectly absolves his anxiety: If a person makes a mistake, but then immediately replaces it with repentance and righteous action (as Moses did in exile), the sin is entirely overridden by "Ghafurun Raheem."

Verse 12
وَأَدْخِلْ يَدَكَ فِى جَيْبِكَ تَخْرُجْ بَيْضَآءَ مِنْ غَيْرِ سُوٓءٍۢ ۖ فِى تِسْعِ ءَايَـٰتٍ إِلَىٰ فِرْعَوْنَ وَقَوْمِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا۟ قَوْمًۭا فَـٰسِقِينَ
"And put your hand into the opening of your garment; it will come out white without disease. [These are] among the nine signs [you will take] to Pharaoh and his people. Indeed, they have been a defiantly disobedient people.'"
Plain Understanding
"Now, slip your hand inside your shirt collar. When you pull it out, it will be shining a brilliant, flawless white. These two miracles are part of nine massive signs you will use to confront Pharaoh and his elite, because they are a deeply corrupt and rebellious nation."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "nine signs" (including the staff, the hand, the locusts, the frogs, the blood, etc.) were a calculated escalation of Divine pressure. God did not destroy Egypt with a single blow; He dismantled their arrogance methodically, proving His dominion over their biology, their agriculture, and their river, providing them nine distinct opportunities to surrender before the sea collapsed on them.

Verse 13
فَلَمَّا جَآءَتْهُمْ ءَايَـٰتُنَا مُبْصِرَةًۭ قَالُوا۟ هَـٰذَا سِحْرٌۭ مُّبِينٌۭ
"But when Our signs came to them, enlightening, they said, 'This is obvious magic.'"
Plain Understanding
But when Moses delivered these clear, eye-opening miracles directly to them, they arrogantly dismissed them, saying, "This is nothing but obvious, cheap magic tricks!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Mubsiratan" (enlightening/visible). The signs were not subtle; they possessed an undeniable, objective brilliance designed to force the eye to see. But physical vision ("Basar") is useless if the internal vision ("Basiirah") is blind. By labeling divine physics as "Sihr" (magic), they protected their ego from the terrifying requirement to submit.

Verse 14
وَجَحَدُوا۟ بِهَا وَٱسْتَيْقَنَتْهَآ أَنفُسُهُمْ ظُلْمًۭا وَعُلُوًّۭا ۚ فَٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَـٰقِبَةُ ٱلْمُفْسِدِينَ
"And they rejected them, while their [inner] souls were convinced thereof, out of injustice and haughtiness. So see how was the end of the corrupters."
Plain Understanding
They openly rejected the miracles, even though deep down inside, they were absolutely convinced they were real! They denied the truth purely out of toxic pride and a desire to maintain their oppressive power. Look at how horrific their final destruction was!
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Jahadu" means to deny something verbally while knowing internally it is true. "Istayaqanatha" indicates absolute, airtight certainty. This verse defines the apex of Kufr (disbelief). Pharaoh did not doubt Moses; Pharaoh *knew* Moses was telling the truth. His rejection was not a failure of intellect, but a failure of character ("Uluwwan" - haughtiness). He chose damnation in the Hereafter over surrendering his throne in the Dunya.

Verse 15
وَلَقَدْ ءَاتَيْنَا دَاوُۥدَ وَسُلَيْمَـٰنَ عِلْمًۭا ۖ وَقَالَا ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى فَضَّلَنَا عَلَىٰ كَثِيرٍۢ مِّنْ عِبَادِهِ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ
"And We had certainly given to David and Solomon knowledge, and they said, 'Praise [is due] to Allah, who has favored us over many of His believing servants.'"
Plain Understanding
The narrative shifts to the glorious kingdom: We granted immense, profound knowledge to Prophets David and Solomon. Their immediate reaction to this power was deep gratitude: "All praise belongs to Allah, who has honored us with this gift above many of His faithful servants!"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The absolute contrast to Pharaoh. Pharaoh was given a fraction of power and claimed "I am your Lord." David and Solomon were given a global, inter-species empire (humans, jinn, animals), yet their immediate reflex was "Alhamdulillah" (All Praise is to Allah). The true metric of a king is not the size of his army, but the depth of his humility before the True King.

Verse 16
وَوَرِثَ سُلَيْمَـٰنُ دَاوُۥدَ ۖ وَقَالَ يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ عُلِّمْنَا مَنطِقَ ٱلطَّيْرِ وَأُوتِينَا مِن كُلِّ شَىْءٍ ۖ إِنَّ هَـٰذَا لَهُوَ ٱلْفَضْلُ ٱلْمُبِينُ
"And Solomon inherited David. He said, 'O people, we have been taught the language of birds, and we have been given from all things. Indeed, this is evident bounty.'"
Plain Understanding
Solomon inherited his father David's kingdom and Prophethood. Solomon announced to his kingdom: "O people! God has taught us how to understand and speak the language of birds, and He has granted us every possible kind of blessing and power! This is undeniably a massive, clear favor from Him."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Waritha Sulayman" (Solomon inherited). Prophets do not leave behind gold or silver as inheritance; they leave behind 'Ilm (knowledge) and Nubuwwah (Prophethood). Solomon publicly acknowledges his supernatural capabilities ("language of birds"), not as a boast of his own genius, but to publicly accredit the "Fadl al-Mubeen" (evident bounty) of God, ensuring the masses worship the Giver, not the gifted.

Verse 17
وَحُشِرَ لِسُلَيْمَـٰنَ جُنُودُهُۥ مِنَ ٱلْجِنِّ وَٱلْإِنسِ وَٱلطَّيْرِ فَهُمْ يُوزَعُونَ
"And gathered for Solomon were his soldiers of the jinn and men and birds, and they were [marching] in rows."
Plain Understanding
Solomon commanded the most incredible army in human history. His military forces were perfectly assembled and marched in highly disciplined, organized rows, consisting of human infantry, powerful jinn, and squadrons of birds.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Yooza'oon" means to be kept in strict check, arranged in perfectly disciplined ranks to prevent chaos. Commanding humans requires leadership; commanding jinn requires supernatural authority; commanding birds requires cross-species linguistics. The empire of Solomon represents the absolute apex of earthly dominion ever granted to a human being, flawlessly managed by Divine wisdom.

Verse 18
حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا أَتَوْا۟ عَلَىٰ وَادِ ٱلنَّمْلِ قَالَتْ نَمْلَةٌۭ يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّمْلُ ٱدْخُلُوا۟ مَسَـٰكِنَكُمْ لَا يَحْطِمَنَّكُمْ سُلَيْمَـٰنُ وَجُنُودُهُۥ وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ
"Until, when they came upon the valley of the ants, an ant said, 'O ants, enter your dwellings that you not be crushed by Solomon and his soldiers while they perceive not.'"
Plain Understanding
As this massive, earth-shaking army marched, they approached a valley filled with ants. A single female ant sensed the danger and shouted: "O ants! Hurry and get inside your underground homes, so that Solomon and his massive army do not accidentally crush you without even realizing it!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
A breathtaking demonstration of justice. The tiny ant recognized the immense power of the King ("Solomon and his soldiers"), but it also recognized the King's moral perfection. It did not say "they will crush us out of cruelty"; it added the crucial disclaimer "Wahum la yash'uroon" (while they perceive not). Even insects in the kingdom of a just ruler know that the ruler would never execute harm intentionally.

Verse 19
فَتَبَسَّمَ ضَاحِكًۭا مِّن قَوْلِهَا وَقَالَ رَبِّ أَوْزِعْنِىٓ أَنْ أَشْكُرَ نِعْمَتَكَ ٱلَّتِىٓ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَىَّ وَعَلَىٰ وَٰلِدَىَّ وَأَنْ أَعْمَلَ صَـٰلِحًۭا تَرْضَىٰهُ وَأَدْخِلْنِى بِرَحْمَتِكَ فِى عِبَادِكَ ٱلصَّـٰلِحِينَ
"So [Solomon] smiled, amused at her speech, and said, 'My Lord, enable me to be grateful for Your favor which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents and to do righteousness of which You approve. And admit me by Your mercy into [the ranks of] Your righteous servants.'"
Plain Understanding
Solomon, hearing the tiny voice from the ground, smiled and laughed in amusement. He immediately halted the army and prayed: "My Master! Force my heart to be constantly grateful for these incredible blessings You have given me and my parents. Guide me to do good deeds that make You happy. And when I die, envelope me in Your mercy and place me among Your righteous servants."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The defining contrast of the Surah. Pharaoh demanded to be worshipped by humans; Solomon smiled affectionately at the warning of a microscopic insect. "Awzi'ni" means to compel or marshal. Solomon begs God to literally force his heart into a state of gratitude, terrified that his unprecedented power might breed arrogance. The man who commands demons begs God to accept him simply as a "righteous servant."

Verse 20
وَتَفَقَّدَ ٱلطَّيْرَ فَقَالَ مَا لِىَ لَآ أَرَى ٱلْهُدْهُدَ أَمْ كَانَ مِنَ ٱلْغَآئِبِينَ

As the Supreme Commander, Solomon inspected his troops. While reviewing the bird squadron, he noticed someone missing and asked: "Why can't I see the Hoopoe bird? Is he hiding, or is he actually absent without permission?"

"Tafaqqada" (he took attendance/investigated). A masterclass in executive leadership. Despite commanding millions of humans and jinn, Solomon's administrative grip was so flawless that he immediately noticed the absence of a single, small bird (the Hoopoe, known for its ability to detect underground water). True leadership requires meticulous attention to the micro details of the state.

Verse 21
لَأُعَذِّبَنَّهُۥ عَذَابًا شَدِيدًا أَوْ لَأَا۟ذْبَحَنَّهُۥٓ أَوْ لَيَأْتِيَنِّى بِسُلْطَـٰنٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ
"'I will surely punish him with a severe punishment or slaughter him unless he brings me clear authorization.'"
Plain Understanding
Solomon declared strict military discipline: "I will absolutely punish him severely, or even execute him for desertion, unless he returns with a completely valid, legally acceptable excuse for his absence!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Compassion (smiling at the ant) does not negate justice (punishing the deserter). Solomon maintains absolute military discipline to prevent chaos in the ranks. However, his justice is tempered by reason ("Sultanin Mubeen" - clear authorization). He does not execute blindly; he leaves the door open for the accused to provide a legitimate defense, modeling the perfect balance of Jalal (Rigor) and Jamal (Mercy).

Verse 22
فَمَكَثَ غَيْرَ بَعِيدٍۢ فَقَالَ أَحَطتُ بِمَا لَمْ تُحِطْ بِهِۦ وَجِئْتُكَ مِن سَبَإٍۭ بِنَبَإٍۢ يَقِينٍ
"But the hoopoe stayed not long and said, 'I have encompassed [in knowledge] that which you have not encompassed, and I have come to you from Sheba with certain news.'"
Plain Understanding
The Hoopoe returned shortly after. Unintimidated by the King's anger, the bird confidently stated: "I have just discovered massive, vital intelligence that you know absolutely nothing about! I bring you guaranteed, undeniable news from the distant kingdom of Sheba (Yemen)!"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Ahattu bima lam tuhit bihi" (I have encompassed what you have not). A staggering theological moment. A tiny bird looks at the most powerful, knowledgeable human king in history and boldly declares: "I know something you do not." God uses the Hoopoe to establish the boundary of human intellect. No matter how vast Solomon's empire was, his knowledge was finite. Absolute, all-encompassing knowledge belongs only to the Creator.

The Hoopoe delivered his intelligence report: "I flew over a wealthy nation ruled by a powerful Queen. She commands a highly advanced civilization, possesses immense economic wealth, and sits upon a magnificent, colossal throne."

The Hoopoe details the geopolitical reality of the Kingdom of Saba (Sheba in modern-day Yemen), ruled by Queen Bilqis. The bird acknowledges her immense worldly power and the breathtaking architecture of her "Arsh 'Adheem" (great throne), setting the stage for the ultimate clash of civilizations.

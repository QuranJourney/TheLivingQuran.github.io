Surah Luqman

The Sage • Makkah • 34 Verses
The Book of Wisdom. Contrasting the profound, heavy guidance of the Creator with the cheap, distracting entertainment of the world, serving as the perfect foundation for the immortal advice of Luqman the Sage to his son.
Verse 1
الٓمٓ
"Alif, Lam, Meem."
Plain Understanding
The Surah begins with these disconnected Arabic letters. It is a profound reminder that the Qur'an is built from the exact same letters that humans speak every day, yet no human can ever replicate its absolute perfection and majesty.

Verse 2
تِلْكَ ءَايَـٰتُ ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ ٱلْحَكِيمِ
"These are the verses of the wise Book."
Plain Understanding
"These words you are hearing are the brilliant, undeniable verses of the Book that is entirely filled with deep, perfect wisdom."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Al-Kitab al-Hakeem" (The wise Book). The Qur'an is described not merely as a container of wisdom, but as an entity that *is* wise. It acts as a living doctor for the heart, prescribing exactly the right spiritual medicine, at the right dosage, for whatever disease the soul of the reader is currently battling.

Verse 3
هُدًۭى وَرَحْمَةًۭ لِّلْمُحْسِنِينَ
"As guidance and mercy for the doers of good."
Plain Understanding
"This Book serves as an illuminated map to guide people, and a massive mercy from God, specifically designed for those who actively strive to do good with excellence."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Lil-Muhsineen" (for the people of Ihsan/excellence). The Qur'an is an open broadcast to all humanity, but its actual transformative guidance is unlocked exclusively by the "Muhsinun." Ihsan is the state of worshiping God as if you see Him. If you approach the Qur'an with a pure, sincere, and excellent heart, the text unlocks its deepest oceans of mercy for you.

Verse 4
ٱلَّذِينَ يُقِيمُونَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَيُؤْتُونَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَهُم بِٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ هُمْ يُوقِنُونَ
"Those who establish prayer and give Zakah, and they, of the Hereafter, are certain [in faith]."
Plain Understanding
"Who are these doers of good? They are the people who constantly guard their daily prayers, who happily give their required charity to the poor, and who possess an absolute, unshakable certainty that the Hereafter is real."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The three pillars of a stable spiritual architecture. "Salah" (Prayer) connects the creation to the Creator. "Zakah" (Charity) connects the creation to the rest of creation in empathy. "Yooqinoon" (Certainty in the Hereafter) is the psychological anchor that sustains the first two. Without certainty in the final audit, prayer becomes a chore and charity feels like a financial loss.

Verse 5
أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًۭى مِّن رَّبِّهِمْ ۖ وَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ
"Those are upon [true] guidance from their Lord, and it is those who are the successful."
Plain Understanding
"These specific people are standing firmly upon the perfect path of light provided by their Master. And make no mistake, they are the only ones who will achieve true, eternal success."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"'Ala hudan" (upon guidance). The preposition "'ala" implies being elevated and physically resting upon something stable. The believer does not just "have" guidance; they are mounted upon it like a rider on a strong horse, safely carrying them over the chaos of the Dunya straight into the gates of absolute success ("Muflihoon").

Verse 6
وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَشْتَرِى لَهْوَ ٱلْحَدِيثِ لِيُضِلَّ عَن سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ بِغَيْرِ عِلْمٍۢ وَيَتَّخِذَهَا هُزُوًا ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌۭ مُّهِينٌۭ
"And of the people is he who buys the amusement of speech to mislead [others] from the way of Allah without knowledge and who takes it in ridicule. Those will have a humiliating punishment."
Plain Understanding
"On the total opposite side, there are people who literally pay money to buy cheap, distracting entertainment and useless stories. Why? Just to distract themselves and mislead others away from God's path out of pure ignorance, making a joke out of religion. Because they mocked God, a deeply humiliating punishment is waiting for them."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Lahw al-Hadeeth" (amusement of speech / distracting tales). Historically, this verse references Nadhr bin al-Harith, a wealthy Meccan who traveled to Persia to buy books containing the legends of ancient kings. He brought them back to Mecca and hired singing girls to distract people whenever the Prophet ﷺ was reciting the Qur'an. It establishes the timeless reality of the "attention economy"—utilizing empty, addictive entertainment to deliberately drown out the frequency of Divine Revelation.

Verse 7
وَإِذَا تُتْلَىٰ عَلَيْهِ ءَايَـٰتُنَا وَلَّىٰ مُسْتَكْبِرًۭا كَأَن لَّمْ يَسْمَعْهَا كَأَنَّ فِىٓ أُذُنَيْهِ وَقْرًۭا ۖ فَبَشِّرْهُ بِعَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍۢ
"And when our verses are recited to him, he turns away arrogantly as if he had not heard them, as if there was in his ears a heaviness. So give him tidings of a painful punishment."
Plain Understanding
"And whenever the beautiful verses of God are recited to this kind of person, he just turns his back and walks away with pure arrogance. He acts completely deaf, as if his ears are plugged with heavy lead. So, promise him that an agonizing, painful punishment is coming for him."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The anatomy of deliberate spiritual deafness. "Mustakbiran" (acting arrogantly). His inability to hear the Qur'an is not a biological ear problem; it is an ego problem. When the heart is gorged on the cheap sugar of "Lahw" (distracting entertainment), it loses its appetite for the heavy, nutritious meat of Divine Wisdom. The "heaviness in his ears" is actually the accumulated wax of his own worldly desires blocking the Light.

Verse 8
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّـٰلِحَـٰتِ لَهُمْ جَنَّـٰتُ ٱلنَّعِيمِ
"Indeed, those who believe and do righteous deeds - for them are the Gardens of Pleasure."
Plain Understanding
"But definitely, those who hold onto their faith and constantly back it up with good, righteous actions—God has prepared for them breathtaking, magnificent Gardens overflowing with eternal pleasure."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The juxtaposition of true versus fake pleasure. The disbeliever in Verse 6 purchased "Lahw" (cheap, fake, temporary amusement) and ended up with humiliating pain. The believer purchased the "Kitab al-Hakeem" (heavy, profound Wisdom) with their patience, and ends up inheriting "Jannat an-Na'eem" (Gardens of absolute, pure, and eternal bliss). The Divine trade is always mathematically superior.

Verse 9
خَـٰلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۖ وَعْدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقًّۭا ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ
"Abiding therein eternally. The promise of Allah is truth. And He is the Exalted in Might, the Wise."
Plain Understanding
"They will live inside those beautiful Gardens forever without end. This is the absolute, unbreakable promise of God! And He alone is the All-Powerful, the All-Wise."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Wa'da Allahi Haqqan" (The promise of Allah is truth). Human promises in the Dunya are fragile because humans can either lose their wealth (unable to pay) or die (unable to deliver). God guarantees the permanence of Paradise by anchoring it to His own Attributes: He is "Al-'Aziz" (He possesses infinite power to deliver the reward) and "Al-Hakeem" (He is perfectly wise, so He will never break a covenant).

Verse 10
خَلَقَ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ بِغَيْرِ عَمَدٍۢ تَرَوْنَهَا ۖ وَأَلْقَىٰ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ رَوَٰسِىَ أَن تَمِيدَ بِكُمْ وَبَثَّ فِيهَا مِن كُلِّ دَآبَّةٍۢ ۚ وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءًۭ فَأَنۢبَتْنَا فِيهَا مِن كُلِّ زَوْجٍۢ كَرِيمٍ
"He created the heavens without pillars that you see and has cast into the earth firmly set mountains, lest it should shift with you, and dispersed therein from every creature. And We sent down rain from the sky and made grow therein [plants] of every noble kind."
Plain Understanding
"Look at God's majestic power! He created the massive, heavy sky above you without using any visible pillars to hold it up. He dropped massive mountains onto the earth like heavy anchors so the ground wouldn't constantly shake beneath your feet. He scattered millions of amazing animals across the globe. And He sends down the sweet rain from the sky to grow every single beautiful, noble type of plant."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The architecture of Divine Majesty. God challenges the arrogant man from Verse 6 (who bought cheap stories) to compare his "art" with the Art of the Creator. "Bighayri 'amadin tarawnaha" (without pillars you can see) implies the invisible pillars of gravity and astrophysics holding the cosmos in perfect suspension. To ignore this flawless, massive engineering and worship a carved piece of wood instead is the peak of intellectual blindness.

Verse 11
هَـٰذَا خَلْقُ ٱللَّهِ فَأَرُونِى مَاذَا خَلَقَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن دُونِهِۦ ۚ بَلِ ٱلظَّـٰلِمُونَ فِى ضَلَـٰلٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ
"This is the creation of Allah. So show Me what those other than Him have created. Rather, the wrongdoers are in clear error."
Plain Understanding
"God points to the entire universe and says: 'This is My creation. Now, I challenge you: show Me exactly what these fake idols and false gods of yours have ever created!' They have created absolutely nothing. Therefore, these unjust people who worship them are completely and obviously lost."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The ultimate theological checkmate. "Arunee" (Show Me!). God issues an aggressive, unanswerable challenge. If your idol, your wealth, or your societal power deserves to be worshipped, point to a single atom, a single blade of grass, or a single fly that it has created from scratch. Since the ledger of creation for everything "other than Him" is mathematically zero, redirecting your worship to them is "Dalalin Mubeen" (obvious, glaring error).

Verse 12
وَلَقَدْ ءَاتَيْنَا لُقْمَـٰنَ ٱلْحِكْمَةَ أَنِ ٱشْكُرْ لِلَّهِ ۚ وَمَن يَشْكُرْ فَإِنَّمَا يَشْكُرُ لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۖ وَمَن كَفَرَ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَنِىٌّ حَمِيدٌۭ
"And We certainly granted wisdom to Luqman, saying, 'Be grateful to God.' And whoever is grateful is grateful only for the benefit of his own soul. And whoever is ungrateful - then indeed, God is completely free of need and worthy of all praise."
Plain Understanding
"We gave Luqman deep, profound wisdom, and the very foundation of that wisdom was simple: 'Be thankful to God.' When you show gratitude, you aren't doing God a favor; you are entirely helping yourself. If someone chooses to be ungrateful, it doesn't harm God at all, because He is infinitely rich and completely independent of us."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Al-Hikmah" (Wisdom) is mathematically equated with "Shukr" (Gratitude). You cannot be wise and ungrateful simultaneously. True wisdom is recognizing the Source of all blessings. God establishes the psychological boundary of worship: "yashkuru linafsihi" (grateful for himself). Our prayers and our thanks do not increase the Kingdom of God by a single atom; they simply expand the capacity of our own hearts to receive more Light.

Verse 13
وَإِذْ قَالَ لُقْمَـٰنُ لِٱبْنِهِۦ وَهُوَ يَعِظُهُۥ يَـٰبُنَىَّ لَا تُشْرِكْ بِٱللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّ ٱلشِّرْكَ لَظُلْمٌ عَظِيمٌۭ
"And remember when Luqman said to his son while advising him, 'O my dear son, never associate partners with God. Truly, associating partners is a massive injustice.'"
Plain Understanding
"Remember the beautiful advice Luqman gave his son: 'My beloved son, never worship anything other than God, and never treat wealth or power as equal to Him. Giving God's rights to fake idols is the ultimate, most terrible form of oppression and injustice.'"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Ya bunayya" (O my dear, little son). Luqman does not lecture his son with harshness; he uses a deeply affectionate, tender Arabic diminutive. The absolute first lesson of parenting is establishing Tawheed (the Oneness of God). He diagnoses "Shirk" (polytheism) not just as a mistake, but as "Dhulm 'Adheem" (a massive injustice), because injustice is defined as placing something where it does not belong. Giving the rights of the Creator to a created object is the highest cosmic treason.

Verse 14
وَوَصَّيْنَا ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ بِوَٰلِدَيْهِ حَمَلَتْهُ أُمُّهُۥ وَهْنًا عَلَىٰ وَهْنٍۢ وَفِصَـٰلُهُۥ فِى عَامَيْنِ أَنِ ٱشْكُرْ لِى وَلِوَٰلِدَيْكَ إِلَىَّ ٱلْمَصِيرُ
"And We have commanded the human being to deeply care for his parents. His mother carried him through hardship upon hardship, and his weaning is in two years. 'Be profoundly grateful to Me and to your parents; to Me is the final destination.'"
Plain Understanding
"God steps in to add His own command: We absolutely require humans to treat their parents with excellence. Think of your mother! She carried you in her womb, suffering exhaustion upon exhaustion, and spent two years nursing you. You must be deeply grateful to Me first, and then to your parents. Never forget that you will ultimately return to Me for judgment."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Wahnan 'ala wahn" (weakness upon weakness). God specifically highlights the biological sacrifice of the mother, elevating her status immensely. The spiritual hierarchy of gratitude is established here: you cannot claim to be grateful to the invisible Creator if you are violently ungrateful to the visible parents who physically constructed your body. Honoring the mother is a direct prerequisite to honoring God.

Verse 15
وَإِن جَـٰهَدَاكَ عَلَىٰٓ أَن تُشْرِكَ بِى مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِۦ عِلْمٌۭ فَلَا تُطِعْهُمَا ۖ وَصَاحِبْهُمَا فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا مَعْرُوفًۭا ۖ وَٱتَّبِعْ سَبِيلَ مَنْ أَنَابَ إِلَىَّ ۚ ثُمَّ إِلَىَّ مَرْجِعُكُمْ فَأُنَبِّئُكُم بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ
"But if they fiercely pressure you to associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, do not obey them. However, still accompany them in this worldly life with beautiful kindness, and follow the path of those who constantly turn back to Me. Ultimately, to Me is your return, and I will inform you of everything you used to do."
Plain Understanding
"But there is a limit! If your parents try to force you to abandon your faith or worship false gods, you must respectfully disobey them. However, even if they fight your religion, you must still treat them with absolute kindness, respect, and financial care in this world. Follow the path of the sincere believers, and remember that everyone will return to God to be judged."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The perfection of Islamic boundaries. The Creator outranks the parent. But God does not allow theological disagreement to become an excuse for bad character. "Sahibhuma fil-dunya ma'roofan" (accompany them in this world with kindness). Even if a mother is an idolater actively trying to destroy your faith, you are still legally required to speak to her gently, care for her in old age, and provide for her. You disobey her theology, but you strictly honor her biology.

Verse 16
يَـٰبُنَىَّ إِنَّهَآ إِن تَكُ مِثْقَالَ حَبَّةٍۢ مِّنْ خَرْدَلٍۢ فَتَكُن فِى صَخْرَةٍ أَوْ فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ أَوْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ يَأْتِ بِهَا ٱللَّهُ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَطِيفٌ خَبِيرٌۭ
"'O my dear son, even if a deed is the weight of a tiny mustard seed and is hidden deep inside a solid rock, or scattered anywhere in the heavens or in the earth, God will bring it forth. Indeed, God is Most Subtle, Perfectly Acquainted.'"
Plain Understanding
"Luqman continues: 'My son, never think your actions don't matter! Even if you do a good or bad deed as tiny and weightless as a mustard seed, and you hide it deep inside a hollow rock, or at the edge of the galaxy, God will effortlessly find it and bring it forward on Judgment Day. God sees all hidden things and knows every microscopic detail.'"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Luqman is instilling "Muraqabah" (Divine Surveillance) into the psychology of his son. Before teaching him how to act in public, he teaches him that he is never truly in private. "Mithqala habbatin min khardal" (the weight of a mustard seed). If the All-Subtle ("Al-Lateef") can locate a microscopic seed buried in the darkness of a granite rock in outer space, He can certainly see the hidden intentions and silent whispers buried within your chest.

Verse 17
يَـٰبُنَىَّ أَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَأْمُرْ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَٱنْهَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَٱصْبِرْ عَلَىٰ مَآ أَصَابَكَ ۖ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ عَزْمِ ٱلْأُمُورِ
"'O my dear son, perfectly establish the prayer, encourage people to do what is right, gently forbid what is wrong, and be unshakably patient over whatever hardship hits you. Truly, these are the matters that require absolute determination and strength.'"
Plain Understanding
"'My son, build these powerful habits: Protect your daily prayers. Always encourage others to do good, and bravely speak out against evil. And because people will inevitably attack you for standing up for the truth, you must be incredibly patient through all the pain. Doing these things requires the highest level of human strength and resolve.'"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The transition from private theology to public sociology. Luqman warns his son: the moment you transition from "Aqim as-salah" (private prayer) to "Amr bil-ma'roof" (publicly commanding good), you will face massive societal resistance. The ego of society hates being corrected. Therefore, "Sabr" (Patience) is immediately attached as the necessary armor. To try and fix the world without the emotional armor of patience is spiritual suicide.

Verse 18
وَلَا تُصَعِّرْ خَدَّكَ لِلنَّاسِ وَلَا تَمْشِ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ مُخْتَالٍۢ فَخُورٍۢ
"'And do not turn your cheek away from people in arrogance, nor walk across the earth exultantly. Indeed, God does not love anyone who is self-deluded and boastful.'"
Plain Understanding
"'My son, never look down on anyone! Do not turn your face away from the poor or the weak out of arrogance, and do not strut around the earth acting like you are better than everyone else. God absolutely despises people who are drunk on their own egos and constantly brag about themselves.'"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The micro-expressions of arrogance. "La tusa'ir khaddaka" refers to a specific disease in camels that causes their necks to twist stiffly to the side. Luqman equates a human turning their face away from someone they deem "inferior" to an animalistic sickness. "Mukhtal" is the one whose internal self-delusion alters their external physical posture (strutting). True strength is carried with deep physical humility.

Verse 19
وَٱقْصِدْ فِى مَشْيِكَ وَٱغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ ۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلْأَصْوَٰتِ لَصَوْتُ ٱلْحَمِيرِ
"'And be moderate and balanced in your walking pace, and lower your voice. Truly, the most disagreeable of all sounds is the braying of donkeys.'"
Plain Understanding
"'Instead, walk with a calm, balanced, and confident pace—not too slow and lazy, and not too fast and frantic. And lower your voice when you speak. There is no need to shout to prove your point; the loudest, most obnoxious sound in the world is the screaming of a donkey!'"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The perfection of Adab (Manners). Luqman concludes his advice by regulating the physical volume of the human being. "Waqsid" means taking the middle, balanced path. A believer's walk should exude quiet dignity. "Waghdud min sawtik" (lower your voice). Truth does not require excessive volume to be effective. Yelling and aggressively talking over others is compared to the braying of a donkey—loud, abrasive, and entirely devoid of intellect.

Verse 20
أَلَمْ تَرَوْا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ سَخَّرَ لَكُم مَّا فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَأَسْبَغَ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعَمَهُۥ ظَـٰهِرَةًۭ وَبَاطِنَةًۭ ۗ وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يُجَـٰدِلُ فِى ٱللَّهِ بِغَيْرِ عِلْمٍۢ وَلَا هُدًۭى وَلَا كِتَـٰبٍۢ مُّنِيرٍۢ
"Do you not clearly see that Allah has subjected to your service whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth, and He has showered upon you His immense blessings, both visible and hidden? Yet among the people is he who argues about Allah without any knowledge, without guidance, and without an illuminating Book."
Plain Understanding
"Open your eyes! Don't you see that God forced the entire universe—the sun, the rain, the oceans, the animals—to serve you and keep you alive? He has drowned you in blessings, both the ones you can see (like food) and the ones you can't (like oxygen and faith). Yet, despite all this, arrogant people still dare to argue about God without possessing a shred of real knowledge or a holy book to back them up!"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Asbagha 'alaykum" (He poured/showered upon you). The universe is not a chaotic void; it is a meticulously engineered delivery system designed entirely to sustain human life ("Sakhkhara lakum"). The tragedy of the intellect is arguing against the Provider while simultaneously consuming His provisions. The ego demands proof of God while actively breathing the oxygen He just created.

Verse 21
وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمُ ٱتَّبِعُوا۟ مَآ أَنزَلَ ٱللَّهُ قَالُوا۟ بَلْ نَتَّبِعُ مَا وَجَدْنَا عَلَيْهِ ءَابَآءَنَآ ۚ أَوَلَوْ كَانَ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنُ يَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَىٰ عَذَابِ ٱلسَّعِيرِ

Mushaf: #102 | Chronological: #16

Surah At-Takathur

The Mutual Rivalry • Makkah • 8 Verses
The Illusion of 'More'. A sobering diagnosis of the human rat race. It captures how the obsessive competition to accumulate wealth, status, and numbers completely blinds us to reality, an illusion only broken by the sudden drop into the grave.
Verse 1
أَلْهَىٰكُمُ ٱلتَّكَاثُرُ
"Competition in [worldly] increase diverts you"
Plain Understanding
The obsessive desire to have *more* than others (more money, children, status) has completely distracted and preoccupied you.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Two rival tribes in Makkah (Banu Abd Manaf and Banu Sahm) were boasting about which tribe was greater. They counted their living members, but when they tied, they actually went to the graveyard to count the graves of their dead ancestors to prove who had a larger population. This verse was revealed condemning this absurd arrogance.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Alhaakum" comes from Lahw (amusement/distraction). The ego treats life as a game of high scores. The tragedy is not just that you gather wealth, but that the *process* of competing completely blinds you to your true purpose: knowing God. You are asleep, dreaming of numbers.

Verse 2
حَتَّىٰ زُرْتُمُ ٱلْمَقَابِرَ
"Until you visit the graveyards."
Plain Understanding
This distraction continues right up until the moment you die and are placed in the grave.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Notice the precise use of the word "Zurtum" (you visited). A visitor does not stay permanently. Allah calls the dead in the graves "visitors" because the grave is just a temporary transit lounge before the eternal Day of Resurrection.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"People are asleep; when they die, they wake up." The pursuit of "more" is a hypnotic trance. The cold dirt of the grave is the violent awakening from the dream of the Dunya.

Verse 3
كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ
"No! You are going to know."
Plain Understanding
No, stop this! Soon enough, you will realize the truth of your delusion.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Kalla" is a sharp rebuke to the soul. It commands the intellect to snap out of its hypnosis. You think your bank account makes you safe? Soon, when the angels of death arrive, you will *know* absolute vulnerability.
WarningThe Unseen
Verse 4
ثُمَّ كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ
"Then no! You are going to know."
Plain Understanding
Again, absolutely not! You will definitely come to know the reality of your actions.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Why repeat it? Ali (RA) and other scholars commented that the first "you will know" refers to the terrifying realization in the grave, and the second "you will know" refers to the realization on the Day of Resurrection. The terror is compounded.

Verse 5
كَلَّا لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ عِلْمَ ٱلْيَقِينِ
"No! If you only knew with knowledge of certainty..."
Plain Understanding
No! If you actually understood your reality with absolute, certain knowledge right now, you would never be distracted by worldly competition.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
There are three levels of certainty: *Ilm al-Yaqeen* (Knowledge of certainty - knowing fire burns because you see smoke), *Ayn al-Yaqeen* (Eye of certainty - seeing the flame), and *Haqq al-Yaqeen* (Truth of certainty - feeling the burn). If you had true *Ilm* now, you would weep rather than boast.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If the light of certainty were to shine in your heart, you would see the Hereafter so near that you would not need to travel to it, and you would see the eclipse of this world before its actual disappearance."
Knowledge & SignsSpiritual Practice
Verse 6
لَتَرَوُنَّ ٱلْجَحِيمَ
"You will surely see the Hellfire."
Plain Understanding
Because you lacked that certainty, you will undoubtedly see the blazing Fire with your own eyes.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This is the consequence of the "if" in the previous verse. Because they did not seek the "knowledge of certainty" through faith, they will be forced to see the reality of Hell as a punishment.

Verse 7
ثُمَّ لَتَرَوُنَّهَا عَيْنَ ٱلْيَقِينِ
"Then you will surely see it with the eye of certainty."
Plain Understanding
Then, on that Day, you will see it not as an abstract concept, but directly, vividly, with the undeniable "eye of certainty."
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The veil drops completely. What the Prophets warned about will suddenly manifest right before their irises. *Ayn al-Yaqeen* (the Eye of Certainty) leaves no room for debate, philosophy, or excuses. The ego's arguments burn away in an instant.

Verse 8
ثُمَّ لَتُسْـَٔلُنَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَنِ ٱلنَّعِيمِ
"Then you will surely be asked that Day about pleasure."
Plain Understanding
Finally, on that terrifying Day, you will be heavily interrogated about every single blessing, comfort, and luxury you enjoyed in your life.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ once left his house hungry and met Abu Bakr and Umar, who were also starving. An Ansari man invited them in, gave them shade, cold water, and fresh dates. The Prophet ﷺ ate, then wept and recited this verse: "By Him in whose Hand is my soul, you will be asked about this *Na'eem* (blessing of shade, dates, and cold water) on the Day of Judgment."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Every blessing that does not draw you closer to God is a calamity." You are not just judged on your sins; you are audited on your blessings. Did the cold water make you say Alḥamdulillah, or did it make you feel entitled? The "mutual rivalry" (Takathur) of Verse 1 is suddenly subjected to the devastating audit of Verse 8.

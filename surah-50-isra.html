Surah Al-Isra

The Night Journey • Makkah • 111 Verses (Part 1: 1-10)
The Book of the Journey. It opens with the miraculous physical transport of the Prophet ﷺ to Jerusalem, establishing the sacred link between the Prophets, and dives into the strict moral and historical laws that govern the rise and fall of civilizations.
Verse 1 ۩
سُبْحَـٰنَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِۦ لَيْلًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ إِلَى ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْأَقْصَا ٱلَّذِى بَارَكْنَا حَوْلَهُۥ لِنُرِيَهُۥ مِنْ ءَايَـٰتِنَآ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلسَّمِيعُ ٱلْبَصِيرُ
"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing."
Plain Understanding
When the earth closed its doors to the Prophet ﷺ during his deepest grief, God opened the heavens. He miraculously transported His servant from Mecca to Jerusalem in a single night, proving that the Creator of space and time can elevate His beloved above all worldly limitations.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The "Isra" (Night Journey) occurred during the "Year of Sorrow," right after the Prophet ﷺ lost his beloved wife Khadijah and his protecting uncle Abu Talib. "Bi-'abdihi" (His servant). God does not use the title "Prophet" or "Messenger" here; He uses "Servant." The highest rank a human being can attain in the Divine Court is absolute servitude. It was his perfect humility that qualified him for this unparalleled cosmic journey.

Verse 2

"And We gave Moses the Scripture and made it a perfect guidance for the Children of Israel that you not take other than Me as Disposer of affairs,"
Plain Understanding
God connects this miraculous journey directly to the ancient Prophets. He reminds us that He gave Moses the Torah with one absolute, foundational rule: Do not entrust your ultimate fate or protection to anyone in this world except the Creator.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Wakeel" (Disposer of affairs). The core disease of human empires is shifting their reliance from the Creator to their political alliances or military strength. God summarizes the entire Torah in one boundary: I am your only Protector. Do not assign divine authority to worldly powers.

"Abdan Shakoora" (A grateful servant). Noah preached for 950 years, facing endless mockery, yet God defines his supreme legacy purely by his "Shukr" (Gratitude). True gratitude is maintaining your thankfulness to the King even when the entire world has turned against you.

Verse 4
وَقَضَيْنَآ إِلَىٰ بَنِىٓ إِسْرَٰٓءِيلَ فِى ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ لَتُفْسِدُنَّ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مَرَّتَيْنِ وَلَتَعْلُنَّ عُلُوًّۭا كَبِيرًۭا
"And We explicitly conveyed to the Children of Israel in the Scripture: 'You will surely cause horrific corruption on the earth twice, and you will surely reach a degree of massive, arrogant haughtiness.'"
Plain Understanding
God warned the Israelites in their own scripture about the ultimate spiritual trap of power. Once they were given a nation and authority, they would tragically succumb to immense arrogance, leading them to spread massive corruption across the earth on two specific occasions.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Qadayna" (We decreed/conveyed authoritatively). God reveals the cyclical law of history. When an oppressed people finally gain power and wealth, they face a severe test. Bani Israel failed this test by engaging in "Fasad" (corruption) and "Uluwwan Kabeera" (massive tyranny), transitioning from victims into arrogant oppressors.

Verse 5
فَإِذَا جَآءَ وَعْدُ أُولَىٰهُمَا بَعَثْنَا عَلَيْكُمْ عِبَادًۭا لَّنَآ أُو۟لِى بَأْسٍۢ شَدِيدٍۢ فَجَاسُوا۟ خِلَـٰلَ ٱلدِّيَارِ ۚ وَكَانَ وَعْدًۭا مَّفْعُولًۭا
"So when the [time of] promise came for the first of them, We sent against you servants of Ours - those of massive, terrifying military might, and they violently probed [even] into the homes, and it was a promise [destined to be] fulfilled."
Plain Understanding
When that first wave of massive corruption peaked, God removed His divine protection. He permitted a terrifying and ruthless foreign army to invade their sacred city, tearing through their defenses and ransacking their very homes to fulfill the promise of divine justice.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The First Destruction. Historically, this refers to the Babylonian conquest led by Nebuchadnezzar. When the Israelites worshipped idols and abandoned the Torah, God allowed the Babylonians—referred to as "servants of Ours" because God uses one oppressive empire to break the arrogance of another—to completely destroy the First Temple.

Verse 6

"Then We gave back to you a return victory over them. And We powerfully reinforced you with immense wealth and sons and made you more numerous in manpower."
Plain Understanding
Yet, God’s mercy is vast. After they were utterly broken and humbled in exile, God granted them a second chance. He restored their nation, helped them overcome their enemies, and blessed them with even greater wealth and military strength than before.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The test of restoration. "Radadna lakum" (We returned to you). After their exile, they repented, and God restored their state. But notice the specific blessings: "Amwal" (wealth) and "Baneen" (sons/manpower). These are the exact elements that, if not grounded in humility, breed the very arrogance that destroyed them the first time.

Verse 7

"[And We said], 'If you do good, you strictly do good for your own souls; and if you do evil, it is entirely against them.' Then when the final terrifying promise came, [We sent your enemies] to violently sadden your faces and to enter the massive temple in Jerusalem, just as they entered it the first time, and to completely destroy whatever they conquered with absolute destruction."
Plain Understanding
God establishes a timeless moral law: Your righteousness elevates your own soul, and your evil only destroys you. Tragically, they fell into corruption again. Consequently, God allowed a second empire to invade, profoundly humiliating them, desecrating their Holy Temple once more, and reducing their civilization to absolute ruin.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Second Destruction. Despite their restoration, they fell back into deep corruption, ultimately rejecting the final Prophets sent to them. This triggered the "latter promise." In 70 CE, the Roman Empire besieged Jerusalem. The Romans "saddened their faces" (a metaphor for profound disgrace), completely burned down the Second Temple, and scattered the population, fulfilling the divine equation: arrogance guarantees destruction.

Verse 8
عَسَىٰ رَبُّكُمْ أَن يَرْحَمَكُمْ ۚ وَإِنْ عُدتُّمْ عُدْنَا ۘ وَجَعَلْنَا جَهَنَّمَ لِلْكَـٰفِرِينَ حَصِيرًا
"[Then Allah said], 'It is highly expected, [if you repent], that your Lord will have mercy upon you. But if you return [to sin], We will return [to punishment]. And We have made Hell, for the disbelievers, a suffocating prison-bed.'"
Plain Understanding
Despite two massive betrayals, God still leaves the door of mercy open, promising forgiveness if they truly repent. However, the divine law is permanently fixed: if humanity returns to tyranny and corruption, God will return to punishing them. And for those who completely reject faith, Hell is prepared as an inescapable prison.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Wa-in 'udtum 'udna" (If you return [to sin], We return [to punishment]). The eternal pendulum of Divine Justice. God responds mathematically to the state of the human heart. "Haseera" (a prison/mat). In this world, the tyrant expands his borders to feel powerful. In the Hereafter, God rolls up those borders, turning Hell into a tight, suffocating enclosure that physically binds the arrogant ego.

After demonstrating how ancient empires self-destructed through arrogance, God reveals the ultimate remedy: The Qur'an. This Book provides the most balanced and upright ethical framework to save humanity from collapse, promising an unimaginably great reward for those who believe and act with righteousness.

"Aqwam" (Most upright/straight). The Qur'an introduces itself as the ultimate architectural stabilizer for the human soul. "Aqwam" means it is perfectly balanced—neither leaning into extreme, burdensome legalism nor falling into the chaos of lawlessness. It is the definitive guide to safely navigating a civilization away from the historical cycle of ruin.

Verse 11
وَيَدْعُ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ بِٱلشَّرِّ دُعَآءَهُۥ بِٱلْخَيْرِ ۖ وَكَانَ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ عَجُولًۭا
"And man supplicates for evil exactly as he supplicates for good, and man is ever incredibly hasty."
Plain Understanding
Human beings are deeply impatient and often blinded by their immediate emotions. In moments of anger or distress, a person might recklessly pray for something that will actually destroy them, with the exact same passion they use to pray for blessings. God highlights this inherent human haste, reminding us that an unanswered prayer is often profound divine protection.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Wa kana al-insanu 'ajoola" (And man is ever hasty). God diagnoses the root of human anxiety: "Ajal" (haste). The ego demands immediate results, lacking the foresight to see long-term spiritual consequences. It prays for "Sharr" (evil) thinking it is "Khayr" (good) because it measures only short-term pleasure. To mature spiritually is to surrender your timetable entirely to the Wisdom of the Creator.

Verse 12

"And We have made the night and day two brilliant signs, and We erased the sign of the night and made the sign of the day visually illuminating that you may actively seek bounty from your Lord and that you may know the precise number of years and the account [of time]. Plain Understanding
The universe is a perfectly engineered system designed for human flourishing. God intentionally plunges the world into the darkness of night to force rest, and then unveils the illuminating day so humanity can build economies, seek sustenance, and measure time. The precision of the cosmos proves that creation is not a chaotic accident, but a meticulously detailed masterpiece.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Famahawna ayata al-layl" (We erased the sign of the night). God draws attention to the profound astronomical mercy of the Earth's rotation. The seamless transition between rest and labor provides the mathematical foundation for human civilization ("number of years and account"). Chaos is an illusion; God has categorized and detailed the physics of reality flawlessly.

Verse 13

"And [for] every single person We have permanently fastened his fate [his record of deeds] upon his own neck, and We will explicitly produce for him on the Day of Resurrection a massive record which he will encounter spread wide open."
Plain Understanding
You are the ultimate author of your own destiny. God has bound every single choice, action, and sin directly to you, like an inescapable collar around your neck. When the Day of Judgment arrives, that invisible record of choices will be transformed into a physical, undeniably clear book, thrown wide open for you to confront.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Ta'irahu fi 'unuqihi" (His bird/fate upon his neck). Ancient Arabs released a bird to divine their future fortune or doom. God violently shatters this superstition. Your destiny is not determined by omens, horoscopes, or curses. Your "fate" is the inescapable accumulation of your own free-will choices, permanently fastened to your own soul.

Verse 15
مَّنِ ٱهْتَدَىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَهْتَدِى لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۖ وَمَن ضَلَّ فَإِنَّمَا يَضِلُّ عَلَيْهَا ۚ وَلَا تَزِرُ وَازِرَةٌۭ وِزْرَ أُخْرَىٰ ۗ وَمَا كُنَّا مُعَذِّبِينَ حَتَّىٰ نَبْعَثَ رَسُولًۭا
"Whoever is guided is only guided for [the massive benefit of] his own soul. And whoever violently strays only strays strictly to its detriment. And no bearer of burdens will ever bear the burden of another. And never would We actively punish until We sent a messenger."
Plain Understanding
God establishes the foundational constitution of Divine Justice: Your righteousness benefits only you, and your sins destroy only you. You will never carry the guilt of your parents or ancestors. Furthermore, God’s justice is so perfect that He will never unleash His punishment upon a people until He has clearly sent a Messenger to deliver the truth to them first.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Wa la taziru waziratun wizra ukhra" (No bearer of burdens bears the burden of another). This verse definitively rejects the concept of inherited guilt or original sin. In the divine court, liability is strictly individualized. The closing phrase guarantees that ignorance is a valid defense if the truth never reached you; divine punishment is only unlocked *after* the undeniable delivery of the message.

Verse 16
وَإِذَآ أَرَدْنَآ أَن نُّهْلِكَ قَرْيَةً أَمَرْنَا مُتْرَفِيهَا فَفَسَقُوا۟ فِيهَا فَحَقَّ عَلَيْهَا ٱلْقَوْلُ فَدَمَّرْنَـٰهَا تَدْمِيرًۭا

How does a society collapse? The spiritual rot always begins at the top. When God tests a civilization, He issues moral commands to its wealthy, untouchable elites. Intoxicated by their wealth, these elites normalize corruption, oppression, and rebellion. Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Mutrafeeha" (Its affluent/luxury-obsessed elite). "Taraf" is wealth that leads to moral softness and arrogance. When the elite normalize "Fisq" (open rebellion), the masses inevitably follow suit. God does not destroy a city arbitrarily; the destruction is the natural, gravitational consequence of a society that removed its own ethical load-bearing walls. Wealth entirely devoid of morality acts as a civilization's detonator.

Verse 18

God permits this transaction, but strictly on His terms ("what We will, to whom We intend"). The supreme tragedy is that by demanding the Dunya as your primary objective, you get exactly what you asked for—and absolutely nothing else. To be "Mathmooman Madhoora" is to endure the ultimate spiritual bankruptcy.

Verse 19
وَمَنْ أَرَادَ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةَ وَسَعَىٰ لَهَا سَعْيَهَا وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌۭ فَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ كَانَ سَعْيُهُم مَّشْكُورًۭا
"But whoever intensely desires the Hereafter and exerts the utmost effort due to it while he is a true believer - it is those whose absolute effort is continuously appreciated [by Allah]."
Plain Understanding
In profound contrast, if your ultimate ambition is the eternal Paradise, and you validate that ambition with the exhausting, daily effort of doing good deeds while maintaining pure faith, God will never ignore your struggle. Your sacrifices will be deeply acknowledged, accepted, and met with the magnificent appreciation of the Creator Himself.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The three pillars of eternal success are laid bare: 1) "Arada" (Intention: prioritizing the eternal over the temporary). 2) "Sa'a" (Action: exerting the requisite effort. Merely wishing for Heaven without working for it is a delusion). 3) "Mu'min" (Faith: the engine validating the action). When these align, God declares the effort "Mashkoora" (Appreciated). For the King of Kings to express gratitude to a biological creation is the absolute pinnacle of Divine Love.

Verse 20

God provides for everyone in this physical world. Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Numiddu... min 'Ataa'i Rabbik" (We extend... from the gift of your Lord). This profoundly dismantles the human tendency to equate material wealth with divine favor. Wealth in the Dunya is merely a function of God's "Rububiyyah" (His role as the Universal Sustainer). Because He is the Master, He feeds both His obedient servants and His rebellious captives. Financial success simply dictates the parameters of your spiritual test.

قُلِ ٱدْعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ أَوِ ٱدْعُوا۟ ٱلرَّحْمَـٰنَ ۖ أَيًّا مَّا تَدْعُوا۟ فَلَهُ ٱلْأَسْمَآءُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ ۚ وَلَا تَجْهَرْ بِصَلَاتِكَ وَلَا تُخَافِتْ بِهَا وَٱبْتَغِ بَيْنَ ذَٰلِكَ سَبِيلًا
"Say, 'Call upon Allah or call upon the Entirely Merciful [Ar-Rahman]. Whichever you call upon - to Him exclusively belong the most majestic, beautiful names.' And do not recite your prayer too loudly or entirely conceal it, but actively seek between that an intermediate way."
Plain Understanding
The pagans got confused when they heard the Prophet pray to "Ar-Rahman" (The Merciful), thinking he was praying to two different gods. God corrects them: "You can call Him Allah, or you can call Him The Merciful. It is the exact same Creator! He owns all the most beautiful, majestic titles." And when you pray, don't scream your prayers to show off, but don't whisper them so quietly you can't hear yourself—find a perfectly balanced, middle volume.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Al-Asma' al-Husna" (The most beautiful names). God is one Essence with infinite attributes. Whether you invoke His Majesty (Allah) or His Grace (Ar-Rahman), you are tapping into the same infinite Source. The command regarding the volume of prayer represents the core of Islam: The Middle Path. True spirituality is neither aggressive public ostentation (screaming) nor extreme, isolated asceticism (silence). It is a dignified, steady presence.

Verse 111
وَقُلِ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى لَمْ يَتَّخِذْ وَلَدًا وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ شَرِيكٌ فِى ٱلْمُلْكِ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ وَلِىٌّ مِّنَ ٱلذُّلِّ ۖ وَكَبِّرْهُ تَكْبِيرًۢا
"And proudly declare, 'All perfect praise exclusively belongs to Allah, who has absolutely not taken a son and has never had a partner in [His] absolute dominion and has never required a protector out of weakness.' And magnify Him with the ultimate, supreme magnification!"
Plain Understanding
The Surah ends with the ultimate, roaring victory cry. Stand up and proudly announce to the universe: "All praise belongs strictly to God! He does not have children. He does not share His Throne with any partners. And He is so unimaginably powerful that He never needs a helper or ally to defend Him!" Scream His greatness, and magnify Him above every fear, every empire, and every worldly power in your life!
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Waliyyun min adh-dhull" (A protector out of weakness). Human kings need allies, armies, and prime ministers strictly because they are biologically limited and weak. God shatters this anthropomorphic illusion. He manages the cosmos completely solo. The final command "Kabbirhu takbeera" (Magnify Him greatly) is the spiritual climax of the Night Journey: having seen the massive, terrifying scale of the universe during the Isra, the believer must shrink the Dunya in their heart and make Allah the "Akbar" (The Greatest).

Surah Al-Anfal

The Spoils of War • Madinah • 75 Verses
Revealed in the immediate aftermath of the monumental Battle of Badr, this Surah acts as a divine military and psychological debriefing. Allah systematically strips away human arrogance regarding victory, establishing absolute martial laws and defining the terrifyingly high internal standard required of a true believer.
Verse 1
يَسْـَٔلُونَكَ عَنِ ٱلْأَنفَالِ ۖ قُلِ ٱلْأَنفَالُ لِلَّهِ وَٱلرَّسُولِ ۖ فَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَصْلِحُوا۟ ذَاتَ بَيْنِكُمْ ۖ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥٓ إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ
"They ask you O Prophet regarding the spoils of war. Say, Their distribution is decided by Allah and His Messenger. So be mindful of Allah, settle your affairs, and obey Allah and His Messenger if you are true believers."
Plain Understanding
Allah aggressively confiscates the material spoils of war, establishing His absolute divine monopoly over their distribution. He violently severs the human ego's attachment to financial reward in the immediate aftermath of combat. By declaring that the spoils belong entirely to Allah and His Messenger, He forces the believers to recalibrate their psychological focus. He diagnoses their internal fracturing and issues a direct command: the true metric of faith is not the accumulation of wealth, but the active maintenance of mindfulness of Allah, the immediate resolution of interpersonal disputes, and unconditional obedience to the divine command structure if they claim to be true believers.
Historical Context
This verse was revealed immediately following the Battle of Badr, the first major military victory for the Muslim state. A dangerous psychological fracture erupted among the ranks: the young vanguard who charged the enemy claimed the spoils, while the veteran guards at the command post demanded equal shares. Allah revealed this verse to instantly shatter the materialistic dispute, actively demonstrating that fighting in His cause is fundamentally incompatible with fighting for profit.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi explains that Allah deliberately engineered this initial ambiguity regarding the spoils to expose the latent greed hiding within human hearts. The ego demands immediate, visible compensation for physical risk. By confiscating the wealth, Allah actively purifies their intentions, ensuring their swords are swung exclusively for the elevation of His Word, not for the acquisition of worldly armor.

Verse 2

"The true believers are only those whose hearts tremble at the remembrance of Allah, whose faith increases when His revelations are recited to them, and who put their trust in their Lord."

A true believer is mathematically identified by three involuntary spiritual reflexes. First, their hearts physically tremble in profound awe at the mere mention of Allah's Name. Second, divine revelation acts as an active catalyst; hearing the verses does not merely impart academic knowledge, but measurably increases their internal conviction. Finally, they execute absolute trust, delegating their entire existence and survival entirely to the management of their Lord.
Historical Context
Following the sharp rebuke over the spoils of war, Allah redirects the psychological focus of the victorious army from the external battlefield to the internal battlefield. He reminds the veterans of Badr that true spiritual rank is not achieved by holding a sword, but by possessing a heart that vibrates uncontrollably before the Majesty of the Creator.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Ghazali)
Al-Ghazali deeply analyzes the trembling heart, defining it not as the panicked terror of prey, but as the shattering awe of a servant who suddenly perceives the infinite reality of the King. When the human intellect truly grasps the staggering magnitude of Allah's power, the physical vessel involuntarily vibrates in absolute submission.

"And whoever does so on such an occasion—unless it is a manoeuvre or to join their own troops—will earn the displeasure of Allah, and their home will be Hell. What an evil destination!"
Plain Understanding
Allah clearly defines the severe, eternal consequence of battlefield desertion, while simultaneously demonstrating absolute tactical brilliance. Fleeing in pure cowardice guarantees the instantaneous "displeasure of Allah" and actively secures a permanent residence in Hellfire. However, Allah grants two highly specific, logical military exemptions: feigning retreat to lure the enemy into a trap ("a manoeuvre") or strategically falling back to regroup with a larger Muslim battalion ("to join their own troops"). This proves that Islam does not demand blind, suicidal charges. Tactical retreat is legally distinguished from cowardly desertion based entirely on the hidden intention of the warrior.
Historical Context
This law became one of the defining major sins (Kaba'ir) in Islamic jurisprudence. It ensured the survival of the early Islamic state against massive empires like the Romans and Persians. By strictly criminalizing desertion, the Muslim armies became famously unbreakable. Later commanders, like Khalid ibn al-Walid, masterfully utilized the "manoeuvre" exception, executing brilliant tactical retreats to exhaust massive enemy forces without violating this divine command.

Al-Qurtubi emphasizes the profound balance of divine legislation. The ego either wants to flee in terror or charge recklessly without strategy. Allah regulates both extremes. He binds the human being to immense courage by threatening Hell for desertion, but He protects human life by explicitly authorizing strategic intelligence. The intention of the heart separates the coward destined for the Fire from the brilliant tactician seeking victory.

"Those who believed, migrated, and struggled in the cause of Allah, and those who gave them shelter and help, they are the true believers. [cite: 307] [cite_start]They will have forgiveness and an honourable provision. [cite: 308]"
Plain Understanding
Allah delivers the ultimate, cosmic certification. He actively validates the two foundational pillars of the new Islamic civilization: the Muhajirun (who physically sacrificed their homes and wealth for the truth) and the Ansar (who actively weaponized their city and swords to protect the truth). He explicitly stamps them as the "true believers," officially differentiating them from those who merely hold faith in their hearts without physical sacrifice. He guarantees them the ultimate twin reward: complete divine erasure of their sins and eternal, honorable sustenance.

This verse acts as the divine medal of honor for the vanguard of Islam. The original migrants from Makkah and the hosts in Madinah endured horrific poverty, assassinations, and total warfare to establish the first state. Allah immortalizes their historical status, officially elevating them above all future generations of Muslims who would enter the faith during times of peace and luxury.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi emphasizes the active verb "struggled" (Jahadu). True faith is never a passive, intellectual exercise. It demands extreme, agonizing, physical friction against falsehood. You are either actively migrating away from sin, actively providing shelter to the oppressed, or actively struggling to establish justice. If you are comfortable, you are not executing the curriculum of the true believers.

"And those who later believed, migrated, and struggled alongside you, they are also with you. [cite: 308] [cite_start]But only blood relatives are now entitled to inherit from one another, as ordained by Allah. [cite: 309] [cite_start]Surely Allah has full knowledge of everything. [cite: 310]"
Plain Understanding
Allah seamlessly closes the Surah by regulating the future sociology of the state. He officially integrates the second wave of converts—those who migrate and fight after the establishment of Madinah—into the unified political brotherhood ("they are also with you"). However, having successfully forged the unbreakable spiritual unity of the Ummah, Allah actively rolls back the emergency economic laws. The initial policy where non-related brothers-in-arms inherited from each other is legally abrogated. Allah restores the biological architecture of society: inheritance is now permanently locked back to the bloodline. He seals the chapter by confirming that His infinite knowledge meticulously regulates every phase of human societal evolution.
Historical Context
When the Muslims first arrived in Madinah, they were penniless refugees. To prevent mass starvation, the Prophet (ﷺ) forged a legal bond where a wealthy Ansar would physically split his property and inheritance with his Muhajir brother, entirely disinheriting his pagan biological relatives. Years later, after the victory at Badr and the stabilization of the economy, this extreme emergency measure was no longer required. Allah revealed this verse to transition the state back to normative, permanent family law.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Ghazali)
Al-Ghazali highlights the perfection of divine legislation. Allah deploys extreme, radical laws to forcefully break the human ego and establish spiritual bonds during times of crisis. But Allah does not intend for humans to live permanently in a state of emergency. Once the heart is purified and the brotherhood is secured, the Almighty gently returns society to its natural, biological equilibrium, proving that His law is a perfectly timed medicine, not a rigid chain.

Surah Al-Mujadilah

The Pleading Woman • Madinah • 22 Verses
The Ear That Never Sleeps. Revealed in Madinah, this Surah opens with one of the most intimately beautiful moments in prophetic history—a vulnerable, heartbroken woman arguing her case and being heard directly by the Sovereign above the seven heavens. From the intimate confines of a domestic dispute, the Surah expands into a profound masterclass on social ethics, the spiritual poison of secret cliques, and the omnipresence of God. It concludes by defining the ultimate psychological and spiritual boundaries of love, teaching the believer how to anchor their heart exclusively in the "Party of Allah."
Verse 1
قَدْ سَمِعَ ٱللَّهُ قَوْلَ ٱلَّتِى تُجَـٰدِلُكَ فِى زَوْجِهَا وَتَشْتَكِىٓ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ وَٱللَّهُ يَسْمَعُ تَحَاوُرَكُمَآ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ سَمِيعٌۢ بَصِيرٌ
"Indeed, Allah has heard the argument of the woman who pleaded with you ˹O Prophet˺ concerning her husband, and appealed to Allah. Allah has heard your exchange. Surely Allah is All-Hearing, All-Seeing."
Plain Understanding
A breathtaking reassurance: the Supreme Creator of the universe paused to listen to the quiet, desperate pleas of a single heartbroken woman, proving that no sincere cry for help ever goes unheard.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
This verse immortalizes Khawlah bint Tha'labah. Her husband, Aws bin As-Samit, had hastily pronounced a pre-Islamic oath of permanent separation (Zihar) upon her in a fit of anger. Devastated that her family would be destroyed, she rushed to the Prophet ﷺ. Aisha (ra) later said: "Blessed is He whose hearing encompasses all things. I was in the same room and could barely hear her whispers, yet Allah heard her from above the seven heavens and immediately sent down revelation to save her."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Whenever He releases your tongue to ask, know that He wants to give to you." Khawlah's desperate complaining to God was not a lack of patience; it was the ultimate manifestation of spiritual poverty (Iftiqar). When the heart is completely shattered and turns exclusively to the Divine, the response is immediate and transformative.

Verse 2
ٱلَّذِينَ يُظَـٰهِرُونَ مِنكُم مِّن نِّسَآئِهِم مَّا هُنَّ أُمَّهَـٰتِهِمْ ۖ إِنْ أُمَّهَـٰتُهُمْ إِلَّا ٱلَّـٰٓـِٔى وَلَدْنَهُمْ ۚ وَإِنَّهُمْ لَيَقُولُونَ مُنكَرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلْقَوْلِ وَزُورًۭا ۚ وَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَعَفُوٌّ غَفُورٌۭ
"Those of you who ˹sinfully˺ divorce their wives by comparing them to their mothers ˹should know that˺ their wives are in no way their mothers. None can be their mothers except those who gave birth to them. What they say is certainly detestable and false. Yet Allah is truly Ever-Pardoning, All-Forgiving."
Plain Understanding
God dismantles a cruel cultural practice. A man cannot magically turn his wife into his mother simply by saying so; words must reflect reality. Using speech to oppress others is a severe lie, yet God leaves the door of forgiveness open.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pre-Islamic practice of ihar involved a husband telling his wife in anger, "You are to me as my mother's back." This vile custom functioned as a permanent divorce, but left the woman trapped in the husband's house without any marital rights or the freedom to remarry. Allah completely criminalized this verbal abuse, protecting women from being held hostage by male temper tantrums.

Verse 3
وَٱلَّذِينَ يُظَـٰهِرُونَ مِن نِّسَآئِهِمْ ثُمَّ يَعُودُونَ لِمَا قَالُوا۟ فَتَحْرِيرُ رَقَبَةٍۢ مِّن قَبْلِ أَن يَتَمَآسَّا ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ تُوعَظُونَ بِهِۦ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ
"Those who divorce their wives in this manner, then ˹wish to˺ retract what they said, must free a slave before they touch each other. This ˹penalty˺ is meant to deter you. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do."
Plain Understanding
Because words of abuse cause real damage, simply apologizing is not enough. If a man wishes to restore his marriage after committing this sin, he must pay a heavy, liberating penalty to prove his sincerity before physical intimacy can resume.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ego speaks recklessly when it feels powerful. By mandating the freeing of a slave as expiation, God forces the oppressor to surrender his own wealth and power. You attempted to enslave your wife with your words; you must now buy your own spiritual freedom by literally freeing another human being.

Verse 4
فَمَن لَّمْ يَجِدْ فَصِيَامُ شَهْرَيْنِ مُتَتَابِعَيْنِ مِن قَبْلِ أَن يَتَمَآسَّا ۖ فَمَن لَّمْ يَسْتَطِعْ فَإِطْعَامُ سِتِّينَ مِسْكِينًۭا ۚ ذَٰلِكَ لِتُؤْمِنُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِۦ ۚ وَتِلْكَ حُدُودُ ٱللَّهِ ۗ وَلِلْكَـٰفِرِينَ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ
"But if the husband cannot afford this, let him then fast two consecutive months before the couple touch each other. But if he is unable ˹to fast˺, then let him feed sixty poor people. This is to re-affirm your faith in Allah and His Messenger. These are the limits set by Allah. And the disbelievers will suffer a painful punishment."
Plain Understanding
God provides alternatives for those lacking wealth or health: exhaust yourself physically through two months of fasting, or financially by feeding sixty hungry people. These difficult boundaries are designed to deeply reprogram your behavior and solidify your faith.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"A disobedience that bequeaths humiliation and extreme need is better than an obedience that bequeaths self-infatuation and pride." The intense physical hunger of a two-month fast humbles the animal soul, crushing the arrogance that caused the man to verbally abuse his wife in the first place.

Verse 5
إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ يُحَآدُّونَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ كُبِتُوا۟ كَمَا كُبِتَ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ ۚ وَقَدْ أَنزَلْنَآ ءَايَـٰتٍۭ بَيِّنَـٰتٍۢ ۚ وَلِلْكَـٰفِرِينَ عَذَابٌۭ مُّهِينٌۭ
"Indeed, those who defy Allah and His Messenger will be debased, just like those before them. We have certainly sent down clear revelations. And the disbelievers will suffer a humiliating punishment."
Plain Understanding
A severe warning to those who arrogantly reject these divine laws: opposing the Creator's boundaries will inevitably lead to your own disgrace and ruin, precisely as it destroyed arrogant civilizations in the past.

Verse 6
يَوْمَ يَبْعَثُهُمُ ٱللَّهُ جَمِيعًۭا فَيُنَبِّئُهُم بِمَا عَمِلُوٓا۟ ۚ أَحْصَىٰهُ ٱللَّهُ وَنَسُوهُ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ شَهِيدٌ
"On the Day Allah resurrects them all together, He will then inform them of what they have done. Allah has kept account of it all, while they have forgotten it. For Allah is a Witness over all things."
Plain Understanding
Humans have a terrifying tendency to commit wrongs and instantly forget them, but the Divine memory is flawless. Every single whispered word and hidden action is perfectly recorded and will be presented to you.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Declare your jihad on thirteen enemies you cannot see..." The ego uses forgetfulness as a shield to avoid the pain of guilt. True self-examination (Muhasabah) requires you to aggressively remember your own flaws before the Day when the veil of amnesia is violently torn away.

Verse 7
أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَعْلَمُ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۖ مَا يَكُونُ مِن نَّجْوَىٰ ثَلَـٰثَةٍ إِلَّا هُوَ رَابِعُهُمْ وَلَا خَمْسَةٍ إِلَّا هُوَ سَادِسُهُمْ وَلَآ أَدْنَىٰ مِن ذَٰلِكَ وَلَآ أَكْثَرَ إِلَّا هُوَ مَعَهُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كَانُوا۟ ۖ ثُمَّ يُنَبِّئُهُم بِمَا عَمِلُوا۟ يَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَـٰمَةِ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِكُلِّ شَىْءٍ عَلِيمٌ
"Do you not see that Allah knows whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth? If three converse privately, He is their fourth. If five, He is their sixth. Whether fewer or more, He is with them wherever they may be. Then, on the Day of Judgment, He will inform them of what they have done. Surely Allah has ˹perfect˺ knowledge of all things."
Plain Understanding
You are never truly alone, and no conversation is ever actually secret. The Creator's encompassing knowledge is the silent, inevitable witness in every single private meeting, no matter how remote the location.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"You will be with created beings so long as you do not witness the Creator of beings. But when you witness Him, the created beings will be with you." True spiritual vigilance (Muraqabah) is living every second acutely aware of this divine gaze. When the heart realizes the King is physically unseen but intensely present in the room, the desire to backbite, scheme, or sin simply melts away.

Verse 8
أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى ٱلَّذِينَ نُهُوا۟ عَنِ ٱلنَّجْوَىٰ ثُمَّ يَعُودُونَ لِمَا نُهُوا۟ عَنْهُ وَيَتَنَـٰجَوْنَ بِٱلْإِثْمِ وَٱلْعُدْوَٰنِ وَمَعْصِيَتِ ٱلرَّسُولِ وَإِذَا جَآءُوكَ حَيَّوْكَ بِمَا لَمْ يُحَيِّكَ بِهِ ٱللَّهُ وَيَقُولُونَ فِىٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ لَوْلَا يُعَذِّبُنَا ٱللَّهُ بِمَا نَقُولُ ۚ حَسْبُهُمْ جَهَنَّمُ يَصْلَوْنَهَا ۖ فَبِئْسَ ٱلْمَصِيرُ
"Have you not seen those who were forbidden from secret talks, yet they ˹always˺ return to what they were forbidden from, conspiring in sin, aggression, and disobedience to the Messenger? And when they come to you ˹O Prophet˺, they greet you not as Allah greets you, and say to one another, “Why does Allah not punish us for what we say?” Hell is enough for them—they will burn in it. And what an evil destination!"
Plain Understanding
God exposes the toxic behavior of those who hold malicious secret meetings to plot against the community. They even mock the Prophet to his face using twisted greetings, foolishly assuming that because lightning didn't strike them immediately, God must not be real.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
This verse addresses a specific psychological warfare tactic used by the enemies in Madinah. They would huddle together and whisper when believers walked by to create paranoia. Furthermore, some Jewish leaders would approach the Prophet ﷺ and subtly twist the greeting of peace, saying "As-Samu 'Alaykum" (Death be upon you). They would then laugh privately, thinking that if he were a true Prophet, God would have destroyed them on the spot for the insult.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Be fearful lest the existence of His generosity toward you and the persistence of your bad behavior toward Him lead you step by step to ruin." The ego's greatest delusion is confusing God's temporary forbearance (Hilm) with His approval. The delay in punishment is not an escape; it is often the rope given to the arrogant so they may hang themselves with their own hubris.

Verse 9
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا تَنَـٰجَيْتُمْ فَلَا تَتَنَـٰجَوْا۟ بِٱلْإِثْمِ وَٱلْعُدْوَٰنِ وَمَعْصِيَتِ ٱلرَّسُولِ وَتَنَـٰجَوْا۟ بِٱلْبِرِّ وَٱلتَّقْوَىٰ ۖ وَٱتَّقُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ٱلَّذِىٓ إِلَيْهِ تُحْشَرُونَ
"O believers! When you converse privately, let it not be for sin, aggression, or disobedience to the Messenger, but let it be for goodness and righteousness. And fear Allah, to Whom you will ˹all˺ be gathered."
Plain Understanding
If you must speak privately, the content of your secrets must be pure. Never use isolation to plot harm or rebel; use it only to encourage goodness, always remembering that you will ultimately stand before your Creator to explain every whispered word.

Verse 10
إِنَّمَا ٱلنَّجْوَىٰ مِنَ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنِ لِيَحْزُنَ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَلَيْسَ بِضَآرِّهِمْ شَيْـًٔا إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ وَعَلَى ٱللَّهِ فَلْيَتَوَكَّلِ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ
"Secret talks are only inspired by Satan to grieve the believers. Yet he cannot harm them whatsoever except by Allah’s Will. So in Allah let the believers put their trust."
Plain Understanding
Malicious whispering is a deliberate psychological weapon designed by Satan to make you feel isolated, paranoid, and sad. However, you must realize that these whispers have absolutely no real power over your destiny; place your unbreakable trust in God, who controls all outcomes.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"In God, there is no sorrow or suffering... hold fast to God, and turn wholly to Him." The cure for the anxiety of what others are plotting behind your back is absolute Tawakkul (Reliance). When you deeply internalize that the entire universe cannot lift a finger to harm you without the Sovereign's permission, the petty gossip of mortals ceases to affect your heart.

Verse 11
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا۟ فِى ٱلْمَجَـٰلِسِ فَٱفْسَحُوا۟ يَفْسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمْ ۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُوا۟ فَٱنشُزُوا۟ يَرْفَعِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ مِنكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا۟ ٱلْعِلْمَ دَرَجَـٰتٍۢ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌۭ
"O believers! When you are told to make room in gatherings, then do so. Allah will make room for you ˹in His grace˺. And if you are told to rise, then do so. Allah will elevate those of you who are faithful, and ˹raise˺ those gifted with knowledge in rank. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do."
Plain Understanding
A profoundly beautiful lesson in social etiquette: when you physically make space for others, God spiritually and literally makes vast space for you in this life and the next. True status is not gained by clinging to the front row, but through deep faith and divine knowledge.
Historical Context (Al-Tabari)
The Prophet ﷺ was in a crowded gathering on a Friday when some esteemed veterans of the Battle of Badr arrived. They stood waiting, but no one offered them a seat. Disappointed, the Prophet asked some of the men sitting near him to rise and give their spots to the veterans. The men who were moved felt slighted, and the hypocrites tried to use this to create division. God revealed this verse to teach that yielding your comfort for a brother is the true mark of nobility.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Bury your existence in the earth of obscurity." The ego fights fiercely for the "best seat" to be seen and respected by creation. God teaches us that when you gracefully surrender your visible status for the sake of another, He elevates your invisible rank among the angels.

Verse 12
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ إِذَا نَـٰجَيْتُمُ ٱلرَّسُولَ فَقَدِّمُوا۟ بَيْنَ يَدَىْ نَجْوَىٰكُمْ صَدَقَةًۭ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكُمْ وَأَطْهَرُ ۚ فَإِن لَّمْ تَجِدُوا۟ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ
"O believers! When you consult the Messenger privately, give something in charity before your consultation. That is better for you and purer. But if you lack the means, then Allah is truly All-Forgiving, Most Merciful."
Plain Understanding
To ensure people respected the Prophet's time and only brought forward serious issues, God instituted a temporary rule: before holding a private meeting with him, you must first give to the poor, purifying your own intentions.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
As Islam grew, people began monopolizing the Prophet's ﷺ time, constantly requesting private meetings for trivial matters simply to boast about their closeness to him. To test their sincerity and relieve the Prophet, Allah commanded a charitable toll. Ali ibn Abi Talib was the only companion who had the opportunity to act upon this, giving a dinar in charity before consulting the Prophet, shortly before the command was mercifully lifted.

Verse 13
ءَأَشْفَقْتُمْ أَن تُقَدِّمُوا۟ بَيْنَ يَدَىْ نَجْوَىٰكُمْ صَدَقَـٰتٍۢ ۚ فَإِذْ لَمْ تَفْعَلُوا۟ وَتَابَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْكُمْ فَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَءَاتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ
"Are you afraid of spending in charity before your private consultations ˹with him˺? Since you are unable to do so, and Allah has turned to you in mercy, then ˹continue to˺ establish prayer, pay alms-tax, and obey Allah and His Messenger. And Allah is All-Aware of what you do."
Plain Understanding
God gently acknowledges human weakness: the requirement to pay charity caused people to hesitate in seeking counsel. Because the lesson of respecting his time was learned, God mercifully removed the toll, asking only for standard devotion, prayer, and obedience moving forward.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Wealth acts as a heavy screen over the heart. When forced to part with their money just to speak to the Guide, the insincere retreated. This divine test brilliantly separated those who desired the Prophet's company for social status from those who genuinely sought the Truth.

Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This specifically referenced individuals like Abdullah ibn Nabtal, a hypocrite who would sit in the gatherings of the Prophet ﷺ, memorize what was said, and immediately report the intelligence to the hostile Jewish tribes in Madinah. When confronted, he would swear by God that he was innocent, utilizing a false oath to mask his treason.

"On the Day Allah resurrects them all, they will ˹falsely˺ swear to Him as they swear to you, thinking they have something to stand on. Indeed, it is they who are the ˹total˺ liars."
Plain Understanding
The tragedy of a heart corrupted by chronic lying: even on the terrifying Day of Resurrection, standing before the All-Knowing God, their first instinct will be to swear another false oath, foolishly thinking it will work.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Sin is not just an action; it shapes the very architecture of the soul. When a person lives their entire life in deceit, deceit becomes their baseline reality. They lose the ability to perceive the Truth, bringing their spiritual blindness with them into the Hereafter.

Verse 19
ٱسْتَحْوَذَ عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنُ فَأَنسَىٰهُمْ ذِكْرَ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱلشَّيْطَـٰنِ هُمُ ٱلْخَـٰسِرُونَ
"Satan has taken hold of them, causing them to forget the remembrance of Allah. They are the party of Satan. Surely Satan’s party is bound to lose."
Plain Understanding
This is how a soul falls: by allowing dark forces to dominate their mind until the thought of God is completely erased from their memory. They have drafted themselves into the faction of Satan, a faction guaranteed to suffer ultimate defeat.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"How astonishing is he who flees from what is inescapable, and searches for what is evanescent!" Satan's primary objective is not to force you into major sins immediately; his goal is simply to make you forget. Once the remembrance (Dhikr) of Allah is removed from the heart, the fortress has fallen, and the enemy simply walks in.

Verse 22
لَّا تَجِدُ قَوْمًۭا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْـَٔاخِرِ يُوَآدُّونَ مَنْ حَآدَّ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥ وَلَوْ كَانُوٓا۟ ءَابَآءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَآءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَٰنَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ كَتَبَ فِى قُلُوبِهِمُ ٱلْإِيمَـٰنَ وَأَيَّدَهُم بِرُوحٍۢ مِّنْهُ ۖ وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّـٰتٍۢ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَـٰرُ خَـٰلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۚ رَضِىَ ٱللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا۟ عَنْهُ ۚ أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ حِزْبُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ أَلَآ إِنَّ حِزْبَ ٱللَّهِ هُمُ ٱلْمُفْلِحُونَ
"You will never find a people who ˹truly˺ believe in Allah and the Last Day loyal to those who defy Allah and His Messenger, even if they were their parents, children, siblings, or extended family. For those ˹believers˺, Allah has instilled faith in their hearts and strengthened them with a spirit from Him. He will admit them into Gardens under which rivers flow, to stay there forever. Allah is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him. They are the party of Allah. Indeed, Allah’s party is bound to succeed."
Plain Understanding
The ultimate litmus test of love: a true believer's heart cannot harbor deep allegiance for someone who aggressively attacks their Creator, even if it is their own flesh and blood. When you choose God over everything else, He carves faith permanently into your heart, surrounds you with His Light, and grants you the highest success: mutual, eternal satisfaction with your Lord.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
This profound verse captures the excruciating sacrifices made by the early companions. At the Battle of Badr, Abu Ubaydah faced his own father in combat, while Mus'ab bin Umayr fought his brother. They did not sever ties out of hatred, but because their relatives were actively trying to extinguish the light of Islam. God honored their agonizing choice by declaring them His own exclusive "Party."
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Take pity on your soul... surrender whatever is besides your Lord to Him." This verse describes the highest psychological station of the believer—Maqam al-Rida (The Station of Contentment). When you empty your heart of worldly attachments and toxic loyalties, God literally 'writes' faith into your chest, fortifying you with a divine spirit, leading to the ultimate state: He is pleased with you, and you are entirely at peace with Him.

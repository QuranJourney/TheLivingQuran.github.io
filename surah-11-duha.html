Surah Ad-Duha

The Morning Hours • Makkah • 11 Verses
The Cure for Spiritual Depression. Revealed after a agonizing pause in revelation. It reassures the Prophet ﷺ of Allah's uninterrupted love, proving that moments of spiritual dryness are not signs of divine abandonment, but necessary phases of growth.
Verse 1
وَٱلضُّحَىٰ
"By the morning brightness,"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the bright, active, and vibrant morning hours (after the sun has fully risen).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This Surah breaks a long period of silence. The oath by the morning light perfectly symbolizes the return of the light of Revelation to the heart of the Prophet ﷺ after his period of darkness and waiting.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The morning represents the state of spiritual expansion (Bast), when the heart feels deeply connected, active, and illuminated by God's presence.

Verse 2
وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَىٰ
"And [by] the night when it covers with darkness,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the night when it becomes completely still, calm, and dark.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Saja" implies a stillness that brings rest. The silence of the night represents the "Fatra" (pause) in revelation. Allah is teaching him that just as the night's stillness is a necessary mercy for physical rest, the pause in revelation was a necessary mercy for his heart to rest and process the heavy words.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He expands you so that He may not keep you in a state of contraction, and He contracts you so that He may not leave you in a state of expansion." Do not hate the night; the stillness is where the soul digests the light of the morning.

Verse 3
مَا وَدَّعَكَ رَبُّكَ وَمَا قَلَىٰ
"Your Lord has not taken leave of you, [O Muhammad], nor has He detested [you]."
Plain Understanding
This is the core message: Your Creator did not abandon you, say goodbye to you, or hate you.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
During the pause in revelation, Umm Jamil (the wife of Abu Lahab) taunted the Prophet ﷺ: "I see your devil has finally forsaken you!" The Prophet ﷺ himself felt immense anxiety, fearing he had done something to anger Allah. This verse is a direct, loving refutation of his enemies and a soothing balm to his heart.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
When the believer experiences a dry spell in worship (Qabd), the ego whispers, "God has abandoned you because you are unworthy." This verse destroys that whisper. Spiritual dryness is a test of your perseverance, not a sign of His hatred.

Verse 4
وَلَلْءَاخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْأُولَىٰ
"And the Hereafter is better for you than the first [life]."
Plain Understanding
The Next Life will be infinitely better and more fulfilling for you than this current worldly life.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
It also carries a secondary meaning: "The latter part of your mission will be far better than the beginning." It is a prophecy that the current persecution in Makkah is temporary, and victory is coming.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not demand that He grant you your request immediately." The delay in reward forces you to look forward to the Akhirah, ensuring your heart is attached to the Eternal rather than the ephemeral.

Verse 5
وَلَسَوْفَ يُعْطِيكَ رَبُّكَ فَتَرْضَىٰٓ
"And your Lord is going to give you, and you will be satisfied."
Plain Understanding
God promises to give you so abundantly in the future that you will reach a state of complete and utter satisfaction.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Upon hearing this verse, the Prophet ﷺ famously said: "Then I will not be satisfied as long as a single one of my Ummah (followers) is in the Fire." This highlights his immense, selfless love for his people.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The highest spiritual station is Rida (Contentment). Allah does not just say "He will give you wealth" or "power"; He promises the psychological and spiritual state of absolute satisfaction, which is the true paradise of the heart.

Verse 6
أَلَمْ يَجِدْكَ يَتِيمًۭا فَـَٔاوَىٰ
"Did He not find you an orphan and give [you] refuge?"
Plain Understanding
Allah asks rhetorically: Think about your past. Were you not a vulnerable orphan, and didn't God provide you with loving shelter and care?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet's father died before he was born, and his mother died when he was six. He was entirely vulnerable in a harsh society. Allah protected him through his grandfather Abdul Muttalib, and then his uncle Abu Talib. Allah uses his past history of care to prove He will not abandon him now.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If you want to know what He will do for you in the future, look at what He has already done for you in the past." Gratitude for past deliverance is the anchor for future trust (Tawakkul).

Verse 7
وَوَجَدَكَ ضَآلًّۭا فَهَدَىٰ
"And He found you lost and guided [you],"
Plain Understanding
Did He not find you wandering/seeking, unaware of the specific laws of religion, and then guided you through Revelation?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Dhaallan" here does not mean sinful or straying into evil. It means he was desperately seeking the truth in the Cave of Hira, confused by the idolatry of his people but not knowing the specific details of faith (Shari'ah) until the Quran was revealed.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The spiritual seeker must first realize they are "lost" before they can be found. The pain of confusion in the Cave of Hira was the prerequisite for the joy of Divine Guidance. Acknowledge your blindness to receive His sight.

Verse 8
وَوَجَدَكَ عَآئِلًۭا فَأَغْنَىٰ
"And He found you poor and made [you] self-sufficient."
Plain Understanding
Did He not find you in poverty and then enriched you, removing your need for others?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ started with no wealth. Allah enriched him through the successful trade and loving support of his wife, Khadijah (RA).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
True wealth (Ghina) is the wealth of the soul. God made him so internally self-sufficient that he never desired the treasures of kings. He owned the world in his hand, but never let it enter his heart.

Verse 9
فَأَمَّا ٱلْيَتِيمَ فَلَا تَقْهَرْ
"So as for the orphan, do not oppress [him]."
Plain Understanding
Therefore, in immense gratitude for what God has done for you, never be harsh, oppressive, or unjust to the orphan.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Allah links gratitude directly to action. You were an orphan, so you know the pain. Use the memory of your own vulnerability to create radical empathy for those who are currently vulnerable. Empathy is the fruit of remembered hardship.

Verse 10
وَأَمَّا ٱلسَّآئِلَ فَلَا تَنْهَرْ
"And as for the petitioner, do not repel [him]."
Plain Understanding
And when someone asks you for help (whether for money, food, or knowledge), do not scold, shout at, or turn them away aggressively.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Because you were once "lost" (seeking knowledge) and "poor" (seeking provision), never humiliate those who come to you seeking the same. If you cannot help them, turn them away with kind and gentle words.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The beggar is a gift from God sent to test your sincerity." Do not view the petitioner as a nuisance; view them as an opportunity to manifest the generosity of the Creator.

Verse 11
وَأَمَّا بِنِعْمَةِ رَبِّكَ فَحَدِّثْ
"But as for the favor of your Lord, report [it]."
Plain Understanding
And as for the immense blessings and guidance your Lord has showered upon you, proclaim them and speak of them!
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This means to actively preach the Quran (the ultimate blessing) and to physically manifest gratitude. To hide God's blessing out of stinginess or false humility is a form of ingratitude.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
There is a fine line between boasting and proclaiming God's favor. Boasting says, "Look at what *I* achieved." Reporting the favor says, "Look at what *Allah* gave a beggar like me." Speak of the blessing, but always point the finger back to the Blesser.

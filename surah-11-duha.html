Mushaf: #93 | Chronological: #11

Surah Ad-Duha

The Morning Hours • Makkah • 11 Verses
The Cure for Spiritual Depression. Revealed after a agonizing pause in revelation. It reassures the Prophet ﷺ of Allah's uninterrupted love, proving that moments of spiritual dryness are not signs of divine abandonment, but necessary phases of growth.
Verse 1
وَٱلضُّحَىٰ
"By the morning brightness,"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the bright, active, and vibrant morning hours (after the sun has fully risen).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This Surah breaks a long period of silence. The oath by the morning light perfectly symbolizes the return of the light of Revelation to the heart of the Prophet ﷺ after his period of darkness and waiting.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The morning represents the state of spiritual expansion (Bast), when the heart feels deeply connected, active, and illuminated by God's presence.

Verse 2
وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَىٰ
"And [by] the night when it covers with darkness,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the night when it becomes completely still, calm, and dark.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Saja" implies a stillness that brings rest. The silence of the night represents the "Fatra" (pause) in revelation. Allah is teaching him that just as the night's stillness is a necessary mercy for physical rest, the pause in revelation was a necessary mercy for his heart to rest and process the heavy words.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He expands you so that He may not keep you in a state of contraction, and He contracts you so that He may not leave you in a state of expansion." Do not hate the night; the stillness is where the soul digests the light of the morning.

Verse 3
مَا وَدَّعَكَ رَبُّكَ وَمَا قَلَىٰ
"Your Lord has not taken leave of you, [O Muhammad], nor has He detested [you]."
Plain Understanding
This is the core message: Your Creator did not abandon you, say goodbye to you, or hate you.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
During the pause in revelation, Umm Jamil (the wife of Abu Lahab) taunted the Prophet ﷺ: "I see your devil has finally forsaken you!" The Prophet ﷺ himself felt immense anxiety, fearing he had done something to anger Allah. This verse is a direct, loving refutation of his enemies and a soothing balm to his heart.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
When the believer experiences a dry spell in worship (Qabd), the ego whispers, "God has abandoned you because you are unworthy." This verse destroys that whisper. Spiritual dryness is a test of your perseverance, not a sign of His hatred.

Verse 4
وَلَلْءَاخِرَةُ خَيْرٌۭ لَّكَ مِنَ ٱلْأُولَىٰ
"And the Hereafter is better for you than the first [life]."
Plain Understanding
The Next Life will be infinitely better and more fulfilling for you than this current worldly life.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
It also carries a secondary meaning: "The latter part of your mission will be far better than the beginning." It is a prophecy that the current persecution in Makkah is temporary, and victory is coming.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not demand that He grant you your request immediately." The delay in reward forces you to look forward to the Akhirah, ensuring your heart is attached to the Eternal rather than the ephemeral.

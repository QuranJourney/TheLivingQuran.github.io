Mushaf: #89 | Chronological: #10

Surah Al-Alaq

The Clot • Makkah • 19 Verses
The first drops of rain from the heavens after a drought of six hundred years. This Surah captures the absolute poverty of man before his Creator and the absolute arrogance of man when he thinks he is independent.
Verse 1
ٱقْرَأْ بِٱسْمِ رَبِّكَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ
"Recite in the name of your Lord who created—"
Plain Understanding
The command is to read or recite, specifically invoking the name of the Sustainer (Rabb) who originated all creation. It links the act of learning directly to the Divine.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ was in the Cave of Hira when Jibreel (AS) appeared and squeezed him three times, commanding "Iqra" (Read). The Prophet ﷺ responded, "I am not a reciter/I do not know how to read." This verse establishes that Prophethood is not an acquired skill (like learning to write), but a divine selection.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This verse purifies the intention behind seeking knowledge. Knowledge sought for ego, status, or debate is darkness. Knowledge sought "in the name of your Lord" is Light. The first step of the spiritual path is to attach every action to the Divine Name.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He who is illumined at the beginning, is illumined at the end." By starting the revelation with the Name of the Lord, Allah guaranteed its perfection and preservation. If you start your affairs with Bismillah, the end is secured.

Verse 2
خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ
"Created man from a clinging substance."
Plain Understanding
Allah created the human being from alaq—a suspended clot of blood or a leech-like substance in the womb.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Alaq" implies a clot of blood or a leech-like substance that clings to the uterine wall. It is a medically accurate description, but spiritually, it signifies "clinging." It contradicts the pride of the pagan Arabs who boasted of noble lineage.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
This is the cure for Arrogance (Kibr). O Man, you who walk the earth with pride, remember your origin. You are not made of gold or light; you are made of a clinging, humble fluid. Realize your lowliness to witness His Greatness.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Your desire to be known among people is a proof of your lack of truthfulness in your servitude." Why do you seek greatness when your origin is so humble? True dignity comes from the Creator, not the created material.

Verse 3
ٱقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ ٱلْأَكْرَمُ
"Recite, and your Lord is the most Generous—"
Plain Understanding
Keep reciting! Your Lord is Al-Akram (The Most Generous/Noble), meaning His giving has no limit and requires no qualification from you.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ feared he could not carry this heavy message. Allah uses the superlative Al-Akram to reassure him: "You don't need to be capable; I am Generous enough to give even to the incapable."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This cures Despair (Ya's). A sinner might say, "I am too broken to read the Quran or pray." Allah replies, "I am the Most Generous." He does not give because you are good; He gives because He is good.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"When He gives, He shows you His kindness (Jamal); when He withholds, He shows you His power (Jalal)." Here, the granting of the Quran is the ultimate display of Divine Generosity.
Attributes of Allah Hope Prophetic History
Verse 4
ٱلَّذِى عَلَّمَ بِٱلْقَلَمِ
"Who taught by the pen—"
Plain Understanding
He taught humanity how to use the pen to record knowledge, preserving it across generations.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Pen (Qalam) was the first creation. Qurtubi notes that writing is a distinct honor given to humans over animals. It allows the knowledge of the past to reach the present.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The Pen has no will of its own; it only writes what the writer intends. The heart of the Murid (seeker) should be like the pen in the hand of Allah—silent, submissive, and moving only when moved by the Divine Command.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He subjected everything to you, so that you might know His kindness in everything." The pen, the paper, the ink—all are subjected to serve your learning. Do not get lost in the tool and forget the Designer of the tool.

Verse 5
عَلَّمَ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ مَا لَمْ يَعْلَمْ
"Taught man that which he knew not."
Plain Understanding
Humans are born knowing nothing. Every piece of knowledge—from fire-making to revelation—is a direct teaching from Allah.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This refers to Adam (AS) being taught the names of things, and specifically to Muhammad ﷺ being taught the Quran despite being unlettered.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This cures Intellectual Pride. If you are intelligent, remember: you were once a baby who knew nothing. Your intelligence is a loan. To claim it as your own is theft.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He prohibits you from claiming what belongs to others... would He then permit you to claim His attribute?" Knowledge is an attribute of Allah (Al-Alim). You are merely a mirror reflecting His light.
Knowledge & Signs Humility Attributes of Allah
Verse 6
كَلَّآ إِنَّ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ لَيَطْغَىٰٓ
"No! [But] indeed, man transgresses."
Plain Understanding
The word Kalla (Nay!) is a rebuke. The nature of the human being, when left unchecked, is to cross the boundaries (Tagha).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This was revealed about Abu Jahl. He was the "Pharaoh" of this nation. When he felt secure in his wealth and tribe, he began to oppress the weak.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The Nafs (Ego) is like a wild horse. If you do not rein it in with fear of Allah, it will run off a cliff. Transgression is the natural state of the ego that has not been purified.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The source of every disobedience, indifference, and passion is satisfaction with one's self." Transgression starts the moment you are satisfied with your spiritual state.

Verse 7
أَن رَّءَاهُ ٱسْتَغْنَىٰ
"Because he sees himself self-sufficient."
Plain Understanding
He transgresses because he thinks he is rich and needs no one.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Istighna means "to do without." Abu Jahl felt he could "do without" God because he had money and sons.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This is the root of Kufr (Disbelief). Faith is the realization of need (Faqr). Disbelief is the illusion of independence. The moment you feel safe from needing Allah, you have fallen.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Your need for Him is essential to your being; it is not something that befalls you." You are needy by definition. Thinking you are self-sufficient is a hallucination.
Human Nature Arrogance Spiritual Practice
Verse 8
إِنَّ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ ٱلرُّجْعَىٰٓ
"Indeed, to your Lord is the return."
Plain Understanding
No matter how rich or powerful you are, you are walking towards your Lord, and you will return to Him for judgment.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A threat to Abu Jahl: "You may rule Makkah, but you will return to Allah soon."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Death (Maut) is the ultimate cure for the ego. When the ego realizes it has an expiration date, its tyranny shrinks.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Travel from the creation to the Creator." The return is inevitable physically, but the wise man makes the return spiritually before he dies.

Verse 9
أَرَءَيْتَ ٱلَّذِى يَنْهَىٰ
"Have you seen the one who forbids—"
Plain Understanding
Look at this amazing, terrible sight: a man trying to stop a servant of God from praying!
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Refers specifically to Abu Jahl.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Identify the "Abu Jahl" inside you. What part of you forbids you from doing good?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He allows you to witness the opposition of people so you may seek refuge in His protection."
Human Nature Prophetic History
Verse 10
عَبْدًا إِذَا صَلَّىٰ
"A servant when he prays?"
Plain Understanding
Forbidding a servant of God from performing prayer.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Abu Jahl vowed to stop the Prophet ﷺ from praying at the Kaaba.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Prayer is the link to the Divine. Breaking that link is the worst crime.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"How can the laws of nature be broken for you so that miracles happen, while you, for your part, do not break your bad habits?" The Prophet ﷺ was breaking the habits of his society through prayer.
Spiritual Practice Commands
Verse 11
أَرَءَيْتَ إِن كَانَ عَلَى ٱلْهُدَىٰٓ
"Have you seen if he is upon guidance—"
Plain Understanding
Consider this: What if the one praying (Muhammad) is actually on the right path and is teaching good?
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Before you judge or stop someone, check yourself. Are you stopping goodness? The heart must always assume the best of others (Husn al-Zann).
Knowledge & Signs Humility
Verse 12
أَوْ أَمَرَ بِٱلتَّقْوَىٰ
"Or enjoins righteousness?"
Plain Understanding
What if he is commanding God-consciousness (Taqwa)?
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The sign of a guided person is that they call to Taqwa (awareness of God), not to themselves.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"A sin that produces humility is better than an obedience that produces pride." Abu Jahl had "obedience" to his tribe but pride; the Prophet called to Taqwa which is humility.
Spiritual Practice Humility
Verse 13
أَرَءَيْتَ إِن كَذَّبَ وَتَوَلَّىٰ
"Have you seen if he denies and turns away?"
Plain Understanding
And consider if the forbidder (Abu Jahl) is the one lying and turning his back on the Truth.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This confirms the state of the opponent. He is not just ignorant; he is actively "turning away" (Tawalla).
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Tawalla" means turning the back. The ego turns its back on the Truth when it contradicts its desires.
Human Nature Arrogance
Verse 14
أَلَمْ يَعْلَم بِأَنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَرَىٰ
"Does he not know that Allah sees?"
Plain Understanding
Is he unaware that God is watching everything he does?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The central warning. Whatever you do in secret or public, the Witness (Ash-Shahid) is present.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This establishes Muraqaba (Vigilance). If a thief knew the police were watching, he would not steal. The sinner sins because he forgets Allah is watching.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The best of your moments is the one in which you witness your poverty and realize your lowliness." When you know He sees you, you stop posturing. You drop the mask.
The Unseen Attributes of Allah Spiritual Practice
Verse 15
كَلَّا لَئِن لَّمْ يَنتَهِ لَنَسْفَعًۢا بِٱلنَّاصِيَةِ
"No! If he does not desist, We will surely drag him by the forelock—"
Plain Understanding
If he doesn't stop, We will grab him and drag him by the front of his head.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Nasiyah (forelock/front of the head) was the place of dignity for the Arab. To be dragged by it was the ultimate humiliation.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
If you raise your head in pride, Allah will bring it down in humiliation. The only way to save your "head" (dignity) is to put it on the ground voluntarily in prayer.

Verse 16
نَاصِيَةٍ كَـٰذِبَةٍ خَاطِئَةٍ
"A lying, sinning forelock."
Plain Understanding
The forelock itself is described as lying and sinful.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah attributes the sin to the forehead/brain because the prefrontal cortex is the center of decision-making. Abu Jahl used his mind to plot against the Truth.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Let not the intention of your heart be anything other than Him." A lying forelock is one that intends other than Allah.

Verse 17
فَلْيَدْعُ نَادِيَهُۥ
"Then let him call his associates."
Plain Understanding
Let him call his club, his gang, his tribe to help him.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Abu Jahl boasted of his "council" (Nadiyah) and his tribe's power. Allah challenges him: "Call your army."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If you want to have a glory that does not vanish, do not glory in a glory that vanishes." Abu Jahl gloried in his tribe (which vanishes).
Human Nature Arrogance
Verse 18
سَنَدْعُ ٱلزَّبَانِيَةَ
"We will call the angels of Hell."
Plain Understanding
We will call the guards of Hell (to seize him).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Zabaniyah implies violent thrusters. Ibn Abbas said: "If he had called his people, the angels would have seized him right there in public."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He allows you to witness the opposition of people so you may seek refuge in His protection." The Prophet ﷺ had no army here; he had only Allah.
The Unseen Divine Justice
Verse 19
كَلَّا لَا تُطِعْهُ وَٱسْجُدْ وَٱقْتَرِب
"No! Do not obey him. But prostrate and draw near."
Plain Understanding
Ignore the tyrant. Prostrate (put your forehead on the ground) and get closer to your Lord.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The final command: Defiance through worship. Do not fight him physically yet; fight him by refusing to stop praying.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
This is the climax. The cure for all distance is Sajdah. In prostration, the ego is at its lowest, and the spirit is at its highest.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He commanded you to pray not because He needs your service, but because He wants to bring you near." The verse connects Sajdah (prostration) directly with Qurb (closeness).

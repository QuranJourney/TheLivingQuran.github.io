Surah Al-Layl

The Night • Makkah • 21 Verses
The Tale of Two Paths. A profound exploration of duality in human choices. It contrasts the path of Abu Bakr—who gave freely to seek the Face of his Lord—with the path of the wealthy elite who hoarded out of a delusion of self-sufficiency.
Verse 1
وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَىٰ
"By the night when it covers,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the night as it casts its deep darkness and covers the earth.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The night covers the light of the sun, hiding the activity of the world. It is the beginning of a sequence of profound contrasts (night vs. day) that will mirror the contrast in human behaviors.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Ignorance and sin are the "night" of the soul. When the ego takes control, it covers the natural light of the heart (Fitrah), leaving the human being stumbling in the dark.

Verse 2
وَٱلنَّهَارِ إِذَا تَجَلَّىٰ
"And [by] the day when it appears,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the day when its light bursts forth, making everything visible and clear.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not despair of a hardship that befalls you, for the day only breaks after the darkest part of the night." The manifestation (Tajalli) of Divine light in the heart always follows the struggle against the darkness of the self.

Verse 3
وَمَا خَلَقَ ٱلذَّكَرَ وَٱلْأُنثَىٰٓ
"And [by] He who created the male and female,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the creation of male and female, completing the series of profound opposites.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah points to the biological duality of all living things. Just as He created the night and day, He designed the male and female. These contrasts are not accidents; they are deliberate signs of the Creator's power.

Verse 4
إِنَّ سَعْيَكُمْ لَشَتَّىٰ
"Indeed, your efforts are diverse."
Plain Understanding
This is the subject of the oaths: just as night and day are total opposites, the goals, actions, and paths of human beings are completely opposed to one another.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Look at the marketplace: everyone is running, sweating, and striving (Sa'y). But one runs toward the accumulation of dust (Dunya), while another runs toward the pleasure of the Eternal. Their physical sweat looks the same, but their spiritual destinations are galaxies apart.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The variety of works arises from the variety of states of the heart." If your efforts are scattered and chaotic, it is because your heart is not unified upon God.

Verse 5
فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَىٰ وَٱتَّقَىٰ
"As for he who gives and fears Allah,"
Plain Understanding
Regarding the person who spends their wealth in charity and maintains reverence/fear of God...
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This specifically references Abu Bakr (RA). When he saw Bilal (RA) being tortured in the scorching sun by Umayyah ibn Khalaf for accepting Islam, Abu Bakr used his own wealth to buy Bilal and immediately set him free.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Giving" (A'ta) removes the disease of greed. "Fearing" (Ittaqa) removes the disease of arrogance. You must have both. Giving without Taqwa leads to showing off (Riya).

Verse 6
وَصَدَّقَ بِٱلْحُسْنَىٰ
"And believes in the best [reward],"
Plain Understanding
And he sincerely believes in the ultimate truth of the Afterlife and its excellent reward.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Abu Bakr's father (who was not yet a Muslim) criticized him: "If you are buying slaves to free them, at least buy strong ones who can defend you!" Abu Bakr replied, "I am only doing it for what is with Allah." He believed in the unseen reward (Al-Husna).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Actions are but lifeless forms whose soul is the secret of sincerity." Believing in the "Husna" (the Best) is the soul that animates the physical act of giving.

Verse 7
فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُۥ لِلْيُسْرَىٰ
"We will ease him toward ease."
Plain Understanding
Because of those three things (giving, fearing, believing), Allah makes the path of goodness, virtue, and ultimate salvation incredibly easy for him.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
When the heart aligns with God's generosity, the friction of life vanishes. Acts of worship that are exhausting for the hypocrite become a source of rest and deep joy (Yusra) for the believer. Grace accelerates grace.

Verse 8
وَأَمَّا مَنۢ بَخِلَ وَٱسْتَغْنَىٰ
"But as for he who withholds and considers himself free of need,"
Plain Understanding
Conversely, the person who is stingy with his wealth and thinks he is totally self-sufficient without God...
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This perfectly describes Umayyah ibn Khalaf (and Abu Jahl). They hoarded their wealth, tortured the weak, and felt their tribal status made them "free of need" (Istighna) from any divine accountability.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The source of every disobedience, indifference, and passion is satisfaction with one's self." Feeling self-sufficient (Istighna) is the veil that blocks the heart from tasting the sweetness of reliance on God.

Verse 9
وَكَذَّبَ بِٱلْحُسْنَىٰ
"And denies the best [reward],"
Plain Understanding
And he flatly rejects the truth of the Afterlife and its reward.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Stinginess is born from lack of faith. If you truly believed that giving a dollar today would yield an eternal palace tomorrow, you would give everything. Denying the reward is what causes the hand to clench.

Verse 10
فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُۥ لِلْعُسْرَىٰ
"We will ease him toward difficulty."
Plain Understanding
Because of his hoarding, arrogance, and denial, Allah will make the path of evil, misery, and destruction "easy" for him to slide down.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Fear His abundant giving to you while you continually disobey Him; it may be a progressive leading to destruction (Istidraj)." God makes the sin "easy" to commit as a form of divine abandonment.

Verse 11
وَمَا يُغْنِى عَنْهُ مَالُهُۥٓ إِذَا تَرَدَّىٰٓ
"And what will his wealth avail him when he falls?"
Plain Understanding
When he tumbles into the grave (or into Hellfire), how will his hoarded money help him?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Taradda" means to fall from a high place into an abyss. Umayyah built his high status on wealth. When he was killed at Badr and cast into a dry well, all the gold in Makkah could not save his body, let alone his soul.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
You spend your entire life building a parachute made of paper money, only to realize it cannot hold your weight when the drop comes. True wealth is what you send ahead, not what you leave behind.

Verse 12
إِنَّ عَلَيْنَا لَلْهُدَىٰ
"Indeed, [incumbent] upon Us is guidance."
Plain Understanding
Allah takes it upon Himself to clearly show humanity the right path from the wrong path.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Allah provides the map and the light. But He does not force you to walk. The responsibility of guidance is His, but the responsibility of choosing it is yours.

Verse 13
وَإِنَّ لَنَا لَلْءَاخِرَةَ وَٱلْأُولَىٰ
"And indeed, to Us belongs the Hereafter and the first [life]."
Plain Understanding
Allah has absolute ownership over the Next Life and this present life.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
If you want the Dunya (this life), you must ask its Owner. If you want the Akhirah (the next), you must ask its Owner. Do not beg the creation for what only the Creator possesses.

Verse 14
فَأَنذَرْتُكُمْ نَارًا تَلَظَّىٰ
"So I have warned you of a Fire which is blazing."
Plain Understanding
Allah says: Because I have guided you and shown you the truth, I have clearly warned you of a roaring, intense Hellfire.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ used to say: "I have been given the responsibility to warn you of a Fire! I have been given the responsibility to warn you of a Fire!" He preached this with such intensity that his voice could be heard in the marketplace.

Verse 15
لَا يَصْلَىٰهَآ إِلَّا ٱلْأَشْقَى
"None will [enter to] burn therein except the most wretched one."
Plain Understanding
Only the absolute most miserable, wretched person will be thrown into it.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The Fire is not arbitrary. It is reserved specifically for the "most wretched"—the one who actively chased darkness when the Light was placed right in front of them.

Verse 16
ٱلَّذِى كَذَّبَ وَتَوَلَّىٰ
"[He] who had denied and turned away."
Plain Understanding
Who is this wretched one? The one who called the truth a lie (Kadhdhaba) and turned his back on obedience (Tawalla).
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Denial is the disease of the intellect; turning away is the disease of the limbs. When the mind and body unite in rebellion, the soul perishes.

Verse 17
وَسَيُجَنَّبُهَا ٱلْأَتْقَى
"But the righteous one will avoid it -"
Plain Understanding
And the one who is deeply God-conscious (Al-Atqa) will be kept completely away from that Fire.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Scholars uniformly agree that "Al-Atqa" (the most righteous/fearful of God) in this specific verse refers directly to Abu Bakr As-Siddiq (RA) because of his unmatched generosity and faith in the early days of Makkah.

Verse 18
ٱلَّذِى يُؤْتِى مَالَهُۥ يَتَزَكَّىٰ
"[He] who gives [from] his wealth to purify himself"
Plain Understanding
Who is this righteous one? The one who gives his money specifically for the purpose of purifying his own soul.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Notice the motive. He doesn't give to look powerful or generous to others. He gives to "purify himself" (Yatazakka). He views wealth as a spiritual toxin if hoarded, and a spiritual medicine if spent for God.

Verse 19
وَمَا لِأَحَدٍ عِندَهُۥ مِن نِّعْمَةٍ تُجْزَىٰٓ
"And not [giving] for anyone who has [done him] a favor to be rewarded"
Plain Understanding
He is not giving this money to pay back a debt or return a favor to someone who helped him in the past.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
When Abu Bakr spent his massive fortune buying and freeing slaves, the pagans gossiped: "He must owe Bilal a favor, and he is paying it back." Allah reveals this verse to shut down the rumor, confirming Abu Bakr owed them absolutely nothing.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The highest level of giving is unconditional. If you give expecting gratitude or a return favor, you are trading, not offering charity.

Verse 20
إِلَّا ٱبْتِغَآءَ وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ ٱلْأَعْلَىٰ
"But only seeking the countenance of his Lord, Most High."
Plain Understanding
His one and only motive in giving is to seek the Face (Pleasure) of his Lord, the Most High.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
This is the pinnacle of Ikhlas. To do an action purely for the "Face" of God means you desire nothing but to be in His presence. You are unconcerned with Heaven or Hell; you are obsessed only with the Creator of Heaven and Hell.

Verse 21
وَلَسَوْفَ يَرْضَىٰ
"And he is going to be satisfied."
Plain Understanding
And Allah promises: this person will soon be completely and utterly satisfied with what God gives him.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A divine guarantee of Jannah to Abu Bakr (RA) while he was still walking the earth.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He gives you the capacity to be content (Rida) with His decree, which is the highest paradise in this world before the next." He sought only the Face of God, so God guarantees him absolute satisfaction. The heart that rests in Him will never be restless again.

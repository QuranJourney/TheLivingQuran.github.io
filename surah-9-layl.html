Surah Al-Layl

The Night • Makkah • 21 Verses
The Tale of Two Paths. A profound exploration of duality in human choices. It contrasts the path of Abu Bakr—who gave freely to seek the Face of his Lord—with the path of the wealthy elite who hoarded out of a delusion of self-sufficiency.
Verse 1
وَٱلَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَىٰ
"By the night when it covers,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the night as it casts its deep darkness and covers the earth.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The night covers the light of the sun, hiding the activity of the world. It is the beginning of a sequence of profound contrasts (night vs. day) that will mirror the contrast in human behaviors.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Ignorance and sin are the "night" of the soul. When the ego takes control, it covers the natural light of the heart (Fitrah), leaving the human being stumbling in the dark.

Verse 2
وَٱلنَّهَارِ إِذَا تَجَلَّىٰ
"And [by] the day when it appears,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the day when its light bursts forth, making everything visible and clear.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not despair of a hardship that befalls you, for the day only breaks after the darkest part of the night." The manifestation (Tajalli) of Divine light in the heart always follows the struggle against the darkness of the self.

Verse 3
وَمَا خَلَقَ ٱلذَّكَرَ وَٱلْأُنثَىٰٓ
"And [by] He who created the male and female,"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the creation of male and female, completing the series of profound opposites.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah points to the biological duality of all living things. Just as He created the night and day, He designed the male and female. These contrasts are not accidents; they are deliberate signs of the Creator's power.

Verse 4
إِنَّ سَعْيَكُمْ لَشَتَّىٰ
"Indeed, your efforts are diverse."
Plain Understanding
This is the subject of the oaths: just as night and day are total opposites, the goals, actions, and paths of human beings are completely opposed to one another.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Look at the marketplace: everyone is running, sweating, and striving (Sa'y). But one runs toward the accumulation of dust (Dunya), while another runs toward the pleasure of the Eternal. Their physical sweat looks the same, but their spiritual destinations are galaxies apart.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The variety of works arises from the variety of states of the heart." If your efforts are scattered and chaotic, it is because your heart is not unified upon God.

Verse 5
فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَىٰ وَٱتَّقَىٰ
"As for he who gives and fears Allah,"
Plain Understanding
Regarding the person who spends their wealth in charity and maintains reverence/fear of God...
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This specifically references Abu Bakr (RA). When he saw Bilal (RA) being tortured in the scorching sun by Umayyah ibn Khalaf for accepting Islam, Abu Bakr used his own wealth to buy Bilal and immediately set him free.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Giving" (A'ta) removes the disease of greed. "Fearing" (Ittaqa) removes the disease of arrogance. You must have both. Giving without Taqwa leads to showing off (Riya).

Verse 6
وَصَدَّقَ بِٱلْحُسْنَىٰ
"And believes in the best [reward],"
Plain Understanding
And he sincerely believes in the ultimate truth of the Afterlife and its excellent reward.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Abu Bakr's father (who was not yet a Muslim) criticized him: "If you are buying slaves to free them, at least buy strong ones who can defend you!" Abu Bakr replied, "I am only doing it for what is with Allah." He believed in the unseen reward (Al-Husna).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Actions are but lifeless forms whose soul is the secret of sincerity." Believing in the "Husna" (the Best) is the soul that animates the physical act of giving.

Surah Al-Infitar

The Cleaving • Makkah • 19 Verses
A deeply piercing Makkan Surah that juxtaposes the violent physical collapse of the universe with the tragic psychological betrayal of the human ego, asking the devastating question: What deceived you about your Generous Lord?
Verse 1
إِذَا ٱلسَّمَآءُ ٱنفَطَرَتْ
"When the sky breaks apart,"
Plain Understanding
The Surah opens with a terrifying conditional statement regarding the Day of Judgment. The sequence begins with the destruction of the atmosphere. The sky, which appears perfectly solid and seamless from the earth, will experience "Infitar"—a violent, deep cleaving or splitting open, completely breaking its structural integrity.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi explains that "Infatarat" specifically denotes a structural tear caused by an overwhelming internal pressure or command, not just a gentle opening. The sky splits open out of sheer terror and submission to the command of the Creator as the Day of Judgment commences.

Verse 2
وَإِذَا ٱلْكَوَاكِبُ ٱنتَثَرَتْ
"And when the stars fall, scattering,"
Plain Understanding
The destruction extends beyond the earth's atmosphere to the celestial bodies. "Al-Kawakib" refers to planets and prominent stars. They will lose the gravitational forces anchoring them to their orbits, resulting in them violently scattering, colliding, and dispersing into the cosmic void ("Intatharat").

Verse 3
وَإِذَا ٱلْبِحَارُ فُجِّرَتْ
"And when the seas are erupted"
Plain Understanding
The catastrophe strikes the surface of the earth. The massive bodies of water will experience "Tafjeer"—a violent bursting forth. The natural barriers separating fresh water from saltwater, and oceans from dry land, will be entirely annihilated, causing a global, catastrophic flood that merges all bodies of water into one chaotic, surging mass.

Verse 4
وَإِذَا ٱلْقُبُورُ بُعْثِرَتْ
"And when the [contents of] graves are scattered,"
Plain Understanding
The final physical event triggers the human reckoning. The graves of the earth will be "Bu'thirat"—violently turned inside out. The earth will vomit forth all of the dead it has consumed since the beginning of human history, throwing their bodies to the surface for the Resurrection.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah notes the profound connection between these four verses. The sky opens, the stars scatter, the oceans burst, and the graves are overturned. Every single physical structure in the universe that previously contained or hid something is forced to violently expose its secrets. This physical exposure perfectly mirrors the impending spiritual exposure of the human heart.

Verse 5
عَلِمَتْ نَفْسٌۭ مَّا قَدَّمَتْ وَأَخَّرَتْ
"A soul will [then] know what it has put forth and kept back."
Plain Understanding
This is the psychological result of the four cosmic events. As every human stands upon the ruined earth, a terrifying clarity will wash over them. Every single individual will know exactly two things: what deeds they physically performed during their life ("put forth"), and what legacy, traditions, or unfulfilled obligations they left behind after their death ("kept back").
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali deeply analyzes "what it kept back." A person does not just answer for the sins they committed while breathing. If they established a corrupt practice, taught people to sin, or built an institution of oppression, they will bear the accumulating weight of every sin committed by others long after they were buried. The true horror of the grave is the sin that continues to earn negative interest after death.

Verse 6
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ مَا غَرَّكَ بِرَبِّكَ ٱلْكَرِيمِ
"O mankind, what has deceived you concerning your Lord, the Generous,"
Plain Understanding
God pauses the description of the apocalypse to deliver one of the most emotional, direct, and piercing rebukes in the entire Qur'an. Addressing humanity directly, God asks: What possible delusion or arrogance tricked you into rebelling against the very Lord who has been endlessly, unconditionally generous ("Al-Kareem") to you?
Historical Context
When this verse was recited, the great early scholar Al-Fudayl bin Iyad wept profusely and answered the verse out loud, saying: "O Lord, it was Your very generosity that deceived me!" The human ego is so perverse that instead of responding to God's continuous blessings with gratitude and obedience, it views God's patience and continued provision as a green light to sin further, mistaking His generosity for weakness or absence.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi emphasizes the deliberate use of the name "Al-Kareem" (The Generous) rather than "Al-Muntaqim" (The Avenger). God is highlighting the sheer ugliness of the human crime. It is one thing to rebel against a tyrant who starves you; it is a profound, sickening betrayal to rebel against the One who continuously feeds, heals, and shelters you while you actively disobey Him.

Verse 7
ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكَ فَسَوَّىٰكَ فَعَدَلَكَ
"Who created you, proportioned you, and balanced you?"
Plain Understanding
God immediately lists the physical manifestations of His generosity to prove the absurdity of human arrogance. He did not merely bring you into existence; He "proportioned" you (designing a flawless biological framework with functional organs) and "balanced" you (giving you an upright posture, physical symmetry, and the intellect required to govern the earth).

Verse 8
فِىٓ أَىِّ صُورَةٍۢ مَّا شَآءَ رَكَّبَكَ
"In whatever form He willed has He assembled you."
Plain Understanding
The biological proof concludes. The human being had absolutely no say in their physical appearance, their genetic makeup, their lineage, or their cognitive baseline. God assembled ("Rakkabaka") the complex human machine precisely according to His sovereign will. To use this God-given body to disobey the God who built it is the height of deception.
Purification of the Self (Al-Gilani)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani notes: "You walk arrogantly upon the earth, boasting of a handsome face, a sharp intellect, or a strong body, forgetting entirely that the very tongue you use to boast, and the very brain you use to plot, were assembled by the King you are rebelling against. You fight Him using His own weapons."

Verse 9
كَلَّا بَلْ تُكَذِّبُونَ بِٱلدِّينِ
"No! But you deny the Recompense."
Plain Understanding
God dismisses all philosophical excuses for their behavior with a sharp "Kalla!" (No!). The root cause of human arrogance and sin is not a lack of evidence, nor is it genuine ignorance. The psychological root of the disease is strictly that they deliberately, actively deny the reality of "Ad-Deen"—the Day of Recompense and Absolute Accountability.

Verse 10
وَإِنَّ عَلَيْكُمْ لَحَـٰفِظِينَ
"And indeed, [appointed] over you are keepers,"
Plain Understanding
To shatter the illusion that they can escape accountability by hiding their sins, God reveals the unseen surveillance system. He confirms that every single human being has a team of "Hafidheen" (Angelic Keepers or Guardians) assigned strictly to them, permanently attached to their presence.

Verse 11
كِرَامًۭا كَـٰتِبِينَ
"Noble and recording;"
Plain Understanding
God establishes the credentials of this surveillance team. They are "Kiram" (incredibly honorable, noble, and incorruptible) and "Katibeen" (meticulous, flawless scribes who write down every detail). Because they are inherently noble, they cannot be bribed, they do not make errors in recording, and they are never influenced by human emotions.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi explains that describing them as "Noble" (Kiram) serves a profound psychological purpose. In worldly life, a person feels immense shame if a highly respected, noble elder catches them committing a filthy act. By informing the believer that incredibly pure, noble angels are forced to watch their private sins, God seeks to trigger the believer's sense of shame (Haya'), preventing the sin before it occurs.

Verse 12
يَعْلَمُونَ مَا تَفْعَلُونَ
"They know whatever you do."
Plain Understanding
The scope of their recording is absolute. These angels are not merely guessing or observing from a distance. They are fully aware of, and comprehensively document, "whatever you do"—encompassing overt physical actions, whispered conversations, and the deepest, hidden intentions of the heart. The dossier they compile will be the exact book unrolled on the Day of Judgment.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali issues a piercing warning: "You lock your doors, close your windows, and hide in the darkness of the night, terrified that a human being with no power over your soul might see your sin. Yet, you brazenly commit the act while two Noble Angels sit on your shoulders documenting it for the King. It proves that you fear the creation more than the Creator, which is the very essence of the deception mentioned in Verse 6."

Verse 13
إِنَّ ٱلْأَبْرَارَ لَفِى نَعِيمٍۢ
"Indeed, the righteous will be in pleasure,"
Plain Understanding
God establishes the final outcome for the successful group. The "Abrar" (the truly virtuous who were faithful to their covenants and excelled in goodness) will be immersed in "Na'eem"—a state of total, multifaceted bliss, encompassing both physical luxury and internal, psychological peace.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi notes that "Na'eem" is expressed in the indefinite form to signify its incomprehensible magnitude. This pleasure begins in the heart during the worldly life through the sweetness of faith, continues in the grave (Barzakh), and reaches its ultimate, physical perfection in the Gardens of Paradise.

Verse 14
وَإِنَّ ٱلْفُجَّارَ لَفِى جَحِيمٍۢ
"And indeed, the wicked will be in Hellfire,"
Plain Understanding
The terrifying counter-reality is presented. The "Fujjar" (the wicked transgressors who tore through the boundaries of God's law) are consigned to "Jaheem"—the intensely roaring, fiercely blazing Fire. There is no middle ground in the final reckoning.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali points out the linguistic precision: "Fujjar" comes from the same root as "Infitar" (to tear/split). Because these individuals spent their lives "tearing" through the sacred boundaries of morality and the rights of others, their final destination is the Fire that "tears" through the flesh. The punishment is the exact image of the sin.

Verse 15
يَصْلَوْنَهَا يَوْمَ ٱلدِّينِ
"They will [enter to] burn therein on the Day of Recompense,"
Plain Understanding
The timing of the sentence is confirmed. On "Yawm al-Deen" (the Day of strict, calculated Recompense), the wicked will be forced to physically enter and endure the burning of the Fire. It is the inescapable fulfillment of the warnings they mocked in the world.

Verse 16
وَمَا هُمْ عَنْهَا بِغَآئِبِينَ
"And never thereof will they be absent."
Plain Understanding
God shatters any hope of escape or relief. Once they enter the Fire, they will never be allowed to leave, nor will they experience even a momentary absence or unconsciousness to escape the pain. Their presence within the punishment is permanent and uninterrupted.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah reflects on the severe irony of this verse: In the Dunya, these individuals were always "absent" from the remembrance of God and "absent" from their prayers. Therefore, in the Akhirah, God ensures they will never be "absent" from the consequences of their choices. You are forced to be present with what you prioritized.

"And what can make you know what is the Day of Recompense?"
Plain Understanding
God poses a rhetorical question to highlight the limits of human cognition. No amount of worldly intellect, philosophy, or imagination can truly encompass or calculate the absolute gravity and terrifying reality of the Day of Recompense until it is witnessed.

Verse 18
ثُمَّ مَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا يَوْمُ ٱلدِّينِ
"Then, what can make you know what is the Day of Recompense?"
Plain Understanding
The question is repeated verbatim. This repetition serves to amplify the awe, terror, and importance of the Day, indicating that it is a reality far beyond any initial human estimation.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi explains that the first question refers to the terrifying *events* of the Day (the cosmic collapse), while the second question refers to the terrifying *judgment* of the Day. God is emphasizing that both the physical environment and the legal reckoning of that Day are beyond human comprehension.

Verse 19 ۩
يَوْمَ لَا تَمْلِكُ نَفْسٌۭ لِّنَفْسٍۢ شَيْـًۭٔا ۖ وَٱلْأَمْرُ يَوْمَئِذٍۢ لِّلَّهِ
"It is the Day when a soul will not possess for [another] soul [power to do] a thing; and the command, that Day, is [entirely] with Allah."
Plain Understanding
The Surah concludes with the definitive, paralyzing definition of the Day of Judgment. It is the Day of absolute individualization: no human being—no matter how powerful, wealthy, or loved—will possess even a shred of power to benefit, protect, or intercede for another soul. All worldly hierarchies, intercessions, and tribal protections are abolished. In that moment, absolute command and sovereignty are physically and visibly returned to Allah alone.
Historical Context
This verse directly attacked the pagan Arab concept of "Shafa'ah" (intercession). The Quraysh believed their stone idols or their powerful tribal leaders would act as mediators to "protect" them from God's wrath. This verse shatters that entire pagan framework, establishing that on the Day of Judgment, every soul stands in absolute, terrifying isolation before the King.
Purification of the Self (Al-Gilani)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani observes: "If you know that on that Day no soul can help you, why do you spend your life exhausting your soul to please other people? Why do you fear the judgment of creation more than the judgment of the Creator? Align your heart with the One who holds the command on the Day when everyone else will be a silent prisoner."

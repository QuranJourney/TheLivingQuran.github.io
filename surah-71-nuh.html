Surah Nuh

Noah • Makkah • 28 Verses
A profound and deeply personal account of Prophet Noah's 950-year mission. This Surah details his immense patience, his varied methods of calling his people to God, and his heartfelt supplication regarding their stubborn rejection.
Verse 1
إِنَّآ أَرْسَلْنَا نُوحًا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِهِۦٓ أَنْ أَنذِرْ قَوْمَكَ مِن قَبْلِ أَن يَأْتِيَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۭ
"Indeed, We sent Noah to his people, [saying], 'Warn your people before there comes to them a painful punishment.'"
Plain Understanding
God initiates the narrative by establishing Noah's primary mission. He was sent to his people with a clear and urgent directive: to warn them of severe consequences if they continued in their disbelief. This demonstrates God's mercy in providing a clear warning before enacting justice.

Verse 2
قَالَ يَـٰقَوْمِ إِنِّى لَكُمْ نَذِيرٌۭ مُّبِينٌ
"He said, 'O my people, indeed I am to you a clear warner,'"
Plain Understanding
Noah addresses his people with compassion, calling them "my people." He clarifies his role to them: he is not seeking power or wealth, but is simply serving as a plain and evident warner sent to deliver a message for their own benefit.

Verse 3
أَنِ ٱعْبُدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱتَّقُوهُ وَأَطِيعُونِ
"'[Commanding you] to worship Allah, fear Him, and obey me.'"
Plain Understanding
Noah outlines the core of his message, which consists of three straightforward principles: to dedicate worship entirely to God, to maintain reverence and consciousness of Him (Taqwa), and to obey the Messenger who conveys His guidance.

Verse 4
يَغْفِرْ لَكُم مِّن ذُنُوبِكُمْ وَيُؤَخِّرْكُمْ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّى ۚ إِنَّ أَجَلَ ٱللَّهِ إِذَا جَآءَ لَا يُؤَخَّرُ ۖ لَوْ كُنتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ
"'Allah will forgive you of your sins and delay you for a specified term. Indeed, the time [set by] Allah, when it comes, will not be delayed, if you only knew.'"
Plain Understanding
Noah explains the direct benefits of their obedience: God will forgive their past sins and allow them to live out their natural lives in peace. However, he warns them that God has an appointed time for judgment. Once that final decree arrives, no amount of pleading can delay it.

"He said, 'My Lord, indeed I invited my people [to truth] night and day.'"
Plain Understanding
The Surah shifts to Noah's intimate prayer to God. He presents his profound dedication to his mission, expressing that he exhausted every possible opportunity, preaching to his people continuously through the night and the day for centuries.

"'But my invitation increased them in nothing except flight.'"
Plain Understanding
Despite Noah's sincere and persistent efforts, the reaction of his people was entirely negative. The more he called them toward guidance and forgiveness, the further they actively ran away from the message.

Verse 7
وَإِنِّى كُلَّمَا دَعَوْتُهُمْ لِتَغْفِرَ لَهُمْ جَعَلُوٓا۟ أَصَـٰبِعَهُمْ فِىٓ ءَاذَانِهِمْ وَٱسْتَغْشَوْا۟ ثِيَابَهُمْ وَأَصَرُّوا۟ وَٱسْتَكْبَرُوا۟ ٱسْتِكْبَارًۭا
"'And indeed, every time I invited them that You may forgive them, they put their fingers in their ears, covered themselves with their garments, persisted, and were arrogant with great arrogance.'"
Plain Understanding
Noah details the physical and psychological extent of their rejection. They would literally plug their ears to avoid hearing his words and pull their clothing over their faces to avoid seeing him. They were stubborn in their disbelief and consumed by a deep-seated pride that prevented them from accepting the truth.

Refusing to give up, Noah diversified his methods. When individual approaches failed, he took his message to public gatherings, speaking openly and loudly so that the entire community would hear the warning.

Plain Understanding
Noah utilized every form of communication available to him. He addressed them in large public announcements, and he also sought them out in private, speaking to them gently in secret. He tailored his approach in hopes that at least one method would reach their hearts.

"'And I said, 'Ask forgiveness of your Lord. Indeed, He is ever a Perpetual Forgiver.'"
Plain Understanding
The core of Noah's preaching was an appeal to God's mercy. He assured his people that regardless of their past idolatry, if they simply turned back and asked for forgiveness, God's nature is to forgive constantly and completely.

Noah questions their lack of reverence. He asks them to reflect on why they do not respect the majesty and greatness of the Creator. Their hearts had become so hardened that they lost the natural awe that a creation should feel toward God.

"'While He has created you in stages?'"
Plain Understanding
To help them understand God's greatness, Noah points them to their own biological existence. He reminds them that God created them through careful, distinct stages of development in the womb. Recognizing the complexity of their own creation should lead them to revere the One who created them.

"Do you not consider how Allah has created seven heavens in layers,"
Plain Understanding
Noah directs the attention of his people away from their own bodies and toward the vast universe. He asks them to reflect on the perfect, layered structure of the seven heavens above them, presenting it as undeniable evidence of God's immense creative power and orderly design.

Verse 16
وَجَعَلَ ٱلْقَمَرَ فِيهِنَّ نُورًۭا وَجَعَلَ ٱلشَّمْسَ سِرَاجًۭا
"And made the moon therein a [reflected] light and made the sun a burning lamp?"
Plain Understanding
He continues to highlight celestial signs, drawing a precise distinction between the light of the moon and the sun. He describes the moon as a source of gentle light (Noor) and the sun as a radiant, heat-producing lamp (Siraj), demonstrating God's meticulous engineering of the solar system to sustain human life.

"And Allah has caused you to grow from the earth a [progressive] growth."
Plain Understanding
Noah connects human existence directly to the earth. Just as God causes plants to sprout and grow gradually from the soil, He originated human life from the earth (through Adam) and sustains our physical growth through the food that the earth produces.

"Then He will return you into it and extract you [another] extraction."
Plain Understanding
This verse establishes the inevitable cycle of human mortality and resurrection. Noah warns his people that their bodies will return to the earth in death, but that is not the end. God will undoubtedly extract them from the earth once again for the Day of Judgment.

Verse 21

"Noah said, 'My Lord, indeed they have disobeyed me and followed one whose wealth and children will not increase him except in loss."
Plain Understanding
After presenting all these logical arguments, Noah returns to his supplication to God. He reports that despite his efforts, the people rejected his message. Instead of following the truth, the masses chose to blindly follow their wealthy and influential leaders—leaders whose worldly success only deepened their arrogance and spiritual ruin.

Verse 23
وَقَالُوا۟ لَا تَذَرُنَّ ءَالِهَتَكُمْ وَلَا تَذَرُنَّ وَدًّۭا وَلَا سُوَاعًۭا وَلَا يَغُوثَ وَيَعُوقَ وَنَسْرًۭا
"And said, 'Never leave your gods and never leave Wadd or Suwa' or Yaghuth and Ya'uq and Nasr.'"
Plain Understanding
The corrupt leaders instructed their followers to hold tightly to their false religion. They specifically named five prominent idols, which according to historical tradition, were originally the names of righteous men from earlier generations. Over time, reverence for these men decayed into full-blown worship, anchoring the society in misguidance.

"And already they have misled many. And, [my Lord], do not increase the wrongdoers except in error."
Plain Understanding
Noah acknowledges the devastating success of the leaders' conspiracy; they had successfully corrupted the vast majority of the population. Seeing that their hostility was permanent and incurable, Noah makes a decisive prayer, asking God to allow the oppressors to sink further into the misguidance they deliberately chose.

Verse 25
مِّمَّا خَطِيٓـَٔـٰتِهِمْ أُغْرِقُوا۟ فَأُدْخِلُوا۟ نَارًۭا فَلَمْ يَجِدُوا۟ لَهُم مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ أَنصَارًۭا
"Because of their sins they were drowned and put into the Fire, and they found not for themselves besides Allah any helpers."
Plain Understanding
God interjects to reveal the ultimate consequence of their actions. Because of their persistent rebellion and sins, they were destroyed by the great flood in this world, and subsequently condemned to the punishment of the Fire in the Hereafter. None of the idols they worshipped or the wealth they hoarded could protect them from God's judgment.

Verse 27
إِنَّكَ إِن تَذَرْهُمْ يُضِلُّوا۟ عِبَادَكَ وَلَا يَلِدُوٓا۟ إِلَّا فَاجِرًۭا كَفَّارًۭا
"Indeed, if You leave them, they will mislead Your servants and not beget except [every] wicked one and [confirmed] disbeliever."
Plain Understanding
Noah explains the logical reasoning behind his severe prayer. The corruption had become systemic and generational. If left alive, these people would not only continue to oppress the few believers, but they would also indoctrinate their children in the exact same wicked traditions, ensuring an endless cycle of disbelief and hostility.

Verse 28
رَّبِّ ٱغْفِرْ لِى وَلِوَٰلِدَىَّ وَلِمَن دَخَلَ بَيْتِىَ مُؤْمِنًۭا وَلِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنَـٰتِ وَلَا تَزِدِ ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ إِلَّا تَبَارًۢا
"My Lord, forgive me and my parents and whoever enters my house a believer and the believing men and believing women. And do not increase the wrongdoers except in destruction.'"
Plain Understanding
The Surah concludes with Noah's comprehensive and compassionate prayer. Despite being a mighty Prophet, he humbles himself by asking for personal forgiveness first. He then prays for his parents, those who sought refuge in his home, and extends his supplication to all believing men and women throughout time. He finalizes his plea against the oppressors, asking that their injustice only results in their ultimate ruin.

Surah Fatir

The Originator • Makkah • 45 Verses
The Absolute Monopoly of Grace. A majestic revelation asserting God's exclusive control over all creation and mercy, dismantling the ego's pursuit of worldly honor, and establishing the profound powerlessness of false deities.
Verse 1
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ فَاطِرِ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ جَاعِلِ ٱلْمَلَٰٓئِكَةِ رُسُلًا أُو۟لِىٓ أَجْنِحَةٍۢ مَّثْنَىٰ وَثُلَٰثَ وَرُبَٰعَ ۚ يَزِيدُ فِى ٱلْخَلْقِ مَا يَشَآءُ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍۢ قَدِيرٌۭ
"[All] praise is [due] to Allah, Creator of the heavens and the earth, [who] made the angels messengers having wings, two or three or four. He increases in creation what He wills. Indeed, Allah is over all things competent."
Plain Understanding
All praise rightfully belongs exclusively to God, who originated the universe from nothing. He created the angels to serve as His envoys, equipping them with varying numbers of wings—two, three, or four. He expands and adds to His creation however He chooses, for His power is absolute.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Fatir" specifically denotes the Originator—the One who brings something into existence without any prior model or precedent. The angels' wings represent their varying capacities, velocity, and ranks in executing Divine commands. The Prophet ﷺ reported seeing the Angel Gabriel (Jibril) in his original form possessing six hundred wings, demonstrating that the numbers mentioned here are not the absolute limit, but an indication of God's limitless capacity to increase ("Yazeedu") creation.

Verse 2
مَّا يَفْتَحِ ٱللَّهُ لِلنَّاسِ مِن رَّحْمَةٍۢ فَلَا مُمْسِكَ لَهَا ۖ وَمَا يُمْسِكْ فَلَا مُرْسِلَ لَهُۥ مِنۢ بَعْدِهِۦ ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْعَزِيزُ ٱلْحَكِيمُ
"Whatever Allah grants to people of mercy - none can withhold it; and whatever He withholds - none can release it thereafter. And He is the Exalted in Might, the Wise."
Plain Understanding
God possesses a total monopoly over grace and provision. If God opens a door of mercy (wealth, health, guidance) for a person, no force in the universe can shut it. And if God chooses to close a door, absolutely no one can force it open. He is Supreme in power, yet perfectly Wise in His distribution.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
This verse is the ultimate cure for the disease of anxiety and human dependency. If you deeply internalize that the keys to the vaults of Mercy are held strictly by "Al-Aziz" (The Unconquerable), you will cease begging at the doors of the creation. The human heart only fears men when it forgets the absolute, unshared sovereignty of the Master over the flow of provision.

Verse 3
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱذْكُرُوا۟ نِعْمَتَ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ ۚ هَلْ مِنْ خَٰلِقٍ غَيْرُ ٱللَّهِ يَرْزُقُكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ۖ فَأَنَّىٰ تُؤْفَكُونَ
"O mankind, remember the favor of Allah upon you. Is there any creator other than Allah who provides for you from the heaven and earth? There is no deity except Him, so how are you deluded?"
Plain Understanding
God appeals directly to human intellect: Remember all the blessings you enjoy. Is there genuinely any other creator who sends down rain from the sky or grows food from the soil? Since He is the sole Provider, He is the sole Deity. How can you possibly be turned away from this obvious logic?
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Tu'fakoon" (deluded/turned away). The ego is inherently forgetful. It consumes the provision of the heaven and earth daily, yet attributes its survival to the employer, the economy, or its own intellect. God demands active, conscious remembrance ("Udhkuru") to trace the bread back to the Baker. Failure to trace the blessing to its Source is the essence of spiritual delusion.

Verse 4
وَإِن يُكَذِّبُوكَ فَقَدْ كُذِّبَتْ رُسُلٌۭ مِّن قَبْلِكَ ۚ وَإِلَى ٱللَّهِ تُرْجَعُ ٱلْأُمُورُ
"And if they deny you, [O Muhammad] - already were messengers denied before you. And to Allah are returned [all] matters."
Plain Understanding
God comforts the Prophet: If your people reject your message, do not be broken by it. Every single Messenger before you faced the exact same rejection. Ultimately, every affair and outcome is returned to God for final judgment.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The rejection of the Prophet is not a failure of his delivery; it is the manifestation of an ancient, recurring human sickness. By anchoring the Prophet's emotional state to the continuum of Prophetic history, God relieves him of the burden of the outcome. "To Allah are returned all matters" signifies that your only duty is the labor; the harvest belongs entirely to the Lord.

Verse 5
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِنَّ وَعْدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقٌّۭ ۖ فَلَا تَغُرَّنَّكُمُ ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۖ وَلَا يَغُرَّنَّكُم بِٱللَّهِ ٱلْغَرُورُ
"O mankind, indeed the promise of Allah is truth, so let not the worldly life delude you and be not deceived about Allah by the Deceiver."
Plain Understanding
A universal warning: The Day of Judgment promised by God is an absolute certainty. Therefore, do not allow the temporary glitter and distractions of this life to trick you, and do not let Satan (the Deceiver) trick you regarding God's patience.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Al-Gharoor" (The Deceiver) is Satan. His primary mechanism of deception is weaponizing God's attributes against the believer. He whispers, "Commit the sin, God is Forgiving. Delay your repentance, you have time." He uses the vastness of Divine Mercy as a tool to induce spiritual complacency, tricking the soul into bankruptcy.

Verse 6
إِنَّ ٱلشَّيْطَٰنَ لَكُمْ عَدُوٌّۭ فَٱتَّخِذُوهُ عَدُوًّا ۚ إِنَّمَا يَدْعُوا۟ حِزْبَهُۥ لِيَكُونُوا۟ مِنْ أَصْحَٰبِ ٱلسَّعِيرِ
"Indeed, Satan is an enemy to you; so take him as an enemy. He only invites his party to be among the companions of the Blaze."
Plain Understanding
God issues a strict directive: Satan is engaged in active warfare against you, so you must treat him as a hostile enemy. His only goal in recruiting followers is to ensure they burn with him in the Hellfire.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Fattakhidhoohu 'aduwwa" (so take him as an enemy). It is not enough to merely acknowledge Satan's existence; one must actively adopt a defensive and offensive posture against his whispers. If an enemy soldier breaks into your house, you do not invite him to tea. Yet the ego frequently entertains the whispers of the devil, negotiating with the very entity whose stated objective is its eternal destruction.

Verse 7
ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌۭ شَدِيدٌۭ ۖ وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّٰلِحَٰتِ لَهُم مَّغْفِرَةٌۭ وَأَجْرٌۭ كَبِيرٌ
"Those who disbelieve will have a severe punishment, and those who believe and do righteous deeds will have forgiveness and great reward."
Plain Understanding
The ultimate sorting of humanity: Those who choose to reject the truth will face intense punishment. Conversely, those who maintain faith and prove it through good actions will receive complete forgiveness for their flaws and a massive reward.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Notice the order of the believer's reward: "Maghfirah" (forgiveness) precedes the "Ajr Kabeer" (great reward). The soul must be cleansed of its impurities before it can be adorned with the garments of Paradise. The removal of the disease must precede the application of the perfume.

Verse 8
أَفَمَن زُيِّنَ لَهُۥ سُوٓءُ عَمَلِهِۦ فَرَءَاهُ حَسَنًۭا ۖ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُضِلُّ مَن يَشَآءُ وَيَهْدِى مَن يَشَآءُ ۖ فَلَا تَذْهَبْ نَفْسُكَ عَلَيْهِمْ حَسَرَٰتٍ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَلِيمٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ
"Then is one to whom the evil of his deed has been made attractive so he considers it good [like one rightly guided]? For indeed, Allah sends astray whom He wills and guides whom He wills. So do not let yourself perish over them in regret. Indeed, Allah is Knowing of what they do."
Plain Understanding
Is the person whose evil actions have been artificially beautified—so much so that they actually believe they are doing good—equal to a guided believer? God leaves to stray those who embrace arrogance, and guides those who seek truth. Therefore, Prophet, do not destroy yourself with grief over their choices. God knows exactly what they are doing.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The most lethal spiritual state is "Zuyyina" (artificial beautification). The pagan leaders of Mecca genuinely believed their persecution of the weak and their idolatry were noble, traditional virtues. When the ego successfully rebrands sin as righteousness, the disease becomes incurable because the patient refuses the medicine. God relieves the Prophet of the psychological burden: you cannot guide a man who is actively in love with his own darkness.

Verse 9
وَٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَرْسَلَ ٱلرِّيَٰحَ فَتُثِيرُ سَحَابًۭا فَسُقْنَٰهُ إِلَىٰ بَلَدٍۢ مَّيِّتٍۢ فَأَحْيَيْنَا بِهِ ٱلْأَرْضَ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا ۚ كَذَٰلِكَ ٱلنُّشُورُ
"And it is Allah who sends the winds, and they stir up clouds, and We drive them to a dead land and give life thereby to the earth after its lifelessness. Thus is the resurrection."
Plain Understanding
God points to the weather as empirical evidence for the afterlife: God sends the winds to gather the clouds, steering them towards barren, dry land, using the rain to bring the dead soil back to vibrant life. The physical resurrection of human bodies from the grave will operate on this exact same principle.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The natural world is not a secular mechanism; it is a continuous, living theological textbook. The resurrection is not a bizarre, unprecedented event; it is the cosmic application of a botanical reality you witness every spring. The same Will that commands a dead seed to sprout commands a dead bone to reassemble.

Verse 10
مَن كَانَ يُرِيدُ ٱلْعِزَّةَ فَلِلَّهِ ٱلْعِزَّةُ جَمِيعًا ۚ إِلَيْهِ يَصْعَدُ ٱلْكَلِمُ ٱلطَّيِّبُ وَٱلْعَمَلُ ٱلصَّٰلِحُ يَرْفَعُهُۥ ۚ وَٱلَّذِينَ يَمْكُرُونَ ٱلسَّيِّـَٔاتِ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌۭ شَدِيدٌۭ ۖ وَمَكْرُ أُو۟لَٰٓئِكَ هُوَ يَبُورُ
"Whoever desires honor - then to Allah belongs all honor. To Him ascends good speech, and righteous work raises it. But they who plot evil deeds will have a severe punishment, and the plotting of those - it will perish."
Plain Understanding
If anyone seeks true honor and power, they must realize that absolute honor belongs exclusively to God. Only pure, truthful speech rises up to Him, and it is validated and lifted by actual righteous actions. Those who try to gain power through evil conspiracies will face a crushing punishment, and their plots will ultimately self-destruct.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Izzah" denotes invulnerability, honor, and prestige. The Meccan elite sought Izzah through wealth, tribal alliances, and idol worship. God shatters this paradigm: Izzah is a Divine monopoly. You cannot lease it from the creation; you must draw it directly from the Creator. Furthermore, "good speech" (Dhikr/Faith) is necessary, but it remains grounded unless the "righteous work" (Amal) acts as its wings to carry it up to the Divine Court.

Verse 11
وَٱللَّهُ خَلَقَكُم مِّن تُرَابٍۢ ثُمَّ مِن نُّطْفَةٍۢ ثُمَّ جَعَلَكُمْ أَزْوَٰجًۭا ۚ وَمَا تَحْمِلُ مِنْ أُنثَىٰ وَلَا تَضَعُ إِلَّا بِعِلْمِهِۦ ۚ وَمَا يُعَمَّرُ مِن مُّعَمَّرٍۢ وَلَا يُنقَصُ مِنْ عُمُرِهِۦٓ إِلَّا فِى كِتَٰبٍ ۚ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ يَسِيرٌۭ
"And Allah created you from dust, then from a sperm-drop; then He made you mates. And no female conceives nor does she give birth except with His knowledge. And no aged person is granted length of life nor is a part of his life reduced but that it is in a register. Indeed, that for Allah is easy."
Plain Understanding
God reminds humanity of their fragile origins: First created from dirt, then reproduced through a tiny drop of fluid, and designed into pairs (male and female). No pregnancy or birth occurs without His direct knowledge. The exact length of every single human life—whether prolonged or cut short—is meticulously recorded in a Divine book. Managing all of this is effortlessly easy for God.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The verse obliterates the foundations of human arrogance by tracing the human pedigree back to its humiliating origins ("Turab" - dirt). It then establishes absolute Divine Omniscience. The progression of every single cell in a mother's womb, and the subtraction of every single second from a dying man's lifespan, is rigorously documented. The ego's illusion of controlling its own destiny is dismantled by the reality of the "Kitab" (the Divine Register).

Verse 12
وَمَا يَسْتَوِى ٱلْبَحْرَانِ هَٰذَا عَذْبٌۭ فُرَاتٌۭ سَآئِغٌۭ شَرَابُهُۥ وَهَٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌۭ ۖ وَمِن كُلٍّۢ تَأْكُلُونَ لَحْمًۭا طَرِيًّۭا وَتَسْتَخْرِجُونَ حِلْيَةًۭ تَلْبَسُونَهَا ۖ وَتَرَى ٱلْفُلْكَ فِيهِ مَوَاخِرَ لِتَبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ
"And not alike are the two bodies of water. One is fresh and sweet, palatable for drinking, and one is salty and bitter. And from each you eat tender meat and extract ornaments which you wear, and you see the ships plowing through [them] that you might seek of His bounty; and perhaps you will be grateful."
Plain Understanding
God contrasts two bodies of water: sweet rivers and salty oceans. Though they are completely different in taste, God's generosity ensures that you extract fresh fish and beautiful pearls/coral from both of them. You sail heavy ships through both to conduct trade. All of this is designed so that you might recognize His favor and be grateful.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
A profound metaphor for human diversity. The believer (fresh water) and the disbeliever (salty water) are fundamentally not equal in the sight of God. Yet, the Mercy of God is so vast that He provides worldly sustenance (meat, ornaments, commerce) from both environments. The Dunya is a shared table; the Akhirah is where the fresh and the salty are permanently separated.

Verse 13
يُولِجُ ٱلَّيْلَ فِى ٱلنَّهَارِ وَيُولِجُ ٱلنَّهَارَ فِى ٱلَّيْلِ وَسَخَّرَ ٱلشَّمْسَ وَٱلْقَمَرَ كُلٌّۭ يَجْرِى لِأَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى ۚ ذَٰلِكُمُ ٱللَّهُ رَبِّكُمْ لَهُ ٱلْمُلْكُ ۚ وَٱلَّذِينَ تَدْعُونَ مِن دُونِهِۦ مَا يَمْلِكُونَ مِن قِطْمِيرٍ
"He causes the night to enter the day, and He causes the day to enter the night and has subjected the sun and the moon - each running [its course] for a specified term. That is Allah, your Lord; to Him belongs dominion, and those you invoke other than Him do not possess [as much as] the membrane of a date seed."
Plain Understanding
God commands the shifting of seasons, weaving the night into the day and the day into the night. He governs the precise orbits of the sun and moon until the end of time. That is your true Master, who owns everything. In stark contrast, the false idols you pray to do not even own the microscopic, translucent skin wrapped around a date pit.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Qitmeer" (the thin membrane on a date stone). The Qur'an uses the most insignificant, worthless object conceivable to mathematically quantify the power of the false gods. When the ego relies on wealth, status, or political alliances instead of God, it is leaning its entire weight against a structure weaker than the skin of a date pit.

Verse 14
إِن تَدْعُوهُمْ لَا يَسْمَعُوا۟ دُعَآءَكُمْ وَلَوْ سَمِعُوا۟ مَا ٱسْتَجَابُوا۟ لَكُمْ ۖ وَيَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَٰمَةِ يَكْفُرُونَ بِشِرْكِكُمْ ۚ وَلَا يُنَبِّئُكَ مِثْلُ خَبِيرٍۢ
"If you invoke them, they do not hear your supplication; and if they heard, they would not respond to you. And on the Day of Resurrection they will deny your association. And none can inform you like [one] Acquainted [with all matters]."
Plain Understanding
If you cry out to these false gods, they are physically deaf and cannot hear you. Even if they could somehow hear, they possess absolutely no power to answer or help you. And the ultimate tragedy: on Judgment Day, they will publicly disown you and reject your worship. No one can give you the truth regarding this reality like the All-Aware God.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The ultimate betrayal of Shirk (idolatry). The polytheists invested their entire spiritual devotion into entities they believed would act as intercessors. God reveals the future courtroom scene: the very entities they worshipped will take the witness stand and declare, "We never asked them to worship us, and we reject their actions." The investment yields a return of absolute zero.

Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)

Every heartbeat and breath is a desperate borrowing from the Divine Treasury. "Al-Ghani" (The Rich/Free of Need) establishes that our worship does not enrich Him, nor does our sin impoverish Him. To recognize this fundamental poverty is the highest station of spiritual maturity.

Verse 18
وَلَا تَزِرُ وَازِرَةٌۭ وِزْرَ أُخْرَىٰ ۚ وَإِن تَدْعُ مُثْقَلَةٌ إِلَىٰ حِمْلِهَا لَا يُحْمَلْ مِنْهُ شَىْءٌۭ وَلَوْ كَانَ ذَا قُرْبَىٰٓ ۗ إِنَّمَا تُنذِرُ ٱلَّذِينَ يَخْشَوْنَ رَبَّهُم بِٱلْغَيْبِ وَأَقَامُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ ۚ وَمَن تَزَكَّىٰ فَإِنَّمَا يَتَزَكَّىٰ لِنَفْسِهِۦ ۚ وَإِلَى ٱللَّهِ ٱلْمَصِيرُ
"And no bearer of burdens will bear the burden of another. And if a heavily laden soul calls [another] to [carry some of] its load, nothing of it will be carried, even if he should be a close relative. You can only warn those who fear their Lord unseen and have established prayer. And whoever purifies himself only purifies himself for [the benefit of] his soul. And to Allah is the [final] destination."
Plain Understanding
On Judgment Day, absolute individual accountability reigns. No soul can carry the sins of another. If a soul drowning in sins begs a relative to carry just a fraction of its punishment, the relative will refuse. Therefore, Prophet, your warnings only benefit those who fear God in private and pray. Whoever cleanses their soul from sin only benefits themselves. Ultimately, everyone returns to God.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse fundamentally dismantles the theology of vicarious atonement and tribal salvation. The pagan Arabs believed their tribal chiefs would protect them in the afterlife. The verse severs all biological and social safety nets ("even if he should be a close relative"). On the Day of Standing, the mother will flee from her infant. Spiritual purification ("Tazakka") is a strictly solitary, non-transferable labor.

"Not equal are the blind and the seeing,"
Plain Understanding
God presents a series of undeniable opposites: A person who is completely blind to reality is never equal to a person whose eyes are wide open to the truth.

إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يُمْسِكُ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ أَن تَزُولَا ۚ وَلَئِن زَالَتَآ إِنْ أَمْسَكَهُمَا مِنْ أَحَدٍۢ مِّنۢ بَعْدِهِۦٓ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ كَانَ حَلِيمًا غَفُورًۭا
"Indeed, Allah holds the heavens and the earth, lest they cease. And if they should cease, no one could hold them [in place] after Him. Indeed, He is ever Forbearing and Forgiving."
Plain Understanding
It is God alone who actively grips the heavens and the earth to prevent them from collapsing out of their orbits. If God were to let go and they began to fall, absolutely no one in the universe could stop them. Yet, despite human rebellion on this earth, He is incredibly Forbearing and Forgiving.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Gravity and orbital mechanics are not independent, autonomous forces; they are the physical manifestation of "Imsak" (the Divine Grasp). The universe does not run on autopilot. God is actively sustaining the terrifying physics of the cosmos moment by moment. That the Earth does not violently shake off the sinners walking upon it is solely due to His "Hilm" (majestic patience and forbearance).

Verse 42
وَأَقْسَمُوا۟ بِٱللَّهِ جَهْدَ أَيْمَٰنِهِمْ لَئِن جَآءَهُمْ نَذِيرٌۭ لَّيَكُونُنَّ أَهْدَىٰ مِنْ إِحْدَى ٱلْأُمَمِ ۖ فَلَمَّا جَآءَهُمْ نَذِيرٌۭ مَّا زَادَهُمْ إِلَّا نُفُورًا
"And they swore by Allah their strongest oaths that if a warner came to them, they would be more guided than [any] one of the [previous] nations. But when a warner came to them, it did not increase them except in aversion."
Plain Understanding
Before Muhammad arrived, the Meccans used to swear their most solemn oaths that if God ever sent them a Prophet like He sent to the Jews or Christians, they would be the most guided, righteous nation on earth. But when the Prophet finally arrived, his presence only increased their hatred and rejection.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Arabs of the Hijaz looked down upon the religious disputes of the People of the Book (Jews and Christians), arrogantly boasting that Arab honor would guarantee perfect obedience if a Messenger appeared among them. Yet, when Muhammad ﷺ emerged from their own elite lineage, tribal politics and economic greed overrode their boasting. The ego loves the *idea* of righteousness, but violently rebels against the *reality* of submitting to a law that restricts its desires.

Verse 43
ٱسْتِكْبَارًۭا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَمَكْرَ ٱلسَّيِّئِ ۚ وَلَا يَحِيقُ ٱلْمَكْرُ ٱلسَّيِّئُ إِلَّا بِأَهْلِهِۦ ۚ فَهَلْ يَنظُرُونَ إِلَّا سُنَّتَ ٱلْأَوَّلِينَ ۚ فَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ ٱللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًۭا ۖ وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ ٱللَّهِ تَحْوِيلًا
"[Due to] arrogance in the land and plotting of evil; but the evil plot does not encompass except its own people. Then do they await except the way of the former peoples? But you will never find in the way of Allah any change, and you will never find in the way of Allah any alteration."
Plain Understanding
Their rejection was entirely due to their political arrogance and their dark conspiracies to stop the truth. But evil plots always end up suffocating the plotters themselves! Are they expecting a different outcome than the destruction that hit ancient civilizations? The physical and spiritual laws of God never change, and they are never altered.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Wa la yaheequ al-makru as-sayyi'u illa bi-ahlih" (The evil plot does not encompass except its own people). This is an absolute spiritual law. Whenever a person digs a pit of deception for another, the divine physics of the universe dictates that the architect of the pit will inevitably fall into it. The "Sunnah" (Way/Law) of Allah regarding the destruction of arrogant oppressors is as immutable as gravity.

Verse 44
أَوَلَمْ يَسِيرُوا۟ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ فَيَنظُرُوا۟ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَٰقِبَةُ ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِهِمْ وَكَانُوٓا۟ أَشَدَّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةًۭ ۚ وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيُعْجِزَهُۥ مِن شَىْءٍۢ فِى ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَلَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۚ إِنَّهُۥ كَانَ عَلِيمًا قَدِيرًۭا
"Have they not traveled through the earth and observed how was the end of those before them, though they were greater than them in power? But Allah is not to be caused failure by anything in the heavens or on the earth. Indeed, He is ever Knowing and Competent."
Plain Understanding
Have these Meccans not traveled the world and seen the ruined castles of civilizations that were vastly stronger, richer, and more heavily armed than they are? Nothing in the entire universe can escape God or overpower Him, for His Knowledge and Power are absolute.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The Qur'an frequently demands archaeological reflection. The ruins of Petra, 'Aad, and Pharaoh are not merely historical sites; they are theological crime scenes. If the empires that carved mountains were reduced to dust by the decree of Heaven, the ego of the modern merchant or politician is mathematically insignificant before the Competence ("Qadeer") of the Creator.

Verse 45
وَلَوْ يُؤَاخِذُ ٱللَّهُ ٱلنَّاسَ بِمَا كَسَبُوا۟ مَا تَرَكَ عَلَىٰ ظَهْرِهَا مِن دَآبَّةٍۢ وَلَٰكِن يُؤَخِّرُهُمْ إِلَىٰٓ أَجَلٍۢ مُّسَمًّۭى ۖ فَإِذَا جَآءَ أَجَلُهُمْ فَإِنَّ ٱللَّهَ كَانَ بِعِبَادِهِۦ بَصِيرًۢا
"And if Allah were to impose blame on the people for what they have earned, He would not leave upon the earth any creature. But He defers them for a specified term. And when their time comes, then indeed Allah has ever been, of His servants, Seeing."
Plain Understanding
The Surah closes with a terrifying, yet merciful reality: If God were to punish humanity instantly for every sin and crime they committed, His wrath would be so great that He would wipe out every single living creature on the face of the earth. Instead, out of His mercy, He delays the punishment until their specific time of death. But when that time arrives, God sees everything they did.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse explains the profound "Hilm" (Patience) of the Almighty. The Meccans mocked the Prophet: "If you are telling the truth, why hasn't God destroyed us yet?" God clarifies that the delay of justice is a structural mechanism of the Dunya, designed to grant the sinner the opportunity for "Tawbah" (Repentance). If justice were instantaneous, human existence would have been terminated centuries ago. The silence of the sky is not absence; it is patience.

"And We have forcefully enjoined upon the human being supreme excellence [Ihsan] toward his parents. His mother carried him with immense hardship and brought him forth with immense hardship, and his carrying and weaning is thirty full months. Until, when he reaches full maturity and exactly reaches forty years, he passionately prays, 'My Lord! Profoundly inspire me to be continuously grateful for Your majestic favor which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents, and to actively do righteous deeds that You perfectly approve of, and make my offspring absolutely righteous for me! Indeed, I have turned back to You in repentance, and indeed, I am entirely of the Muslims [those who completely submit].'"
Plain Understanding
God issues a massive command regarding parents, explicitly highlighting the mother's biological trauma. She endured the agonizing weakness of pregnancy, the sheer terror of labor, and the draining exhaustion of nursing. Then, God defines the "peak" of human maturity at age 40. At this age, a righteous person doesn't have a mid-life crisis; they have a spiritual awakening! They suddenly realize the massive sacrifices their parents made, and they launch into a beautiful prayer asking God to help them be grateful, and to protect their own children, creating an unbroken, multi-generational chain of faith.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Wa hamluhu wa fisaluhu thalathoona shahran" (And his carrying and weaning is thirty months). The early Islamic scholars (like Ibn Abbas) used brilliant deductive logic with this verse. Since Surah Luqman (31:14) states that a mother nurses a child for a maximum of two years (24 months), and this verse says the total time of pregnancy *plus* nursing is 30 months, they mathematically deduced that the absolute minimum viable term for a human pregnancy to produce a surviving child is exactly 6 months!

When you realize the test is only 60 minutes long, having patience ("Sabr") becomes infinitely easier.

Surah An-Nazi'at

Those Who Drag Forth • Makkah • 46 Verses
An intensely vivid Makkan Surah that opens with terrifying oaths sworn by the angels of death. It details the violent extraction of the disbeliever's soul, contrasting it with the gentle removal of the believer's, before exposing the arrogance of the Quraysh regarding the resurrection.
Verse 1
وَٱلنَّـٰزِعَـٰتِ غَرْقًۭا
"By those [angels] who extract with violence"
Plain Understanding
God opens the Surah with a highly descriptive oath sworn by a specific group of Angels of Death. These angels are assigned to the disbelievers and tyrants. "Gharqan" indicates a process of pulling from the absolute depths; they extract the soul violently, tearing it from the deepest recesses of the human body.
Historical Context
The Quraysh arrogantly denied the unseen realities of death and resurrection, treating death simply as a biological end to a material life. To shatter this illusion, the Prophet (ﷺ) described the unseen reality of this verse in authentic Hadith: when the angel arrives for the wicked person, their soul scatters and hides throughout the veins of the body in pure terror. The angel then tears it out forcefully, like a multi-pronged iron skewer being violently pulled through wet wool.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi emphasizes the profound spiritual transition. Death is not a peaceful sleep for the oppressor; it is a violent, terrifying arrest. Because the arrogant soul spent its entire life violently clinging to the Dunya and refusing to submit to the Creator, the angels must violently rip it away from the physical world it worshipped.

Verse 2
وَٱلنَّـٰشِطَـٰتِ نَشْطًۭا
"And [by] those who remove with ease"
Plain Understanding
The second oath contrasts the first. God swears by the Angels of Death assigned to the righteous believers. "Nashtan" refers to untying a knot effortlessly or drawing something out smoothly. These angels approach the believer with absolute gentleness, slipping the soul out of the body with ease and honor.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali explains the psychological reason for this ease. The soul of the sincere believer is already eager to meet its Lord. Because the believer spent their life actively detaching their heart from the illusions of the Dunya, the angel does not need to use force. The soul slips out as smoothly as a drop of water pouring gracefully from the spout of a jug.

The Creator's use of a complex administrative system teaches humans to respect order, hierarchy, and delegation within their own societal and governmental structures.

Plain Understanding
They emphasize what they perceive as the biological impossibility of the claim. They argue that once their skeletal structure has completely decayed, hollowed out, and crumbled into dust ("Nakhirah"), physically reconstructing them defies all known science and logic.
Historical Context
The hostility to resurrection was deeply physical. Prominent Makkan idolaters, such as Ubayy ibn Khalaf, would literally go to graveyards, pick up ancient, brittle, and decaying bones, crush them into dust with their hands, blow the dust into the wind toward the Prophet ﷺ, and mockingly demand: "Who will give life to these bones after they have rotted away?"

Plain Understanding
God abruptly transitions the subject by addressing the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ directly with a rhetorical question. He introduces the historical narrative of Prophet Moses (Musa) as a profound case study in dealing with extreme human arrogance and the absolute certainty of divine judgment.

By introducing the story of Moses confronting Pharaoh—the most powerful dictator in human history—God provides immediate psychological comfort to the Prophet ﷺ, proving that worldly power is an illusion before the decree of the Creator.

"'Go to Pharaoh. Indeed, he has transgressed.'"
Plain Understanding
The command is brief, direct, and overwhelmingly dangerous. Moses is ordered to confront the supreme ruler of Egypt. Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi draws a parallel between the word "Tagha" used here for Pharaoh and the word "Taghiyah" (the overpowering blast) used to destroy Thamud in Surah Al-Haqqah. When human authority crosses its limits and plays God, it triggers a divine response that inevitably restores the balance of justice.

God now reveals the exact opposite formula: the blueprint for eternal success. It consists of an internal reality and an external action. Purification of the Self (Al-Gilani)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani describes this verse as the absolute core of spiritual warfare (Jihad al-Nafs). The ego is like a wild, ravenous dog that constantly barks for the forbidden. Without that fear, the ego runs wild and destroys the soul.

The verse praises the one who *prevents* the soul from acting upon those desires. The victory is not the absence of temptation; the victory is the active, painful discipline of saying "no" to the self for the sake of God.

Verse 43
فِيمَ أَنتَ مِن ذِكْرَىٰهَا
"In what [position] are you that you should mention it?"
Plain Understanding
God intervenes to defend His Messenger. The Prophet is instructed that it is not his responsibility, nor is it within his capacity, to know or declare the exact timing of the end of the world. The exact knowledge of the unseen is intentionally withheld even from the greatest of Prophets.
Historical Context
This reality was cemented in the famous "Hadith of Jibril." When the Archangel Gabriel came in the form of a man and explicitly asked the Prophet ﷺ, "Tell me about the Hour," the Prophet replied, "The one being asked knows no more about it than the one asking." Both the greatest Angel and the greatest Prophet submitted completely to their lack of knowledge regarding this specific secret of God.

Verse 45
إِنَّمَآ أَنتَ مُنذِرُ مَن يَخْشَىٰهَا
"You are only a warner for those who fear it."
Plain Understanding
God refocuses the Prophet ﷺ on his true mission. He is not a fortune-teller predicting dates; he is a profound warner. However, God highlights a psychological truth: the warnings of the Prophet only benefit and penetrate the hearts of those who already possess "Khashyah" (a sincere, internal fear and awe of the accountability). For the arrogant, the warning is useless.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah teaches: "Rain does not bring dead stone to life; it only brings forth plants from fertile soil." The Prophet's warning is the pure rain of revelation, but it only produces the fruit of guidance in a heart that has cultivated the fertile soil of humility and fear.

Verse 46 ۩
كَأَنَّهُمْ يَوْمَ يَرَوْنَهَا لَمْ يَلْبَثُوٓا۟ إِلَّا عَشِيَّةً أَوْ ضُحَىٰهَا
"It will be, on the Day they see it, as though they had not remained [in the world] except for an afternoon or a morning thereof."
Plain Understanding
The Surah concludes with a mind-bending revelation regarding the relativity of time. On the Day of Judgment, when the arrogant are finally exposed to the infinite reality of eternity, their perception of their worldly life will collapse. Decades of earthly wealth, power, and arrogant rebellion will feel as brief and fleeting as a single afternoon or a quick morning. The ultimate tragedy is trading an infinite eternity for a single morning of illusion.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali eloquently summarizes the human condition using this verse. The Dunya is a dream, and death is the moment you wake up. When you wake up from a dream, you realize that years of events in the dream actually took only a few seconds of real time. When the soul wakes up to the reality of the Akhirah, the seventy years of the Dunya will feel like a passing shadow. Do not sell your waking reality to chase a phantom in a dream.

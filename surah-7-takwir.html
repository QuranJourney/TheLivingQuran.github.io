Surah At-Takwir

The Overthrowing • Makkah • 29 Verses
The Unveiling of Reality. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever wishes to look at the Day of Resurrection as if he were seeing it with his own eyes, let him read Surah At-Takwir." It details the collapse of the physical universe, leading to the ultimate exposure of the soul.
Verse 1
إِذَا ٱلشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ
"When the sun is wrapped up [in darkness]"
Plain Understanding
When the sun, the primary source of light and life for the earth, loses its light and is folded away.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Kuwwirat" means to roll up like a turban. The sun, which many ancient cultures worshipped as a god, will be unceremoniously rolled up and thrown away, proving its created, subjugated nature.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The physical sun represents the intellect and worldly clarity. On that Day, human logic and the "light" of the Dunya will completely black out. You can only navigate that Day with the light of faith (Nur) built in the heart.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not rely on what perishes, for you will perish with it." The greatest, most reliable cosmic body will fail. Rely only on the Eternal (Al-Baqi).

Verse 2
وَإِذَا ٱلنُّجُومُ ٱنكَدَرَتْ
"And when the stars fall, dispersing,"
Plain Understanding
When the stars lose their brilliance and scatter from their orbits.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Arabs used stars for navigation in the desert. "Inkaderat" means they will become murky and fall like dry leaves. The ultimate navigational tools will fail.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Stars are the 'guides' of this world (wealth, leaders, false idols). When the Truth is fully manifest, all false guides will fall away and lose their luster.

Verse 3
وَإِذَا ٱلْجِبَالُ سُيِّرَتْ
"And when the mountains are removed,"
Plain Understanding
When the massive, solid mountains are uprooted and blown away like dust.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Mountains represent stability and arrogance in the human psyche. The most stubborn, deeply rooted ego will be obliterated in the blink of an eye before the Majesty of God.

Verse 4
وَإِذَا ٱلْعِشَارُ عُطِّلَتْ
"And when full-term she-camels are neglected,"
Plain Understanding
When the most valuable possessions are completely abandoned by their owners.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Ishar" refers to a ten-month pregnant camel—the absolute most prized, valuable wealth to an ancient Arab. It represented guaranteed future wealth. The terror of the Day will cause people to abandon their most precious assets without a second thought.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He gave you the ephemeral world so you would not be too attached to it." The terror of the Real will detach you from the illusion you loved, forcefully.

Lions and gazelles will huddle together, ignoring predator/prey dynamics because the overriding terror of the apocalypse unites them in fear of the Creator.

Verse 6

"And when the seas are filled with flame,"
Plain Understanding
When the oceans boil over and erupt into blazing fire.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Water, the element that extinguishes fire, becomes fire. The laws of physics reverse. Do not rely on "causes and effects" (Asbab); rely on the Causer of causes (Musabbib al-Asbab), who can change their nature at will.

Verse 7
وَإِذَا ٱلنُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ
"And when the souls are paired,"
Plain Understanding
When every soul is joined with its bodily form, or joined with others of its exact kind (the righteous with the righteous, the wicked with the wicked).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Zuwwijat" means sorting and pairing. The Jews with the Jews, the idolaters with idolaters, the believers with believers. You will be resurrected and grouped with whoever you loved and acted like in the Dunya.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not keep company with one whose state does not inspire you." Your companions in this world determine your pairing in the next.

Verse 8
وَإِذَا ٱلْمَوْءُۥدَةُ سُئِلَتْ
"And when the girl [who was] buried alive is asked"
Plain Understanding
When the infant girl, murdered by her parents, is brought forward and asked.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A horrifying pre-Islamic Arab custom where fathers would bury infant daughters alive out of "shame" or fear of poverty. Islam eradicated this. On Judgment Day, the victim is asked, not the murderer, to heighten the terror and shame of the killer.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Metaphorically: When the innocent "Fitrah" (pure nature) or the gentle, feminine qualities of mercy and compassion that you "buried alive" inside yourself due to societal pressure are resurrected and question your heart.

The ultimate display of Divine Justice. The weakest, most silenced victim in the world is given the floor in the Divine Court. Absolute justice leaves no hidden crime unanswered.

Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
In this world, Allah provides Satr (concealment) for our sins. On that Day, the veil is ripped away. The terror of public exposure (Fadiha) is often more painful to the ego than physical fire. Purify your secret life now.

Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Kushitat" is the word used for flaying an animal. The sky, our protective roof, will be violently removed, exposing humanity directly to the raw realities of the Unseen (the Throne, the Angels, Heaven and Hell).

Verse 14
عَلِمَتْ نَفْسٌۭ مَّآ أَحْضَرَتْ
"A soul will [then] know what it has brought [with it]."
Plain Understanding
This is the conclusion to all 12 "When" statements. When all this happens, every single soul will know exactly what deeds it brought forward.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
All illusions are shattered. In the Dunya, you can deceive yourself into thinking your bad deeds are good. On that Day, absolute clarity strikes. You see your true spiritual bank account.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The ultimate awakening. The goal of Sufism is to achieve this realization *before* death. "Die before you die." Audit your soul now, so you aren't horrified by what you brought later.

"So I swear by the retreating stars -"
Plain Understanding
Allah begins a series of oaths, swearing by the planets/stars that recede or hide.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Khunnas" refers to planets (like Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn) that appear to move backward in retrograde, or stars that fade during the daylight.

Verse 16

"Those that run [their courses] and disappear -"
Plain Understanding
Swearing by the celestial bodies that sweep across the sky and then sweep into their hiding places.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Everything in creation has a phase of appearance and disappearance. The soul goes through states of expansion (Basta) and contraction (Qabd). Do not despair when the spiritual light seems to "disappear"; it is just following its orbit.

"As'as" is a unique word that can mean both the coming of the night's darkness and the retreating of the night at dawn. It signifies transition.

Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Quraish claimed the Prophet was possessed by devils. Allah swears by the cosmos that the messenger who delivered this to Muhammad ﷺ is the highest, most noble Angel.

Allah uses the word "Sahibukum" (your companion). He is reminding the Quraish: "You have known him for 40 years. He was your closest companion, the Trustworthy (Al-Amin). How can you suddenly call him mad?"

"And he has already seen Gabriel in the clear horizon."
Plain Understanding
The Prophet ﷺ literally saw the Angel Jibreel in his true, majestic form across the sky.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This refers to the event when the Prophet ﷺ saw Jibreel filling the entire horizon with 600 wings. It validates that his revelation is based on a massive, objective physical encounter, not a hallucination.

"And Muhammad is not a withholder of [knowledge of] the unseen."
Plain Understanding
The Prophet is not stingy with the knowledge of heaven; he shares everything God gives him freely.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Soothsayers charged money for their "secrets." The Prophet ﷺ gives the ultimate secrets of the Unseen to everyone, poor or rich, for absolutely free.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
True spiritual teachers do not hoard light. If someone charges you for spiritual access, they are a merchant, not a guide.

Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
This is the question every seeker must ask their ego daily. When you sin, when you chase the world: "Fa ayna tadhhabun? Where do you think you are going?" Every path other than Allah leads to a dead end.

Plain Understanding

Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Istiqama (steadfastness on the straight path) requires volition. You cannot float into Jannah. You must "will" to stand upright against the gravity of your desires.

"And you do not will except that Allah wills - Lord of the worlds."
Plain Understanding
Your free will is real, but it operates entirely within the overarching Will and Permission of the Lord of the universes.

"Let not your obedience make you joyous because it comes from you; rather, be joyous because it is a gift from Him to you." You chose the right path, but you could only do so because the Lord of the Worlds enabled you. This verse perfectly balances free will with absolute humility.

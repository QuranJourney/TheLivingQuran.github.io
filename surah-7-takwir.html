Surah At-Takwir

The Overthrowing • Makkah • 29 Verses
The Unveiling of Reality. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever wishes to look at the Day of Resurrection as if he were seeing it with his own eyes, let him read Surah At-Takwir." It details the collapse of the physical universe, leading to the ultimate exposure of the soul.
Verse 1
إِذَا ٱلشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ
"When the sun is put out,"
Plain Understanding
The brilliant, life-sustaining sun will be gently folded up and its light extinguished, marking the peaceful end of the temporary physical world.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Whatever is not God is bound to vanish." The sun is a magnificent servant, but it is created and temporary. Do not attach your tired heart to fading earthly light when you can attach it to the eternal Light-Giver.
Knowledge & SignsIllusion of Dunya
Verse 2
وَإِذَا ٱلنُّجُومُ ٱنكَدَرَتْ
"and when the stars fall down,"
Plain Understanding
The beautiful, scattering stars will lose their brilliance and fall, gently dismantling the physical heavens.
Historical Context (Al-Tabari)
In 7th-century Arabia, the desert dwellers relied entirely on the stars for navigation across the vast, dark sands. Witnessing their fall signifies the end of all worldly navigation, leaving the soul to rely exclusively on divine guidance.
Knowledge & SignsGuidance
Verse 3
وَإِذَا ٱلْجِبَالُ سُيِّرَتْ
"and when the mountains are blown away,"
Plain Understanding
The massive, seemingly unshakeable mountains will be scattered like weightless dust, proving that nothing physical possesses true permanence.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
We often view our worldly anxieties and traumas as immovable mountains. This verse comforts the anxious heart: the Creator who can blow away physical mountains can effortlessly remove the heavy burdens weighing on your soul. Surrender them to Him.

Verse 4

"and when pregnant camels are left untended,"
Plain Understanding
The most valuable, cherished material possessions of this world will be completely abandoned and forgotten by their owners.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
An 'ishaar (a ten-month pregnant camel) was the absolute pinnacle of wealth and pride for a Makkan Arab. It was fiercely guarded and never left untended. This verse shatters the illusion of materialism, showing that a day is coming when our most prized earthly portfolios will mean nothing to us.

Verse 8

"and when baby girls, buried alive, are asked"
Plain Understanding
The Supreme Court of the Creator will convene, giving a powerful, loving voice to the most vulnerable, voiceless victims of earthly cruelty.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pre-Islamic Quraysh practiced 'Wa'd'—the horrific custom of burying infant daughters alive out of severe poverty or misplaced tribal shame. The Prophet ﷺ would weep openly at the mention of this cruelty. This verse establishes that God’s justice is actively watching over the broken and oppressed.

Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
Notice the profound, majestic mercy of the Creator. He does not address the murderer first. He gently, tenderly addresses the victim—the innocent little girl—elevating her status and validating her pain before the entire cosmos. If you are oppressed, know that the King will speak to you before He speaks to your oppressor.

Verse 22

"And your fellow man is not insane."
Plain Understanding
A beautiful, loving defense of the Prophet ﷺ is declared, completely clearing him of the malicious slander meant to break his spirit.
Historical Context (Ibn Hisham)
The Makkan elites launched a vicious psychological campaign against the Prophet ﷺ, publicly calling him "Majnun" (a madman). Notice how Allah uses the tender, intimate word "Sahibukum" (your lifelong companion/friend). He gently reminds them: *You have known his flawless, beautiful character for forty years; you know in your hearts he is not insane.*
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Empty your heart of the creation so it can be filled with the Creator." The world often views the lovers of God as "insane" because their hearts are completely detached from materialism and status. What the blind world calls madness, the Heavens recognize as supreme spiritual awakening.

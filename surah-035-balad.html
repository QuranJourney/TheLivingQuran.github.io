Surah Al-Balad

The City • Makkah • 20 Verses
The Anatomy of the Steep Climb. Revealed during the agonizing early years in Makkah when the Prophet ﷺ was facing brutal persecution in his own hometown. This Surah tenderly acknowledges his pain, confirms the inherent struggle of human existence, and then beautifully redefines true power: it is not found in arrogant displays of wealth, but in the exhausting, uphill climb of freeing the enslaved and feeding the starving.
Verse 1
لَآ أُقْسِمُ بِهَـٰذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ
"I do swear by this city 'of Mecca'- "
Plain Understanding
The Creator opens with a profound, majestic oath, swearing by the sacred sanctuary of Makkah itself.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Makkah was universally recognized as a sanctuary of absolute peace. For centuries, the Arab tribes honored its sanctity so deeply that a man could walk past the murderer of his own father inside the city limits and refuse to draw his sword. Allah swears by this deeply respected haven of peace to set the stage for a heartbreaking contrast.
Knowledge & SignsProphetic History
Verse 2
وَأَنتَ حِلٌّۢ بِهَـٰذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ
"even though you 'O Prophet' are subject to abuse in this city- "
Plain Understanding
A tender, heartbreaking acknowledgment from the Lord: the very city that provides safety to criminals has been made a place of violent persecution and abuse for you.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
The word 'Hillun' implies that the Quraysh made it "lawful" for themselves to abuse the Prophet ﷺ. They violently broke their own ancient, sacred codes of honor just to inflict pain upon him, turning his beloved hometown into a prison of persecution.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"When Allah loves a servant, He detaches them from the comfort of the creation so they find their comfort only in Him." The pain of being rejected by your own people is a severe test, meant to empty the heart of worldly attachments so it can be entirely filled with the Divine presence.
ComfortProphetic HistoryHardship
Verse 3
وَوَالِدٍۢ وَمَا وَلَدَ
"and by every parent and 'their' child!"
Plain Understanding
The oath expands to include the profound, universal miracle of human life, swearing by the deep bond between a parent and their offspring.
Historical Context (Al-Tabari)
Classical scholars note this specifically refers to Prophet Adam (AS) and all of humanity that sprang from him. Allah links the sacredness of the physical city (Makkah) to the profound sacredness of human life and lineage, both of which were being violated by the elite.

Verse 4
لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ فِى كَبَدٍ
"Indeed, We have created humankind in 'constant' struggle."
Plain Understanding
The fundamental reality of earthly existence is established: human life is an unending series of physical exhaustion, emotional pain, and spiritual tests.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Kabad" means severe, friction-filled struggle. You struggled in the darkness of the womb, you struggle to earn a living, you struggle against illness, and you will struggle in the throes of death. The ego's greatest illusion is the belief that it can achieve a frictionless, perfectly comfortable life in the Dunya. Embrace the struggle; it is the anvil upon which your soul is forged.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Do not be surprised by the occurrence of sorrows as long as you are in this world, for it only manifests what is its inherent nature." The Dunya was never designed to be a place of rest. True, unbroken peace is reserved exclusively for the Hereafter. To struggle is simply to be human.
Human NatureHardshipComfort
Verse 5
أَيَحْسَبُ أَن لَّن يَقْدِرَ عَلَيْهِ أَحَدٌۭ
"Do they think that no one has power over them,"
Plain Understanding
A direct challenge to the arrogant tyrant who, blinded by his temporary worldly strength, behaves as if he is totally invincible and untouchable.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse refers specifically to the excessively wealthy and physically massive men of Quraysh, like Abu al-Ashaddayn, who would stand on a piece of leather and challenge ten men to pull it from under his feet without him moving. He genuinely believed his physical might made him immune to divine justice.
ArroganceDivine Justice
Verse 6
يَقُولُ أَهْلَكْتُ مَالًۭا لُّبَدًا
"boasting, 'I have wasted enormous wealth!'?"
Plain Understanding
They proudly brag about intentionally squandering massive amounts of money, using their extreme extravagance purely as a status symbol to intimidate others.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The elite would spend huge amounts of money funding wars against the Prophet ﷺ or throwing massive, wasteful feasts just to build their own worldly reputations. Notice the word "Ahlaktu" (I destroyed/wasted). Deep down, the soul knows that spending money strictly for ego is not an investment; it is the literal destruction of wealth.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Wasting wealth to secure the applause and fear of people is the ultimate spiritual bankruptcy. You are permanently destroying the beautiful blessings Allah gave you in exchange for the temporary, meaningless ashes of human validation.

Verse 7
أَيَحْسَبُ أَن لَّمْ يَرَهُۥٓ أَحَدٌ
"Do they think that no one sees them?"
Plain Understanding
A piercing, terrifying reminder: does the boastful human truly believe the Creator does not witness the secret arrogance in his heart and the complete waste of His blessings?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"The One who watches you in the darkness of your private sins is the same One who watches the hidden motives behind your public boasts." Absolute awareness belongs only to Him.
AccountabilityThe UnseenTruth
Verse 8
أَلَمْ نَجْعَل لَّهُۥ عَيْنَيْنِ
"Have We not given them two eyes,"
Plain Understanding
Allah gently lists His profound, intimate physical blessings, beginning with the miraculous gift of sight that allows humans to navigate the world.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
He gave you two eyes so you could witness His magnificent signs and look upon His creation with deep mercy. Do not use the very gifts He gave you to look down upon the weak with arrogant contempt.
Divine Wisdom
Allah begins listing the biological miracles He installed in the human body for free, contrasting His supreme generosity with the human's arrogant boasting about spending a little money (v. 6).
GratitudeKnowledge & Signs
Verse 9
وَلِسَانًۭا وَشَفَتَيْنِ
"a tongue, and two lips;"
Plain Understanding
And He provided the profound ability to speak, granting the tongue and lips to express the deepest thoughts of the human heart.
Historical Context (Al-Tabari)
These specific organs are beautifully highlighted because they are the exact instruments the elite were abusing. They used their eyes to sneer at the oppressed, and their tongues and lips to boast of their squandered wealth and to slander the Prophet ﷺ.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
You use the tongue Allah manufactured to articulate your denial of Allah. The very instrument of your rebellion is a loan from the King. Lips are also the gates of the mouth; Allah gave you two doors to keep your tongue locked away, yet you constantly leave them open to boast.
AccountabilityHuman Nature
Verse 10
وَهَدَيْنَـٰهُ ٱلنَّجْدَيْنِ
"and shown them the two ways 'of right and wrong'?"
Plain Understanding
Beyond physical gifts, the Creator provided the ultimate gift of spiritual clarity, instilling the soul with free will and clearly illuminating the stark paths of virtue and vice.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Jilani)
"Allah gave you the eyes to see the path, the intellect to understand the map, and the tongue to ask for help—but you must be the one to physically command your feet to walk the steep road of purification."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Najdayn" refers to two elevated, highly visible roads. Allah did not hide the truth in obscurity. The path of salvation and the path of destruction are both brightly lit. The failure of the human is not a lack of vision (he has two eyes); it is a lack of willpower.
GuidanceTruthAccountability
Verse 11
فَلَا ٱقْتَحَمَ ٱلْعَقَبَةَ
"If only they had attempted the challenging path 'of goodness instead'!"
Plain Understanding
Instead of wasting their money on empty boasting, they should have bravely and aggressively rushed to tackle the steep, difficult climb of true virtue.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The word 'Al-'Aqabah' refers to a treacherous, steep mountain pass that exhausts the traveler. Allah uses this precise metaphor because overcoming the selfish desires of the ego to do acts of genuine, unglamorous good feels exactly like a lung-burning, uphill climb.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Iqtahama" is a violent, aggressive word. It means to throw yourself into a brutal, terrifying obstacle. True religion is not a casual, comfortable hobby. Overcoming the greed of the ego (the Aqabah) requires a violent, breathless, internal struggle against your own selfishness.
Spiritual PracticeHardshipSincerity
Verse 12
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلْعَقَبَةُ
"And what will make you realize what 'attempting' the challenging path is?"
Plain Understanding
A majestic, rhetorical question designed to elevate the magnitude and profound spiritual beauty of what this challenging climb actually entails.
Divine Wisdom
The pagan elite thought "success" was funding tribal wars. Allah shatters their paradigm. The true mountain you must climb is not on a battlefield of swords; it is on the battlefield of sociology and economics.
TruthGuidance
Verse 13
فَكُّ رَقَبَةٍ
"It is to free a slave;"
Plain Understanding
The very first step on the steep path to Allah is fiercely breaking the physical chains of a subjugated, oppressed human being.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
In a society entirely built on the brutal exploitation of slave labor, Abu Bakr As-Siddiq (RA) embodied this verse perfectly. He aggressively purchased and freed vulnerable, tortured believers like Bilal ibn Rabah at exorbitant prices. While the elite wasted wealth to buy status (Verse 6), the believers spent their wealth to buy human freedom.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Freeing a physical slave from bondage is magnificent, but the steep climb is not complete until you also fiercely free your own soul from the slavery of your ego, your greed, and your desires.
CompassionEqualityCharity
Verse 14
أَوْ إِطْعَـٰمٌۭ فِى يَوْمٍۢ ذِى مَسْغَبَةٍۢ
"or to give food in times of famine"
Plain Understanding
The climb continues by selflessly parting with your own vital provisions to feed others during days of severe starvation and economic collapse.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"To give when your pockets are full is simple generosity; to give when you yourself are terrified of starving is the absolute pinnacle of certainty (Yaqeen) in the Provider."
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
It is easy to give away surplus food when your fridge is full. The "Steep Path" (Aqabah) is feeding someone else when the economy has collapsed (Masghabah) and your own Nafs is screaming in panic, "Save it for yourself!" True charity only begins when it hurts to give.
CharitySincerityCompassion
Verse 15
يَتِيمًۭا ذَا مَقْرَبَةٍ
"to an orphaned relative"
Plain Understanding
Specifically seeking out and feeding the fatherless child who also has a tie of kinship, beautifully fulfilling both the duty of charity and the sacred duty of family love.
Divine Wisdom
Islam radically prioritizes the structure of charity. It is a tragedy when people donate thousands to distant international charities for the ego boost, while ignoring their own orphaned nephews and nieces struggling in silence. Fix your immediate radius first.
CompassionSocial Ethics
Verse 16
أَوْ مِسْكِينًۭا ذَا مَتْرَبَةٍۢ
"or to a poor person in distress;"
Plain Understanding
Or desperately rushing to feed the utterly destitute person who is metaphorically, or literally, clinging to the dust out of sheer starvation and poverty.
Historical Context (Al-Tabari)
The Arabic phrase 'Dha matrabah' literally means 'a person covered in dirt.' This refers to a human being so utterly broken by poverty that they have no home, no bed, and sleep in the dust. Allah is teaching humanity that true spiritual elevation is not found in high-society gatherings, but in lifting the person sleeping in the dirt.
HumilityCompassionEquality
Verse 17
ثُمَّ كَانَ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَتَوَاصَوْا۟ بِٱلصَّبْرِ وَتَوَاصَوْا۟ بِٱلْمَرْحَمَةِ
"and- above all- to be one of those who have faith and urge each other to perseverance and urge each other to compassion."
Plain Understanding
These physical acts of charity must be crowned by a sincere internal faith, creating a community that constantly reminds one another to patiently endure hardships and to be infinitely merciful.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Patience without compassion is mere endurance, and compassion without patience is fleeting emotion." The spiritual peak is not just struggling uphill yourself; it is holding the hand of your brother and sister on the steep climb, ensuring they do not fall.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Action without faith is empty humanitarianism; faith without action is religious hypocrisy. You must have both. Furthermore, you cannot survive the "Steep Path" alone. "Tawasaw" means a mutual, continuous exchange of encouragement. You must build a community that injects patience into your spine and mercy into your heart when the climb exhausts you.
Spiritual PracticeCompassionHope
Verse 18
أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلْمَيْمَنَةِ
"These are the people of the right."
Plain Understanding
Those who successfully conquer the steep path of selfless love, faith, and endurance will be the honored, beautifully blessed companions of the right hand.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
They gave up the "comfort" of their wealth in the Dunya, so Allah grants them the ultimate, eternal comfort of Paradise. The right hand signifies success, honor, and receiving the book of deeds with joy.
HopeJannah
Verse 19
وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ بِـَٔايَـٰتِنَا هُمْ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلْمَشْـَٔمَةِ
"As for those who deny Our signs, they are the people of the left."
Plain Understanding
Conversely, those who arrogantly reject these beautiful truths, hoarding their wealth and stepping on the weak, will be the wretched companions of the left hand.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Mash'amah" is associated with the left side, which in ancient Arab culture symbolized disaster, bad omens, and utter ruin. They chose the "easy" path of greed, which inevitably terminates at the cliff of disaster.
WarningArrogance
Verse 20
عَلَيْهِمْ نَارٌۭ مُّؤْصَدَةٌۢ
"The Fire will be sealed over them."
Plain Understanding
Their terrifying final consequence is a blazing fire whose massive doors are permanently locked shut, trapping them in the misery they constructed.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Divine justice is perfectly poetic. Because they "sealed their wallets" (v.6) bolted their doors and sealed their hearts to the orphan, the slave (v.14-16), and the starving in the Dunya, the doors of the Fire are justly sealed over them in the Akhirah. The punishment is merely the mirror of the crime.

Surah Az-Zumar

The Troops • Makkah • 75 Verses
The Pure Religion. An intensely emotional and profound Surah that centers on absolute sincerity (Ikhlas) to God. It breaks down the false justifications of idolatry and highlights the supreme spiritual rank of those who patiently seek God in the depths of the night.
Verse 1
تَنزِيلُ ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ مِنَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلْعَزِيزِ ٱلْحَكِيمِ
"The revelation of this Book is from Allah, the Exalted in Might, the Wise."
Plain Understanding
The descent of this Quran into the world comes directly from God, who is absolutely Powerful and perfectly Wise in all His decrees.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Surah opens by establishing the ultimate authority of the text. It is not poetry or philosophy; it is a direct download from the Creator. Because He is "Aziz" (Mighty), no one can stop its truth, and because He is "Hakeem" (Wise), every law within it is perfectly balanced for human success.

Verse 2
إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَآ إِلَيْكَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبَ بِٱلْحَقِّ فَٱعْبُدِ ٱللَّهَ مُخْلِصًۭا لَّهُ ٱلدِّينَ
"Indeed, We have sent down to you the Book in truth. So worship Allah, making religion purely for Him."
Plain Understanding
We have revealed this Book to you, O Prophet, carrying the absolute truth. Therefore, dedicate your worship entirely to God, stripping it of any hidden motives.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Mukhlisan" (sincerity) is the beating heart of this entire Surah. Sincerity means that when you pray, give charity, or smile at someone, your internal gaze is fixed 100% on God's pleasure, completely uncontaminated by the desire for human applause or social status.

Verse 3
أَلَا لِلَّهِ ٱلدِّينُ ٱلْخَالِصُ ۚ وَٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّخَذُوا۟ مِن دُونِهِۦٓ أَوْلِيَآءَ مَا نَعْبُدُهُمْ إِلَّا لِيُقَرِّبُونَآ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ زُلْفَىٰٓ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَحْكُمُ بَيْنَهُمْ فِى مَا هُمْ فِيهِ يَخْتَلِفُونَ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِى مَنْ هُوَ كَـٰذِبٌۭ كَفَّارٌۭ
"Unquestionably, pure religion belongs entirely to Allah. And those who take protectors besides Him [say], 'We only worship them so that they may bring us closer to Allah in position.' Indeed, Allah will judge between them concerning that over which they differ. Indeed, Allah does not guide the one who is a liar and an extreme ingrate."
Plain Understanding
Absolute purity in worship belongs only to God. The pagans justify their idolatry by saying, "We don't actually think these statues are God; we just use them as middlemen to get closer to God." God rejects this lie. He does not guide those who deliberately invent lies to justify their ungratefulness.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
God is closer to you than your jugular vein (Surah Qaf, v. 16). The ultimate insult to His Mercy is assuming you need to bribe a "middleman" (a saint, a statue, or a priest) to gain His attention. To place an intermediary between yourself and the Most Merciful is to call Him distant, which is a lie against His nature.

Verse 4
لَّوْ أَرَادَ ٱللَّهُ أَن يَتَّخِذَ وَلَدًۭا لَّٱصْطَفَىٰ مِمَّا يَخْلُقُ مَا يَشَآءُ ۚ سُبْحَـٰنَهُۥ ۖ هُوَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلْوَٰحِدُ ٱلْقَهَّارُ
"If Allah had intended to take a son, He could have chosen from what He creates whatever He willed. Exalted is He; He is Allah, the One, the Prevailing."

Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse directly dismantles the Christian concept of Jesus as the literal Son, and the Meccan pagan concept that the angels were the "daughters of God." Having offspring implies a need for legacy, assistance, or companionship. The "Qahhar" (Supreme Conqueror) has absolutely zero needs.

Verse 5

Plain Understanding
He built the universe with precise purpose. He smoothly overlaps the night and the day like wrapping a turban, and controls the sun and moon until their designated end. He is immensely Powerful, yet constantly Forgiving.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Yukawwir" (wrapping/coiling) perfectly describes the spherical rotation of the earth, seamlessly blending light and dark. If God smoothly transitions the pitch-black night into the bright day on a cosmic scale, do you doubt His ability to transition your heart from the darkness of grief into the light of joy?

Verse 6

"He created you from one soul. Then He made from it its mate, and He produced for you from the grazing livestock eight mates. He creates you in the wombs of your mothers, creation after creation, within three darknesses. That is Allah, your Lord; to Him belongs dominion. There is no deity except Him, so how are you averted?"
Plain Understanding
He originated all of humanity from Adam, created Eve from him, and provided livestock for your survival. He builds your bodies inside your mothers' wombs in distinct stages, shielded by three layers of darkness. This Master Designer is your Lord. Since He alone holds all power, how can your minds be turned away from worshipping Him?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The "three darknesses" scientifically correspond to the abdominal wall, the uterine wall, and the amniotic sac enclosing the fetus. The Quran states biological facts centuries before microscopes, proving it is the word of the One who engineered the womb.

"If you disbelieve - indeed, Allah is Free of need of you. And He does not approve for His servants disbelief. And if you are grateful, He approves it for you; and no bearer of burdens will bear the burden of another. Then to your Lord is your return, and He will inform you about what you used to do. Indeed, He is Knowing of that within the breasts."
Plain Understanding
If the entire world rejects God, it doesn't harm Him—He doesn't need you. But He hates to see you destroy yourselves through disbelief. If you show gratitude, He is deeply pleased with you. No one can carry another's sins on Judgment Day. He knows exactly what you hide in your chest.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
God is "Ghani" (absolutely self-sufficient). Your prayers do not increase His majesty, and your sins do not decrease it. He commands you to pray solely because *you* need the light of the prayer to survive. His pleasure in your gratitude is the pleasure of a merciful guide watching a blind man successfully find his way home.

Verse 8

"And when adversity touches man, he calls upon his Lord, turning to Him [in desperation]; then when He bestows on him a favor from Himself, he forgets Him whom he called upon before, and he attributes to Allah equals to mislead [people] from His way. Plain Understanding
When humans face severe crisis, they instantly drop their pride and beg God for help. But the moment God rescues them, they completely forget their prayers, credit their success to other things (money, doctors, themselves), and go back to misleading others. God tells them: Enjoy your temporary illusion; the Fire awaits.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Crisis strips away the ego's illusions. In a crashing plane, there are no atheists. The tragedy of the human condition is that "peace" often causes spiritual amnesia. If suffering forces you to remember God, it is a massive blessing. If ease causes you to forget Him, it is a massive curse.

Verse 9
أَمَّنْ هُوَ قَـٰنِتٌ ءَانَآءَ ٱلَّيْلِ سَاجِدًۭا وَقَآئِمًۭا يَحْذَرُ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةَ وَيَرْجُوا۟ رَحْمَةَ رَبِّهِۦ ۗ قُلْ هَلْ يَسْتَوِى ٱلَّذِينَ يَعْلَمُونَ وَٱلَّذِينَ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ ۗ إِنَّمَا يَتَذَكَّرُ أُو۟لُوا۟ ٱلْأَلْبَـٰبِ

Plain Understanding
Compare the ungrateful man (in v. 8) to the beautiful believer who spends the quiet hours of the night bowing and standing before God—balancing his deep fear of the Judgment with profound hope in God's mercy. Ask yourself: Are the spiritually awake equal to the spiritually blind?
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This is the portrait of the spiritual elite. Notice the balance: "fearing the Hereafter" (Khawf) keeps the ego disciplined, while "hoping for His mercy" (Raja') keeps the heart from falling into despair. Like a bird, the soul requires both wings to fly toward the Divine.

Verse 10
قُلْ يَـٰعِبَادِ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمْ ۚ لِلَّذِينَ أَحْسَنُوا۟ فِى هَـٰذِهِ ٱلدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةٌۭ ۗ وَأَرْضُ ٱللَّهِ وَٰسِعَةٌ ۗ إِنَّمَا يُوَفَّى ٱلصَّـٰبِرُونَ أَجْرَهُم بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍۢ
"Say, 'O My servants who have believed, fear your Lord. For those who do good in this world is good, and the earth of Allah is spacious. Indeed, the patient will be given their reward without account.'"
Plain Understanding
God tells the believers: Remain conscious of your Lord. If you act with excellence here, you will be rewarded here. If your homeland forces you to sin, migrate—God's earth is vast. And know that those who endure with patience will be handed their ultimate reward without any limits or calculations.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This was a crucial revelation for the early Muslims being tortured in Makkah. "God's earth is spacious" was the divine green light permitting them to migrate (Hijrah) to Abyssinia to protect their faith. Furthermore, while most deeds have a specific multiplier (10x to 700x), the reward for Sabr (Patience) is "Bighayri Hisab"—an infinite, blank check from God.

وَنُفِخَ فِى ٱلصُّورِ فَصَعِقَ مَن فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَمَن فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ إِلَّا مَن شَآءَ ٱللَّهُ ۖ ثُمَّ نُفِخَ فِيهِ أُخْرَىٰ فَإِذَا هُمْ قِيَامٌۭ يَنظُرُونَ
"And the Horn will be blown, and whoever is in the heavens and whoever is on the earth will fall dead, except whom Allah strictly wills. Then it will be blown again, and instantly they will be standing, looking on."
Plain Understanding
The cosmic Trumpet will blast, and the sheer terror of the sound will instantly kill every living thing in the universe, except a few angels God chooses to spare. Then, a second blast will sound, and instantly all of humanity will rise from their graves, standing in shock, waiting for Judgment.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Your life is sandwiched between two breaths. The breath God blew into Adam to start human history, and the breath blown into the Horn to end it. Between these two breaths, your only job is to ensure your soul is awake before the Trumpet violently wakes it up.

Verse 69
وَأَشْرَقَتِ ٱلْأَرْضُ بِنُورِ رَبِّهَا وَوُضِعَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبُ وَجِا۟ىٓءَ بِٱلنَّبِيِّـۧنَ وَٱلشُّهَدَآءِ وَقُضِىَ بَيْنَهُم بِٱلْحَقِّ وَهُمْ لَا يُظْلَمُونَ
"And the earth will violently shine with the blinding light of its Lord, and the record [of deeds] will be placed, and the prophets and the witnesses will be brought forward, and it will be judged between them in absolute truth, and they will not be wronged."
Plain Understanding
The new earth of Judgment Day will be illuminated by the brilliant light of God Himself. The massive books detailing every human action will be laid open. The Prophets and angels will be brought to testify, and God will issue His flawlessly fair verdicts. No one will suffer even an atom of injustice.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
There is no sun or moon on Judgment Day. The only illumination is the terrifying, majestic "Noor" (Light) of the Creator as He descends to judge. In a court lit by the Divine Light, and witnessed by the Prophets themselves, hiding a sin or telling a lie becomes a metaphysical impossibility.

Verse 70
وَوُفِّيَتْ كُلُّ نَفْسٍۢ مَّا عَمِلَتْ وَهُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا يَفْعَلُونَ
"And every single soul will be paid entirely in full for what it did, and He is most knowing of whatever they do."
Plain Understanding
Every person will receive their exact, perfect paycheck for the deeds they committed. God does not need the books; He already knows intimately everything they ever did.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ultimate reassurance and the ultimate terror wrapped in one sentence. For the oppressor, being "paid in full" is a nightmare. For the oppressed, the exhausted mother, and the quiet believer crying in the night, being "paid in full" is the greatest promise ever made.

Verse 71
وَسِيقَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓا۟ إِلَىٰ جَهَنَّمَ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوهَا فُتِحَتْ أَبْوَٰبُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَآ أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ رُسُلٌۭ مِّنكُمْ يَتْلُونَ عَلَيْكُمْ ءَايَـٰتِ رَبِّكُمْ وَيُنذِرُونَكُمْ لِقَآءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَـٰذَا ۚ قَالُوا۟ بَلَىٰ وَلَـٰكِنْ حَقَّتْ كَلِمَةُ ٱلْعَذَابِ عَلَى ٱلْكَـٰفِرِينَ
"And those who disbelieved will be violently driven to Hell in massive groups, until, when they reach it, its gates are thrown open and its keepers will say, 'Did there not come to you messengers from yourselves, reciting to you the verses of your Lord and warning you of the meeting of this Day of yours?' They will say, 'Yes, but the word of punishment has justifiably come true upon the disbelievers.'"
Plain Understanding
The disbelievers will be herded like cattle toward Hell in terrified crowds. The moment they arrive, the gates will rip open, and the angelic guards will ask them in shock: "Didn't God send you human messengers to warn you about this exact moment?!" Defeated, they will admit, "Yes, they did. But we arrogantly ignored them, so we entirely deserve this punishment."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Notice the word "Zumar" (groups/troops) which gives the Surah its name. Sinners often justify their actions by saying "everyone else is doing it." God accommodates their desire for the crowd: they will be driven into Hell in the exact same blind, massive groups they used to follow in the Dunya.

Verse 72
قِيلَ ٱدْخُلُوٓا۟ أَبْوَٰبَ جَهَنَّمَ خَـٰلِدِينَ فِيهَا ۖ فَبِئْسَ مَثْوَى ٱلْمُتَكَبِّرِينَ
"It will be commanded, 'Enter the terrifying gates of Hell to abide eternally therein, and wretched is the residence of the arrogant.'"
Plain Understanding
The final, crushing verdict will be delivered: "Walk through the gates. You will remain in this fire forever. What a horrific, miserable final home for those whose egos were too big to bow to God."
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
God specifically identifies them as "Al-Mutakabbireen" (the arrogant). It wasn't just that they committed sins; it was that they believed they were too important, too smart, or too wealthy to submit. Hell is essentially a hospital designed to permanently burn away the disease of arrogance.

Verse 73
وَسِيقَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّقَوْا۟ رَبَّهُمْ إِلَى ٱلْجَنَّةِ زُمَرًا ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا جَآءُوهَا وَفُتِحَتْ أَبْوَٰبُهَا وَقَالَ لَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا سَلَـٰمٌ عَلَيْكُمْ طِبْتُمْ فَٱدْخُلُوهَا خَـٰلِدِينَ
"But those who deeply feared their Lord will be gently escorted to Paradise in magnificent groups, until, when they reach it while its massive gates have been wide open, its keepers will lovingly say, 'Peace be upon you; you have become completely pure; so enter it to abide eternally.'"
Plain Understanding
In joyous contrast, the believers will be escorted like VIPs to Heaven in beautiful groups. They will find the gates already wide open waiting for them. The angels guarding the gates will smile and welcome them: "Absolute peace is yours! You are spiritually pure now; step inside and enjoy this forever!"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Look at the incredibly subtle grammatical difference. For Hell (v. 71), it says "the gates opened" (implying they were locked like a prison, and open suddenly as a terrifying shock). For Heaven, an extra "wa" (and) is used: "and its gates had *already* been opened." Jannah is not a prison; it is a grand palace, and the King has left the front doors wide open in loving anticipation of His honored guests arriving.

Verse 74
وَقَالُوا۟ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى صَدَقَنَا وَعْدَهُۥ وَأَوْرَثَنَا ٱلْأَرْضَ نَتَبَوَّأُ مِنَ ٱلْجَنَّةِ حَيْثُ نَشَآءُ ۖ فَنِعْمَ أَجْرُ ٱلْعَـٰمِلِينَ
"And they will joyously say, 'All praise is due to Allah, who has fulfilled His magnificent promise to us and made us inherit the earth, so we may settle in Paradise wherever we please.' And how incredibly excellent is the reward of the [righteous] workers!"
Plain Understanding
As they step inside, the believers will cry tears of joy, saying: "Thank God! He kept every single promise He made to us when we were suffering on earth. Now He has given us this heavenly kingdom to explore freely!" What an amazing paycheck for those who put in the hard work on earth!
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
In the Dunya, land is fought over, inherited with blood, and then ultimately lost to death. In Jannah, the land is inherited through patience, it stretches infinitely, and you can never be evicted. The believers finally exhale, realizing every tear, every late-night prayer, and every suppressed anger was absolutely, beautifully worth it.

Verse 75
وَتَرَى ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةَ حَآفِّينَ مِنْ حَوْلِ ٱلْعَرْشِ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ ۖ وَقُضِىَ بَيْنَهُم بِٱلْحَقِّ وَقِيلَ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَـٰلَمِينَ
"And you will see the angels vividly surrounding the Throne, constantly exalting their Lord with praise. And it will be judged between them in absolute truth, and it will be universally declared, 'All praise is due to Allah, Lord of the worlds.'"
Plain Understanding
The Surah closes with a majestic vision: billions of angels circling the massive Throne of God in brilliant light, singing His praises. Every single dispute has been perfectly resolved. And the final, echoing sound of Judgment Day will be the entire universe crying out in unison: "Praise be to God, the Master of all existence!"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Creation began with the praise of God, and creation ends with the praise of God. The noise of human arrogance, the debating of philosophers, and the screams of the tyrants have all been permanently silenced. The only sound left vibrating through eternity is "Alhamdulillah" (All Praise belongs to God).

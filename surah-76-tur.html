Surah At-Tur

The Mount • Makkah • 49 Verses
An intensely majestic Surah that opens with five divine oaths. It establishes the unshakeable certainty of the Day of Judgment before delivering a devastating logical critique of those who deny the Creator.
Verse 1
وَٱلطُّورِ
"By the mount,"
Plain Understanding
God begins the Surah by swearing a divine oath by "At-Tur." This specifically refers to Mount Sinai, the geographically elevated and sacred location where God spoke directly to Prophet Moses.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi explains the theology of divine oaths: God does not need to swear an oath to validate His truth. He swears by His creations to draw the human intellect's attention to their profound significance. Swearing by Mount Sinai establishes the absolute reality of revelation; just as God's speech to Moses on that mountain was an undeniable historical truth, the coming Day of Judgment is equally true.
Purification of the Self (Al-Gilani)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani reflects that "The Mount" represents the firm, unshakeable foundation of faith. Just as a physical mountain provides stability to the earth, the revelation given to the Prophets provides absolute stability to the human soul against the earthquakes of doubt.

Verse 2
وَكِتَـٰبٍۢ مَّسْطُورٍۢ
"And [by] a Book inscribed,"
Plain Understanding
The second oath is sworn by an inscribed, written scripture. This refers broadly to divine revelation documented by angels, including the Preserved Tablet (Al-Lawh Al-Mahfuz), the Torah given to Moses, and the Qur'an itself.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi notes the progression of the oaths: God first swears by the physical location of revelation (the Mount), and immediately follows it by swearing by the tangible result of that revelation (the inscribed Book). This proves that God's guidance is not a fleeting emotion or abstract philosophy; it is codified, preserved law.

Verse 3
فِى رَقٍّۢ مَّنشُورٍۢ
"In parchment spread open,"
Plain Understanding
The third oath describes the condition of this divine Book. It is not hidden, locked away, or inaccessible. It is explicitly written on "Raqq" (fine parchment) that is spread wide open for anyone who genuinely seeks to read and comprehend the truth.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali teaches that while the physical parchment of the Qur'an is spread open for the eyes to see, the spiritual reality of the Qur'an remains closed until the heart is unrolled and opened through sincere humility. A closed, arrogant heart cannot read an open book.

Verse 4
وَٱلْبَيْتِ ٱلْمَعْمُورِ
"And [by] the frequented House,"
Plain Understanding
The fourth oath is sworn by "Al-Bayt Al-Ma'mur." This refers to the sacred, heavenly sanctuary situated directly above the Kaaba on earth, continuously frequented and populated by angels in perpetual worship.
Historical Context
During the Night Journey (Al-Isra wal-Mi'raj), the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was physically taken to the seventh heaven where he witnessed this exact reality. He described it in an authentic Hadith (Sahih Bukhari): "Then I was shown Al-Bayt Al-Ma'mur (The Frequented House). I asked Gabriel about it and he said, 'This is Al-Bayt Al-Ma'mur where 70,000 angels perform prayers daily; and when they leave, they never return to it (but another 70,000 come into it daily).'"
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah states that God does not command human beings to pray because He needs their worship; He has an infinite, unceasing army of angels fulfilling that role in the heavens. Prayer is strictly a command designed to elevate the human soul from the dirt of the Dunya to the purity of the heavens.

Verse 7
إِنَّ عَذَابَ رَبِّكَ لَوَٰقِعٌۭ
"Indeed, the punishment of your Lord will occur."
Plain Understanding
This is the subject of the previous six oaths. God swears by the holiest mountain, the sacred scriptures, the sanctuary of the angels, the heavens, and the oceans to confirm one absolute, inescapable reality: divine justice and punishment will undeniably strike those who deserve it.
Historical Context
This specific verse had a profound, physical impact on the second Caliph of Islam, Umar ibn al-Khattab. Authentic historical records state that while Umar was patrolling the streets of Medina at night, he passed by a house where a man was reciting Surah At-Tur. When the reciter reached this verse ("Indeed, the punishment of your Lord will occur"), Umar stopped, leaned against a wall in sheer terror, and collapsed. The intense fear of God's account caused him to fall physically ill, and people visited him in his sickbed for nearly a month without knowing the specific cause of his illness. (Tafsir Ibn Kathir / Al-Qurtubi).
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali points out that Umar's reaction is the benchmark of true spiritual hearing. The average person hears this verse as a historical or theological statement. The purified heart hears it as a direct, imminent threat to its own survival, triggering an acute, physical manifestation of fear (Khawf).

"Who are in [empty] discourse amusing themselves."
Plain Understanding

Verse 13
يَوْمَ يُدَعُّونَ إِلَىٰ نَارِ جَهَنَّمَ دَعًّا
"The Day they are thrust toward the fire of Hell with a [violent] thrust, [its angels will say],"
Plain Understanding
The punishment physically mirrors their worldly attitude. Because they treated religion lightly and arrogantly pushed the truth away, they are violently and forcefully shoved ("Da'a") into the fire of Hell by the angels of punishment.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi notes the intensity of the word "Da'a". It is not a gentle push; it is a violent, humiliating thrust on the back of the neck. Those who walked with extreme arrogance on earth, refusing to bow to the Creator, are driven into the fire in the most degrading physical posture possible.

"'This is the Fire which you used to deny.'"
Plain Understanding
As they are thrust into the flames, they are met with a devastating psychological rebuke. The angels explicitly remind them that this agonizing physical reality is the exact concept they used to laugh at and dismiss as a fairy tale during their worldly lives.

Verse 15
أَفَسِحْرٌ هَـٰذَآ أَمْ أَنتُمْ لَا تُبْصِرُونَ
"'Then is this magic, or do you not see?'"
Plain Understanding
The rebuke continues with bitter irony. In the Dunya, whenever the Prophet ﷺ presented them with a miracle or a clear verse, they dismissed it by saying, "This is just magic!" The angels now mock their previous arguments, asking them as they burn: "Is this fire an illusion of magic too? Or are your eyes still blind?"
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali states that the psychological and emotional torment in Hell is often more severe than the physical burning. Being mocked with your own arrogant arguments, while realizing your absolute foolishness when it is too late to fix it, causes the soul to burn with a regret that eclipses physical pain.

Verse 16
ٱصْلَوْهَا فَٱصْبِرُوٓا۟ أَوْ لَا تَصْبِرُوا۟ سَوَآءٌ عَلَيْكُمْ ۖ إِنَّمَا تُجْزَوْنَ مَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ
"'[Enter to] burn therein; then be patient or impatient - it is all the same for you. You are only being recompensed [for] what you used to do.'"
Plain Understanding
The final terrifying reality of Hell is established: the complete futility of human reaction. On earth, patience (Sabr) brings reward and eventual relief. In Hell, patience and panic yield the exact same result. The punishment is the strict, objective consequence of their own deliberate actions on earth, and no emotional reaction will alter the justice enacted upon them.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah draws a profound contrast. In the worldly life, patience is the key to victory; it alters your spiritual trajectory and attracts God's mercy. In the Akhirah (for the condemned), the time for planting seeds has ended. The command to "be patient or impatient" signifies the absolute closure of all spiritual transactions. The time for Sabr was yesterday.

Verse 17
إِنَّ ٱلْمُتَّقِينَ فِى جَنَّـٰتٍۢ وَنَعِيمٍۢ
"Indeed, the righteous will be in gardens and bliss,"
Plain Understanding
In direct contrast to the deniers who are thrust into the Fire, God establishes the destination of the "Muttaqin" (those who maintained a conscious, protective fear of God). Their eternal reality will be characterized by expansive, beautiful gardens and absolute, uninterrupted bliss.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi notes the linguistic precision here. "Jannat" (Gardens) represents the physical, external environment of beauty and provision, while "Na'eem" (Bliss) represents the internal, psychological state of absolute peace and joy. The righteous are granted perfection in both their surroundings and their inner state.

Verse 18
فَـٰكِهِينَ بِمَآ ءَاتَىٰهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ وَوَقَىٰهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ عَذَابَ ٱلْجَحِيمِ
"Enjoying what their Lord has given them, and their Lord protected them from the punishment of Hellfire."
Plain Understanding
Their joy is twofold. First, they are actively delighting ("Fakiheen") in the magnificent provisions God has bestowed upon them. Second, their happiness is infinitely magnified by the profound relief of knowing they were protected from the torment of Hellfire.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali teaches that true joy in Paradise is not merely the consumption of its fruits, but the realization of the Giver. The phrase "what their Lord has given them" implies that the ultimate sweetness of the reward is the direct awareness of God's personal care, love, and protection over them.

Verse 19

The angels, or God Himself, will welcome them with a command of pure hospitality. They are invited to consume the provisions of Paradise with absolute satisfaction ("Hani'an"—meaning without any negative side effects, sickness, or fullness). It is explicitly stated that this royal treatment is the direct result of the righteous efforts they put forth in the worldly life.

Verse 20
مُتَّكِـِٔينَ عَلَىٰ سُرُرٍۢ مَّصْفُوفَةٍۢ ۖ وَزَوَّجْنَـٰهُم بِحُورٍ عِينٍۢ
"They will be reclining on thrones lined up, and We will marry them to fair women with large, [beautiful] eyes."
Plain Understanding
God describes the physical posture of the believers. They are "reclining"—a posture associated exclusively with relaxation, security, and royal authority—on luxurious couches arranged in perfect order. Furthermore, they are granted pure, beautiful, and perfectly matched spouses (Hoor al-'Ayn) for eternal companionship.
Historical Context
In the harsh desert environment of 7th-century Arabia, the concepts of continuous rest, lush gardens, pure water, and majestic thrones represented the absolute pinnacle of unimaginable luxury and royal status, serving as a powerful motivator for the early Muslims enduring severe physical torture.

Verse 21
وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَٱتَّبَعَتْهُمْ ذُرِّيَّتُهُم بِإِيمَـٰنٍ أَلْحَقْنَا بِهِمْ ذُرِّيَّتَهُمْ وَمَآ أَلَتْنَـٰهُم مِّنْ عَمَلِهِم مِّن شَىْءٍۢ ۚ كُلُّ ٱمْرِئٍۭ بِمَا كَسَبَ رَهِينٌۭ
"And those who believed and whose descendants followed them in faith - We will join with them their descendants, and We will not deprive them of anything of their deeds. Every person, for what he earned, is retained."
Plain Understanding
God reveals one of the most magnificent manifestations of His mercy. If a parent attains a high level in Paradise, and their children also die as believers (even if the children's deeds only qualify them for a lower level), God will miraculously elevate the children to the parent's highest level so the family can be reunited. This elevation does not decrease the parent's reward in any way.
Historical Context
According to authentic narrations from Ibn Abbas, this verse was revealed to comfort the believers who worried that the differing levels of Paradise would permanently separate them from their beloved children. God assured them that He would honor the righteous parent by raising their faithful offspring to their level, purely out of Divine generosity.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi points out the profound legal reality established at the end of the verse: "Every person, for what he earned, is retained." While God freely *elevates* the believers' children out of sheer mercy, He never punishes anyone for another's sins. No one is dragged *down* to Hell by their family; justice is strictly individual, but mercy is generously expansive.

Verse 23

"They will exchange with one another a cup [of wine] wherein [results] no ill speech or commission of sin."
Plain Understanding
In their joyful gatherings, they will pass beautifully crafted cups of the wine of Paradise to one another. God explicitly clarifies that this heavenly drink contains none of the negative properties of worldly alcohol; it does not cause intoxication, loss of intellect, foolish speech (Laghw), or sinful behavior (Ta'theem). It provides pure delight without corruption.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah draws a spiritual parallel. Just as the wine of Paradise brings pure joy without the intoxication of sin, the spiritually awakened heart in the Dunya drinks from the cup of Divine Love (Mahabbah)—experiencing profound ecstasy without losing its grounding in the sacred law (Shariah).

"They will say, 'Indeed, we were previously among our people fearful [of displeasing Allah].'"

If a person feels entirely safe and arrogant from God's judgment in the Dunya, they will be struck with paralyzing fear in the Akhirah. But if they maintain a humble, cautious fear of God in the Dunya (as mentioned in this verse), God will grant them absolute, eternal safety in the Akhirah.

Verse 27
فَمَنَّ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيْنَا وَوَقَىٰنَا عَذَابَ ٱلسَّمُومِ
"'So Allah conferred favor upon us and protected us from the punishment of the Scorching Fire.'"
Plain Understanding
The believers do not boast about their own good deeds. They attribute their salvation entirely to God's sheer grace and favor ("Manna"). They recognize that it was not their own perfection, but God's merciful protection that saved them from the intense, penetrating heat of Hellfire.
Historical Context
This verse was deeply beloved by the early Muslims. Masruq narrated that he once visited Aisha (the Prophet's wife) and found her standing in voluntary prayer, continuously repeating this specific verse: "So Allah conferred favor upon us and protected us from the punishment of the Scorching Fire," while weeping profusely. He went to the market, returned later, and found her still standing in the exact same spot, weeping and repeating this verse. (Tafsir Ibn Kathir).

Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani notes that the believers do not say "we used to perform massive deeds," but rather "we used to supplicate Him." This teaches that the highest form of worship is realizing your absolute poverty and continuous need for God's intervention. Du'a is the purest expression of servitude.

Verse 29
فَذَكِّرْ فَمَآ أَنتَ بِنِعْمَتِ رَبِّكَ بِكَاهِنٍۢ وَلَا مَجْنُونٍ
"So remind [O Muhammad], for you are not, by the favor of your Lord, a soothsayer or a madman."
Plain Understanding
God addresses the Prophet ﷺ directly, commanding him to continue his mission of reminding the people regardless of their mockery. God categorically clears the Prophet's name: he is neither a "Kahin" (a fortune-teller who deals in deception) nor a "Majnun" (one whose intellect is compromised). His eloquence and mission are a direct favor from God.
Historical Context
During the height of the Makkan opposition, the elite leaders of Quraysh gathered in "Dar al-Nadwa" (the assembly house) to brainstorm a unified smear campaign against the Prophet to deter pilgrims from listening to him. They debated calling him a poet, a madman, a magician, or a soothsayer. This verse directly addresses and dismantles the specific false titles they attempted to force upon him.

Plain Understanding

Ibn Ata'illah states that the ego will invent a thousand intellectual excuses to justify its spiritual disobedience. The accusation of forgery was not born from textual analysis; it was born from a heart that had already decided to reject submission before the text was even read.

Verse 34
فَلْيَأْتُوا۟ بِحَدِيثٍۢ مِّثْلِهِۦٓ إِن كَانُوا۟ صَـٰدِقِينَ
"Then let them produce a statement like it, if they should be truthful."
Plain Understanding
God issues the ultimate literary and logical challenge. If they genuinely believe that an illiterate human being fabricated this masterpiece of law, theology, and poetry, then they—who are the master poets and orators of Arabia—should easily be able to collaborate and produce a single document that equals it.
Historical Context
This is known as the "Challenge of the Qur'an" (Tahaddi). The Arabs were at the absolute peak of linguistic mastery, regularly holding massive poetry competitions (like at the market of Ukaz). Yet, despite their intense hatred for the Prophet ﷺ and their desperate desire to discredit him, no poet or group of orators in Arabian history was ever able to produce a text that rivaled the structure, depth, and linguistic perfection of the Qur'an. Their silence was the ultimate proof of its Divine origin.

Verse 35
أَمْ خُلِقُوا۟ مِنْ غَيْرِ شَىْءٍ أَمْ هُمُ ٱلْخَـٰلِقُونَ
"Or were they created by nothing, or were they the creators [of themselves]?"
Plain Understanding
The Qur'an presents an unassailable logical trilemma regarding human existence. Either humans sprang into existence from absolute nothingness without a cause, or they engineered and created themselves, or they were created by an external, necessary Creator. Since the first two options violate fundamental laws of logic and physics, the third is the only rational conclusion.
Historical Context
Jubayr ibn Mut'im (prior to his conversion to Islam) traveled to Medina to negotiate for the prisoners captured at the Battle of Badr. He arrived at the mosque while the Prophet ﷺ was leading the Maghrib (sunset) prayer, reciting Surah At-Tur. When the Prophet reached this exact verse, Jubayr later narrated: "My heart almost flew out of my chest. That was the first time faith settled in my heart." The sheer logical force of the verse shattered his pagan beliefs instantly. (Narrated in Sahih Bukhari).
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi emphasizes that this verse is the ultimate textual foundation for the theological science of Kalam (Islamic logic). It utterly dismantles the atheist argument of a causeless universe using foundational rational axioms. Something cannot come from nothing, and something that does not yet exist cannot act to create itself.

Verse 36
أَمْ خَلَقُوا۟ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضَ ۚ بَل لَّا يُوقِنُونَ
"Or did they create the heavens and the earth? Rather, they are not certain."
Plain Understanding
Expanding on the previous argument, God challenges their logic on a cosmic scale. If they recognize they did not create themselves, did they perhaps engineer the massive universe around them? They know they did not. Therefore, their denial of a Creator is not based on intellectual certainty, but on stubbornness and a lack of sincere conviction.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali notes that the human ego shrinks when confronted with the majesty of the cosmos. Acknowledging that you did not create the heavens forces the realization of your own utter insignificance and absolute dependence on the true Sustainer. The ego denies God simply to maintain the illusion of its own importance.

Verse 37
أَمْ عِندَهُمْ خَزَآئِنُ رَبِّكَ أَمْ هُمُ ٱلْمُصَيْطِرُونَ
"Or do they have the depositories [of the provision] of your Lord? Or are they the controllers [of them]?"
Plain Understanding
The interrogation shifts from the origin of creation to sustenance and authority. God asks if these human deniers physically hold the keys to God's infinite treasures, allowing them to distribute wealth, mercy, and prophethood to whomever they please. Or are they the supreme dictators who govern the natural laws of the universe?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah extracts a profound spiritual comfort from this verse: "How can you fear poverty, or fear the oppression of human beings, when the treasuries of the heavens and the earth belong strictly to your Lord? No human has the power to control these depositories or lock them against God's decree."

Verse 38
أَمْ لَهُمْ سُلَّمٌۭ يَسْتَمِعُونَ فِيهِ ۖ فَلْيَأْتِ مُسْتَمِعُهُم بِسُلْطَـٰنٍۢ مُّبِينٍۢ
"Or have they a stairway [into the heaven] upon which they listen? Then let their listener produce a clear authority."
Plain Understanding
God mocks their false claims to spiritual supremacy. If they confidently reject the Prophet's revelation, do they possess their own physical stairway to the heavens where they can eavesdrop on divine decrees to verify their stance? If so, they must produce undeniable, objective proof of what they have heard.

Verse 39
أَمْ لَهُ ٱلْبَنَاتُ وَلَكُمُ ٱلْبَنُونَ
"Or has He daughters while you have sons?"
Plain Understanding
This addresses a specific, highly contradictory belief held by the pagan Arabs. They despised having daughters, yet they hypocritically claimed that the angels were the "daughters of God." God highlights the extreme absurdity of assigning to the Creator what they themselves find disgraceful.
Historical Context
In pre-Islamic Arabia (Jahiliyyah), female infanticide was practiced out of fear of poverty or social dishonor. A man's pride was entirely tied to having sons. Simultaneously, their pagan theology included worshipping angels as female deities, claiming they were the literal daughters of Allah. The Qur'an frequently exposes this deeply flawed and insulting contradiction to shatter their theological framework.

"Or do you, [O Muhammad], ask of them a payment, so they are by debt burdened down?"
Plain Understanding
God provides another logical reason why their rejection is completely unjustified. The Prophet is not demanding any financial compensation, taxes, or wealth in exchange for his guidance. They are not being financially crushed by his message, meaning their refusal to listen is born purely out of unprovoked arrogance.

Verse 42
أَمْ يُرِيدُونَ كَيْدًۭا ۖ فَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ هُمُ ٱلْمَكِيدُونَ
"Or do they intend a plan? But those who disbelieve - they are the object of a plan."
Plain Understanding
If they cannot win the intellectual debate, do they intend to use physical violence and conspiracies to stop the truth? God reassures the Prophet that human plots are entirely encompassed by God's master plan. The disbelievers will ultimately find themselves trapped in their own conspiracies.
Historical Context
This refers to the continuous secret meetings held by the Makkan elite in "Dar al-Nadwa" (the assembly house), where they constantly plotted to boycott, smear, exile, or assassinate the Prophet ﷺ. God's "plan" outmaneuvered them completely, utilizing their own hostility to prompt the Prophet's safe migration to Medina (Hijrah), which ultimately led to the political establishment of Islam and their total defeat.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi clarifies that assigning "a plan" (Kayd) to God is not an attribute of deception. It is an attribute of perfect justice and strategic supremacy. It means God allows the oppressors to build their traps, only to use those very traps to execute their own ruin.

Verse 43
أَمْ لَهُمْ إِلَـٰهٌ غَيْرُ ٱللَّهِ ۚ سُبْحَـٰنَ ٱللَّهِ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ
"Or have they a deity other than Allah? Exalted is Allah above whatever they associate with Him."
Plain Understanding
The fifteen-part interrogation concludes with the ultimate theological question. Is there any other actual God capable of protecting them from the true Creator? The answer is an absolute negative. God declares His supreme purity and elevation above their powerless, fabricated idols.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi notes the brilliant, unyielding structure of these verses (35-43). God launches consecutive questions (all beginning with "Am" - "Or...") that systematically block every possible mental exit the ego could invent. It leaves the sincere intellect with absolutely no room to maneuver, forcing an undeniable surrender to Tawhid (Absolute Monotheism).

Verse 44
وَإِن يَرَوْا۟ كِسْفًۭا مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ سَاقِطًۭا يَقُولُوا۟ سَحَابٌۭ مَّرْكُومٌۭ
"And if they were to see a fragment from the sky falling, they would say, '[It is merely] clouds heaped up.'"
Plain Understanding
God exposes the severe depth of their psychological denial. Their rejection is not due to a lack of evidence. Their hearts are so hardened that even if God dropped a literal, physical piece of the sky to crush them as a miracle or a punishment, they would stubbornly rationalize it away as a naturally occurring, thick cloud formation.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali states that when the heart is diseased with arrogance, the eyes lose their spiritual vision (Basirah). The ego will invent any physical or scientific rationalization to avoid admitting a spiritual truth that demands submission. Miracles cannot cure a willfully blind heart.

Verse 47
وَإِنَّ لِلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا۟ عَذَابًۭا دُونَ ذَٰلِكَ وَلَـٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَهُمْ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ
"And indeed, for those who have wronged is a punishment before that, but most of them do not know."
Plain Understanding
God warns that the oppressors will not have a peaceful wait until Judgment Day. There is a preliminary punishment reserved specifically for them prior to the Hereafter, though the majority of them remain completely ignorant of this impending reality.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi explains that "a punishment before that" refers to two specific historical and spiritual realities. Physically, it foretold their humiliating, devastating defeat at the Battle of Badr and the severe seven-year famine that later struck Mecca. Spiritually, it serves as explicit scriptural proof for the punishment of the grave ('Adhab al-Qabr) that bridges the gap between physical death and the Resurrection.

Verse 48
وَٱصْبِرْ لِحُكْمِ رَبِّكَ فَإِنَّكَ بِأَعْيُنِنَا ۖ وَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ حِينَ تَقُومُ
"And be patient, [O Muhammad], for the decision of your Lord, for indeed, you are in Our eyes. And exalt [Allah] with praise of your Lord when you arise."
Plain Understanding
After addressing the deniers, God turns to His Messenger with an intensely beautiful and intimate command. He instructs the Prophet to bear the hardships with patience, reassuring him with the most comforting phrase: "You are in Our eyes." God then instructs him to maintain his spiritual strength by praising and glorifying the Lord whenever he stands up to pray.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah points out the profound solace in the phrase "For indeed, you are in Our eyes." The pain of any trial vanishes when the servant realizes they are being continuously, lovingly observed and protected by the Beloved. The trial is no longer a punishment, but a sacred space of divine intimacy.
Purification of the Self (Al-Gilani)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani writes that true patience (Sabr) is achieved when one realizes the gaze of the Creator is upon them. When you know the King is watching you endure for His sake, the endurance itself becomes sweeter than comfort.

Verse 49 ۩
وَمِنَ ٱلَّيْلِ فَسَبِّحْهُ وَإِدْبَارَ ٱلنُّجُومِ
"And in a part of the night exalt Him and after [the setting of] the stars."
Plain Understanding
The Surah concludes by detailing the specific times for this intimate worship. The Prophet (and by extension all believers) is directed to glorify God in the depths of the night (Tahajjud prayer) and at the breaking of dawn when the stars fade away (Fajr prayer), cementing the daily cycle of spiritual connection.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali highlights the profound significance of these two times. The night prayer isolates the soul from the noise of creation, allowing pure communion with God. The fading of the stars at dawn symbolizes the fading of the physical world's illusions, reminding the believer that all created lights eventually set, and only the Light of the Creator remains eternal.

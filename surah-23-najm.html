Surah An-Najm

The Star • Makkah • 62 Verses
The Zenith of Revelation. This is the first Surah the Prophet ﷺ recited openly at the Kaaba. It vividly details his breathtaking encounter with Jibreel and his ascension to the boundary of the cosmos, defending his sanity and the absolute purity of the revelation.
Verse 1
وَٱلنَّجْمِ إِذَا هَوَىٰ
"By the star when it descends,"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the star as it sets or falls through the sky.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Arabs relied heavily on stars for navigation in the featureless desert. Allah swears by the cosmic guides to introduce the ultimate human guide. Some scholars say "An-Najm" specifically refers to the Pleiades cluster, while others say it refers to the Quran itself, which descended in parts ("Nujum").
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "star" represents the human intellect. Just as the stars must set and fade when the sun rises, human logic and ego must "descend" and submit when the sun of Divine Revelation appears.

Verse 2
مَا ضَلَّ صَاحِبُكُمْ وَمَا غَوَىٰ
"Your companion has not strayed, nor has he erred,"
Plain Understanding
Muhammad has not lost his way in ignorance, nor has he purposely chosen a wrong path.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah deliberately uses the word "Sahibukum" (Your companion). He is reminding the Quraish: "You have known him his entire life. He grew up among you as the Trustworthy one. He is your close companion; how can you suddenly call him a madman?"
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
There are two ways to fail: "Dalal" (straying out of ignorance) and "Ghawa" (knowing the truth but acting upon desire). The Prophet ﷺ is declared absolutely pure of both intellectual error and moral corruption. He is the perfect vessel.

Plain Understanding

In ancient Arab culture, when two leaders made an unbreakable covenant, they would place their two bows together, overlapping them to signify that they were now one force. The distance signifies unparalleled spiritual intimacy and transmission.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)

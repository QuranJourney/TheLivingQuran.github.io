Surah Al-Muzzammil

The Enshrouded One • Makkah • 20 Verses
The Spiritual Training Camp. Overwhelmed by the early revelations, the Prophet ﷺ wrapped himself in a cloak for comfort. Allah calls him to shed his comfort and stand in the night, building the spiritual fortress required to carry the heavy word of the Quran.
Verse 1
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلْمُزَّمِّلُ
"O you who wraps himself [in clothing],"
Plain Understanding
A direct, gentle address to the Prophet ﷺ, who had wrapped himself in a garment.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Following the first revelations and the hostility of society, the Prophet ﷺ was physically exhausted and sought the comfort of a blanket. Allah calls him not by his name, but by his current physical state, showing immense Divine tenderness.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "cloak" represents the comfort zone of the ego. To walk the path of God, one must shed the wrapping of laziness, fear, and attachment to physical rest.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If you want to know your standing with Him, look at where He has placed you." Allah is about to pull him from sleep to prayer, signifying a high station of intimacy.

Verse 2
قُمِ ٱلَّيْلَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا
"Arise [to pray] the night, except for a little -"
Plain Understanding
A command to stand in prayer for the majority of the night, leaving only a small portion for sleep.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
For the first year of revelation, Tahajjud (Night Prayer) was mandatory for the Prophet ﷺ and the early believers. It was the training ground required before they faced the persecution of Makkah.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The day is for the creation; the night is for the Creator. You cannot receive divine secrets while unconscious. Mujahada (spiritual struggle) against sleep is the key to spiritual awakening.

Verse 3
نِّصْفَهُۥٓ أَوِ ٱنقُصْ مِنْهُ قَلِيلًا
"Half of it - or subtract from it a little"
Plain Understanding
Stand for half the night, or a bit less than half.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The precision of the timing teaches discipline. The soul thrives on structured devotion, not just random bursts of emotion. Sleep is granted its right, but the spirit is given dominance.
Spiritual Practice
Verse 4
أَوْ زِدْ عَلَيْهِ وَرَتِّلِ ٱلْقُرْءَانَ تَرْتِيلًا
"Or add to it, and recite the Qur'an with measured recitation."
Plain Understanding
Or pray slightly more than half, but most importantly: recite the Quran slowly, clearly, and with reflection (Tarteel).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Tarteel means separating the words so the meaning penetrates. The Prophet ﷺ would stop at verses of mercy to ask for it, and verses of punishment to seek refuge.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Reading fast is the desire of the ego to "finish" a task. Tarteel is the desire of the soul to "connect" with the Speaker. Rushing through worship empties it of light.

Verse 5
إِنَّا سَنُلْقِى عَلَيْكَ قَوْلًا ثَقِيلًا
"Indeed, We will cast upon you a heavy word."
Plain Understanding
We are about to give you a massive, weighty revelation.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The "Heavy Word" is the Quran—its laws, obligations, and the spiritual gravity of carrying the final message to humanity. Physically, the Prophet ﷺ would sweat profusely even on cold days when revelation descended.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
A heavy vessel requires a strong foundation. The exhaustive night prayer was not a punishment; it was the strengthening required to ensure the Prophet's heart would not be crushed by the Divine Word.

Verse 6
إِنَّ نَاشِئَةَ ٱلَّيْلِ هِىَ أَشَدُّ وَطْـًٔا وَأَقْوَمُ قِيلًا
"Indeed, the hours of the night are more effective for concurrence [of heart and tongue] and more suitable for words."
Plain Understanding
The deep night is the best time for the heart and tongue to align, and the words spoken then hit deeper.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
During the day, the senses are bombarded, pulling the heart in a thousand directions. At night, the darkness shuts off the physical senses, forcing the spiritual senses to awaken. The silence of the world allows the heart to hear God.

Verse 7
إِنَّ لَكَ فِى ٱلنَّهَارِ سَبْحًا طَوِيلًا
"Indeed, for you by day is prolonged occupation."
Plain Understanding
During the daytime, you have long, continuous duties and struggles.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The daytime was filled with preaching, enduring the attacks of Quraish, and managing the affairs of the few believers.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Balance the Zahir (Outer) and the Batin (Inner). The deep connection built in the isolation of the night empowers the rigorous social action required during the day.

Verse 8
وَٱذْكُرِ ٱسْمَ رَبِّكَ وَتَبَتَّلْ إِلَيْهِ تَبْتِيلًا
"And remember the name of your Lord and devote yourself to Him with [complete] devotion."
Plain Understanding
Constantly remember Allah's name and cut yourself off from everything else to attach solely to Him.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Tabattul" means severing ties. It does not mean monasticism (abandoning society), but severing the heart's reliance on anything other than Allah.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
To truly remember His name, the heart must be emptied of all idols (money, status, fear of people). Devotion means your internal compass always points to Him, even while your hands are busy in the world.
CommandsSpiritual Practice
Verse 9
رَّبُّ ٱلْمَشْرِقِ وَٱلْمَغْرِبِ لَآ إِلَـٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ فَٱتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلًا
"[He is] the Lord of the East and the West; there is no deity except Him, so take Him as Disposer of [your] affairs."
Plain Understanding
He owns all directions and there is no other god. Therefore, make Him your Wakil (Trustee/Manager).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Relieve yourself of the burden of management (Tadbir). What someone else has undertaken on your behalf, do not undertake for yourself." Since He rules the East and West, your anxiety over the future is a lack of trust in His management.

Verse 10
وَٱصْبِرْ عَلَىٰ مَا يَقُولُونَ وَٱهْجُرْهُمْ هَجْرًا جَمِيلًا
"And be patient over what they say and avoid them with gracious avoidance."
Plain Understanding
Endure their insults patiently, and separate yourself from them in a beautiful, non-vengeful way.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The command on how to deal with the psychological warfare of Makkah. "Hajran Jameela" (Gracious avoidance) means walking away without returning insults.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Patience without complaint is true Sabr. Abandoning them physically is easy; abandoning hatred for them in your heart is the "beautiful" part of the avoidance. Protect your inner peace.

Verse 11
وَذَرْنِى وَٱلْمُكَذِّبِينَ أُو۟لِى ٱلنَّعْمَةِ وَمَهِّلْهُمْ قَلِيلًا
"And leave Me with [the matter of] the deniers, those of ease [in life], and allow them respite a little."
Plain Understanding
Allah tells the Prophet ﷺ: "Leave the rich, comfortable deniers to Me. I will deal with them shortly."
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not let the fact that He delays the reward or punishment cause you to doubt His promise." The wealth of the oppressors is not a sign of God's favor; it is Istidraj (a rope given so they may hang themselves).

Verse 12
إِنَّ لَدَيْنَآ أَنكَالًا وَجَحِيمًا
"Indeed, with Us [for them] are shackles and burning fire"
Plain Understanding
Allah has prepared heavy chains and a blazing Hellfire for them.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A direct contrast to the "ease" (Ni'mah) they enjoy in this world. Their worldly freedom to oppress will become physical shackles.

Verse 13
وَطَعَامًا ذَا غُصَّةٍ وَعَذَابًا أَلِيمًا
"And food that chokes and a painful punishment -"
Plain Understanding
They will be fed food that gets stuck in the throat, bringing excruciating pain.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The punishment mirrors the sin. The elite of Makkah gorged on luxuries while starving the poor. Their punishment is food (Zaqqum) that chokes and burns.

Verse 14
يَوْمَ تَرْجُفُ ٱلْأَرْضُ وَٱلْجِبَالُ وَكَانَتِ ٱلْجِبَالُ كَثِيبًا مَّهِيلًا
"On the Day the earth and the mountains will convulse and the mountains will become a heap of sand pouring down."
Plain Understanding
This punishment will occur on the Day when the solid mountains shake and crumble into loose, pouring sand.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Arabs viewed the mountains as the ultimate symbols of permanence and safety. Allah shatters this illusion, showing that nothing in the material world is stable.

Verse 15
إِنَّآ أَرْسَلْنَآ إِلَيْكُمْ رَسُولًا شَـٰهِدًا عَلَيْكُمْ كَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَآ إِلَىٰ فِرْعَوْنَ رَسُولًا

Plain Understanding
We sent Muhammad to you to bear witness over your actions, exactly like We sent Moses to Pharaoh.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Quraish are being compared to Pharaoh. They share his arrogance. The Prophet ﷺ is the witness against them.

Verse 16
فَعَصَىٰ فِرْعَوْنُ ٱلرَّسُولَ فَأَخَذْنَـٰهُ أَخْذًا وَبِيلًا
"But Pharaoh disobeyed the messenger, so We seized him with a ruinous seizure."
Plain Understanding
Pharaoh rejected Moses, so God destroyed him utterly. The warning to Makkah is clear.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
History repeats for those who do not purify their egos. Power and military might (like Pharaoh's) are useless against the Divine Seizure.

Verse 17
فَكَيْفَ تَتَّقُونَ إِن كَفَرْتُمْ يَوْمًا يَجْعَلُ ٱلْوِلْدَٰنَ شِيبًا
"Then how can you fear, if you disbelieve, a Day that will make the children white-haired?"
Plain Understanding
If you reject faith now, how will you protect yourselves on a Day so terrifying it turns children's hair white?
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The horror of the Day of Judgment strips away youth and vitality instantly. The only protection (Taqwa) is built in this life, during the night prayers and righteous deeds.

The terror will be so great that the very sky will split open. Allah's promise of this Day is guaranteed.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
If the massive, terrifying sky cannot withstand the majesty and terror of that Day, how can the fragile human body withstand it without faith?

"He gave you the power of choice so that your servitude may be out of love, not compulsion." The path is open, but the step must be taken by you.

Verse 20
إِنَّ رَبَّكَ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ تَقُومُ أَدْنَىٰ مِن ثُلُثَىِ ٱلَّيْلِ وَنِصْفَهُۥ وَثُلُثَهُۥ وَطَآئِفَةٌ مِّنَ ٱلَّذِينَ مَعَكَ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ يُقَدِّرُ ٱلَّيْلَ وَٱلنَّهَارَ ۚ عَلِمَ أَن لَّن تُحْصُوهُ فَتَابَ عَلَيْكُمْ ۖ فَٱقْرَءُوا۟ مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنَ ٱلْقُرْءَانِ ۚ عَلِمَ أَن سَيَكُونُ مِنكُم مَّرْضَىٰ ۙ وَءَاخَرُونَ يَضْرِبُونَ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ يَبْتَغُونَ مِن فَضْلِ ٱللَّهِ ۙ وَءَاخَرُونَ يُقَـٰتِلُونَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ ۖ فَٱقْرَءُوا۟ مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ ۚ وَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَءَاتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَقْرِضُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا ۚ وَمَا تُقَدِّمُوا۟ لِأَنفُسِكُم مِّنْ خَيْرٍ تَجِدُوهُ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ هُوَ خَيْرًا وَأَعْظَمَ أَجْرًا ۚ وَٱسْتَغْفِرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَّحِيمٌۢ
"Indeed, your Lord knows that you stand [in prayer] almost two thirds of the night or half of it or a third of it, and [so do] a group of those with you... He knows that you will not be able to endure it, so He has forgiven you. So recite what is easy of the Qur'an... And whatever good you put forward for yourselves - you will find it with Allah... And seek forgiveness of Allah. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving and Merciful."
Plain Understanding
Allah sees the immense effort of the Prophet ﷺ and believers. Because He knows people get sick, must travel for work, or fight to defend themselves, He cancels the strict obligation. Now, just pray what is easy, give charity, and ask forgiveness.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse was revealed up to a year after the first 19 verses. It officially abrogated (canceled) the mandatory nature of Tahajjud for the Ummah, turning it into a highly recommended voluntary prayer (Sunnah). The intense "training camp" was complete.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Look at Divine Mercy. Allah demanded rigor to build their spiritual foundation, but once their hearts were pure, He removed the hardship so religion wouldn't become impossible. Religion is fundamentally based on ease (Yusr).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Actions are but lifeless forms whose soul is the secret of sincerity." The amount of time spent in the night prayer was reduced, but the sincerity was preserved. End all actions, no matter how great, with Istighfar (seeking forgiveness), realizing your worship is always imperfect.

Surah Saba

Sheba • Makkah • 54 Verses
The Kingdom of Gratitude. A profound exploration of power and wealth, contrasting the deeply thankful empires of David and Solomon with the arrogant, self-destructing civilization of Saba (Sheba).
Verse 1
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ ٱلَّذِى لَهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَلَهُ ٱلْحَمْدُ فِى ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةِ ۚ وَهُوَ ٱلْحَكِيمُ ٱلْخَبِيرُ
"All praise belongs to Allah, to whom belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth; and to Him belongs all praise in the Hereafter. And He is the Wise, the Acquainted."
Plain Understanding
"The Surah opens with absolute gratitude: All perfect praise belongs entirely to God because He legally owns and controls every single thing in the universe. Not only is He praised here on earth, but all praise in the eternal life to come also belongs to Him alone. He acts with perfect wisdom and is intimately aware of everything."
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This Surah is one of the five Surahs in the Qur'an that begin directly with "Alhamdulillah" (the others being Al-Fatihah, Al-An'am, Al-Kahf, and Fatir). It sets the immediate theological tone: God is not merely the Creator; He is the active, managing Owner. True praise ("Hamd") is the logical response of a sane intellect acknowledging that it exists entirely on the property of the King.

Verse 2

"His knowledge is staggeringly detailed. He knows every single drop of rain and seed that sinks into the soil, every plant and insect that comes out of it, every angel and blessing that comes down from the sky, and every prayer and soul that travels up to it. The Divine Omniscience. "Ma ya'ruju feeha" (what ascends into it). This includes the invisible, weightless ascent of human deeds. Every secret tear you shed in the dark, every silent prayer you make, and every intention you harbor physically ascends through the atmosphere directly to His Throne. Because He tracks the trajectory of every atom, your unseen spiritual struggles are never wasted.

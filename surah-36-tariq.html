Surah At-Tariq

The Nightcomer • Makkah • 17 Verses
The Piercing Reality. This Surah links the brilliant, pulsing stars of the night sky directly to the unblinking, angelic forces monitoring our every action. It is a severe warning against spiritual hypocrisy, detailing the exact Day when all secret motives will be put on trial.
Verse 1
وَٱلسَّمَآءِ وَٱلطَّارِقِ
"By the sky and the night comer -"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the vast heaven and by the entity that arrives suddenly in the darkness of the night.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Tariq" literally means someone who knocks on a door at night. In ancient Arabia, travelers usually arrived at night and would knock to announce their presence. Here, it refers to a cosmic body that "knocks" on the darkness of the sky to make itself known.

Verse 2
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلطَّارِقُ
"And what can make you know what is the night comer?"
Plain Understanding
And what could possibly make you comprehend the sheer majesty of this night-knocker?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Whenever God asks this rhetorical question ("Wa ma adraka"), it is designed to shatter human presumption. You look at the night sky and see dots of light; God is about to reveal that you are looking at forces of unimaginable, terrifying power.

Verse 3
ٱلنَّجْمُ ٱلثَّاقِبُ
"It is the piercing star -"
Plain Understanding
It is a brilliant, pulsing star whose intense light aggressively pierces right through the blackness of the night.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Thaqib" means to drill a hole through something solid. The darkness of space is so dense, yet the light of this star acts like a drill, piercing the veil so it can be seen from Earth.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This physical star is a metaphor for the light of faith. When the heart is plunged into the pitch-black night of sin and depression, a single, sincere flash of Tawheed (Monotheism) acts as an "An-Najm Ath-Thaqib"—piercing the despair and illuminating the soul instantly.

Verse 4
إِن كُلُّ نَفْسٍۢ لَّمَّا عَلَيْهَا حَافِظٌۭ
"There is no soul but that it has over it a protector."
Plain Understanding
This is the reality the oath points to: Every single human soul has a Guardian Angel assigned to watch over it and perfectly record its deeds.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Look at the connection: Just as the piercing star monitors the sky, breaking through the cover of darkness, the assigned angelic watcher (Hafizh) monitors your life, piercing through your closed doors and secret motives. The ego thinks it is alone in the dark; the soul knows the watcher is always there.

Verse 5
فَلْيَنظُرِ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنُ مِمَّ خُلِقَ
"So let man observe from what he was created."
Plain Understanding
If the arrogant human doubts that he will be judged, let him stop and carefully investigate what he was actually made from.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
When you forget your accountability, you become arrogant. The cure for arrogance is biological reflection. Trace the timeline of your own ego backwards until you hit the origin of your physical form. The mirror of biology shatters the illusion of supremacy.

Verse 6
خُلِقَ مِن مَّآءٍۢ دَافِقٍۢ
"He was created from a fluid, ejected,"
Plain Understanding
He was physically formed from a drop of fluid (semen) that bursts forth forcefully.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The human being demands proof of God's power to conquer death, entirely ignoring God's power to conquer non-existence. You came from a rushing, microscopic fluid. How can the byproduct of a fluid challenge the majesty of the Eternal?

Verse 7
يَخْرُجُ مِنۢ بَيْنِ ٱلصُّلْبِ وَٱلتَّرَآئِبِ
"Emerging from between the backbone and the ribs."
Plain Understanding
This fluid emerges from deep within the anatomy, originating from the areas between the spine and the ribcage.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Centuries before the discovery of embryology and the descent of reproductive glands during fetal development, the Quran accurately located the original, deep internal structural origins of human reproductive systems. God proves His intimate knowledge of the human interior.

Verse 8
إِنَّهُۥ عَلَىٰ رَجْعِهِۦ لَقَادِرٌۭ
"Indeed, Allah, to return him [to life], is Able."
Plain Understanding
Undoubtedly, the God who engineered this astonishingly complex biological process the first time is fully capable of bringing the human back to life after death.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
If He can extract life from a seemingly dead fluid in the darkness of the womb, He can extract your soul from the seemingly dead dirt in the darkness of the grave. Despair is an insult to the Creator's capability.

Verse 9
يَوْمَ تُبْلَى ٱلسَّرَآئِرُ
"The Day when secrets will be put on trial,"
Plain Understanding
He will return you to life on the terrifying Day when all hidden motives, secret sins, and private intentions are aggressively audited and exposed.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Tubla" means to test, expose, and completely unravel something to see what it is actually made of. In the Dunya, courts judge the exterior (the action). On Judgment Day, God judges the "Sara'ir" (the deep, innermost secrets of the heart). The hypocrite whose actions looked perfect but whose heart was rotten will be completely ruined.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"If the secrets of the hearts were exposed in this world, people would refuse to bury each other." Be intensely grateful that God covers your sins today, and spend your life begging Him not to lift that cover when the secrets are audited tomorrow.

Verse 10
فَمَا لَهُۥ مِن قُوَّةٍۢ وَلَا نَاصِرٍۢ
"Then man will have no power or any helper."
Plain Understanding
When those secrets are violently exposed, the arrogant man will have absolutely no internal strength to defend himself, and no external ally to rescue him.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The two pillars of worldly confidence are personal capability (Quwwah) and social connections (Nasir). When the veil of the Dunya drops, both pillars instantly turn to dust. The only power left is the mercy you banked through sincere worship.

Verse 11
وَٱلسَّمَآءِ ذَاتِ ٱلرَّجْعِ
"By the sky which returns [rain]"
Plain Understanding
Another oath sworn by the sky that constantly cycles and returns with rainfall.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Raj'" means a continuous, cyclical return. The sky sucks up moisture and faithfully returns it as rain, over and over, sustaining the earth.

Verse 12
وَٱلْأَرْضِ ذَاتِ ٱلصَّدْعِ
"And [by] the earth which cracks open,"
Plain Understanding
And by the earth that splits and cracks open to let the plants grow out of it.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Look at the divine poetry: Just as the sky "returns" rain to crack open the dead earth to bring out living plants, the Creator will "return" your soul (v. 8) to crack open your grave and bring out your living body. Nature is a constant, looping documentary of your own resurrection.

Verse 13
إِنَّهُۥ لَقَوْلٌۭ فَصْلٌۭ
"Indeed, the Qur'an is a decisive statement,"
Plain Understanding
Without a doubt, this Quran is the ultimate, absolute word that clearly separates truth from falsehood.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Fasl" means a blade that cuts cleanly. The Quran is not meant to be passively listened to for its rhythm; it is a surgical blade meant to cut the disease of hypocrisy out of your heart. When the Quran speaks, human debate must end.

Verse 14
وَمَا هُوَ بِٱلْهَزْلِ
"And it is not amusement."
Plain Understanding
This message is not a joke, it is not idle poetry, and it is not a casual topic for entertainment.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The elite of Makkah would hold late-night gatherings where they would casually mock the verses of Hellfire to entertain their guests. God warns them: You are laughing in the face of a loaded gun. This is a matter of eternal life and death.

"Indeed, they are planning a plan,"
Plain Understanding
The deniers are intensely plotting and scheming to destroy this message and assassinate the Prophet.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
They hold their secret meetings in the dark, whispering their conspiracies, completely forgetting that the "Nightcomer" (the piercing star) and the Guardian Angel (v. 4) are actively recording every single syllable they utter.

Verse 16

"But I am planning a plan."
Plain Understanding
But God is actively, perfectly engineering His own unstoppable plan to defeat them.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The ego plots using finite resources (money, swords, influence). God plots using the infinite universe. The ultimate divine "plot" against the arrogant is letting them think they are winning, right up until the exact moment the trap of justice snaps shut around them.

Verse 17
فَمَهِّلِ ٱلْكَـٰفِرِينَ أَمْهِلْهُمْ رُوَيْدًۢا
"So concede to the disbelievers. Let them be for a little while."
Plain Understanding
So be patient with these deniers, O Prophet. Just give them a very short amount of time, and watch what happens.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A chilling, confident conclusion. God tells the Prophet ﷺ to stop stressing over their attacks. "Ruwayda" means just a tiny bit of time. A few years after this revelation, the invincible leaders of Makkah were decisively defeated at the Battle of Badr, and their ultimate Judgment commenced.

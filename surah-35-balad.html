Surah Al-Balad

The City • Makkah • 20 Verses
The Steep Path of the Soul. A profound psychological dissection of human existence. It establishes that life is inherently a state of exhausting struggle (Kabad). The ego tries to escape this by buying status, but true elevation requires the steep, agonizing climb of radical charity and selfless mercy.
Verse 1
لَآ أُقْسِمُ بِهَـٰذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ
"I swear by this city, Makkah -"
Plain Understanding
God swears an undeniable oath by the sacred, highly revered city of Makkah.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Makkah was the heart of Arabia, universally recognized as a sanctuary of peace where bloodshed was forbidden. God swears by this physical center of gravity to draw attention to the ultimate spiritual center of gravity: the human being living within it.
Knowledge & SignsProphetic History
Verse 2
وَأَنتَ حِلٌّۢ بِهَـٰذَا ٱلْبَلَدِ
"And you, [O Muhammad], are free of restriction in this city -"
Plain Understanding
And you, O Prophet, are a resident of this city, yet they treat you as if your blood is lawful to spill.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Hillun" carries a tragic double meaning. It means "you are a lawful resident here," but it also exposes the hypocrisy of the Quraish: they considered Makkah a holy sanctuary where even hunting an animal was a sin, yet they made it "lawful" (Hillun) to persecute, torture, and attempt to assassinate the Prophet ﷺ.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
A profound lesson in spiritual isolation. The Prophet ﷺ was physically inside his hometown, yet he was a complete stranger and an outcast because his heart belonged to the Heavens. The true seeker will often feel like a foreigner in their own city when their soul awakens to God.
Prophetic HistoryTestSteadfastness
Verse 3
وَوَالِدٍۢ وَمَا وَلَدَ
"And [by] the father and that which was born [of him],"
Plain Understanding
An oath by the parent and the offspring they produce.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Scholars interpret this as an oath by the Prophet Abraham (the father) and his descendants (the Arabs of Makkah), or Adam and the entirety of human existence. It establishes the biological chain of humanity that populates the "city."
Knowledge & SignsHuman Nature
Verse 4
لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ فِى كَبَدٍ
"We have certainly created man into hardship."
Plain Understanding
This is the object of the oaths: God explicitly designed the human condition to be a state of continuous, exhausting struggle and toil.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
"Kabad" means severe, friction-filled struggle. You struggled in the darkness of the womb, you struggle to earn a living, you struggle against illness, and you will struggle in the throes of death. The ego's greatest illusion is the belief that it can achieve a frictionless, perfectly comfortable life in the Dunya. Embrace the struggle; it is the anvil upon which your soul is forged.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not be surprised by the occurrence of sorrows as long as you are in this world, for it only manifests what is strictly in accordance with its nature." Pain is not a glitch in the matrix; it is the operating system. When you stop fighting the reality of "Kabad," you finally find peace within it.
Human NatureTestSteadfastness
Verse 5
أَيَحْسَبُ أَن لَّن يَقْدِرَ عَلَيْهِ أَحَدٌۭ
"Does he think that never will anyone overcome him?"
Plain Understanding
Despite this inherent weakness, does the arrogant human actually believe that no one has power over him?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This addresses the physical brutes of Quraish (like Abu Al-Ashaddayn, a man famous for his massive strength). They used their physical power to abuse the weak Muslims, completely deluded into thinking their muscles made them invincible against divine judgment.

Verse 6
يَقُولُ أَهْلَكْتُ مَالًۭا لُّبَدًا
"He says, 'I have spent wealth in abundance.'"
Plain Understanding
He boasts, "I have blown through massive piles of cash!"
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The elite would spend huge amounts of money funding wars against the Prophet ﷺ or throwing massive, wasteful feasts just to build their own worldly reputations. Notice the word "Ahlaktu" (I destroyed/wasted). Deep down, the soul knows that spending money strictly for ego is not an investment; it is the literal destruction of wealth.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The ego seeks to escape the "Kabad" (struggle) of life by buying an identity. It believes that liquidating wealth to impress society will fill the void in the heart. But buying applause only buys heavier chains.

Verse 7
أَيَحْسَبُ أَن لَّمْ يَرَهُۥٓ أَحَدٌ
"Does he think that no one has seen him?"
Plain Understanding
Does he seriously think that God didn't see the dark intentions hidden behind his "generous" spending?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
He spent the money specifically *so* that people would see him. But he entirely forgot the only Witness that actually matters. God doesn't just see the coins leaving the hand; He sees the pride infecting the heart.

Verse 8
أَلَمْ نَجْعَل لَّهُۥ عَيْنَيْنِ
"Have We not made for him two eyes?"
Plain Understanding
Did We not physically design and give him the two eyes he uses to see the world?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
God begins listing the biological miracles He installed in the human body for free, contrasting His supreme generosity with the human's arrogant boasting about spending a little money (v. 6).
Knowledge & SignsHumility
Verse 9
وَلِسَانًۭا وَشَفَتَيْنِ
"And a tongue and two lips?"
Plain Understanding
And did We not grant him the tongue and lips he uses to speak, boast, and articulate his thoughts?
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
You use the tongue God manufactured to articulate your denial of God. The very instrument of your rebellion is a loan from the King. Lips are also the gates of the mouth; God gave you two doors to keep your tongue locked away, yet you constantly leave them open to boast.
Knowledge & SignsSpiritual Practice
Verse 10
وَهَدَيْنَـٰهُ ٱلنَّجْدَيْنِ
"And have shown him the two ways?"
Plain Understanding
And did We not clearly point out to him the two distinct paths of life: the path of good and the path of evil?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Najdayn" refers to two elevated, highly visible roads. God did not hide the truth in obscurity. The path of salvation and the path of destruction are both brightly lit. The failure of the human is not a lack of vision (he has two eyes); it is a lack of willpower.

Verse 11
فَلَا ٱقْتَحَمَ ٱلْعَقَبَةَ
"But he has not broken through the difficult pass."
Plain Understanding
Yet, despite all his boasting and wealth, he completely failed to aggressively push through the steep, uphill obstacle to achieve true goodness.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
"Iqtahama" is a violent, aggressive word. It means to throw yourself into a brutal, terrifying obstacle. True religion is not a casual, comfortable hobby. Overcoming the greed of the ego (the Aqabah) requires a violent, breathless, internal struggle against your own selfishness.
Spiritual PracticeTestHuman Nature
Verse 12
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلْعَقَبَةُ
"And what can make you know what is [breaking through] the difficult pass?"
Plain Understanding
And what will make you realize the magnitude of what this uphill climb actually requires?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pagan elite thought "success" was funding tribal wars. God shatters their paradigm. The true mountain you must climb is not on a battlefield of swords; it is on the battlefield of sociology and economics.
Knowledge & SignsSocial Ethics
Verse 13
فَكُّ رَقَبَةٍ
"It is the freeing of a slave,"
Plain Understanding
The steep climb is taking your beloved wealth and using it to buy a human being's freedom from slavery.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Slavery was the economic engine of the ancient world. Slaves were intensely expensive. To "free a neck" (Fakku Raqabah) meant spending massive capital with zero return on investment, simply to grant dignity to another human. It was the ultimate blow to capitalist greed.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
You cannot break the chains of your own ego until you spend your wealth to break the chains off another human being. Liberation is reciprocal.

Verse 14
أَوْ إِطْعَامٌۭ فِى يَوْمٍۢ ذِى مَسْغَبَةٍۢ
"Or feeding on a day of severe hunger,"
Plain Understanding
Or giving away your food during a time of famine and severe economic depression, when you yourself are terrified of starving.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
It is easy to give away surplus food when your fridge is full. The "Steep Path" (Aqabah) is feeding someone else when the economy has collapsed (Masghabah) and your own Nafs is screaming in panic, "Save it for yourself!" True charity only begins when it hurts to give.
CharityTestSocial Ethics
Verse 15
يَتِيمًۭا ذَا مَقْرَبَةٍ
"An orphan of near relationship,"
Plain Understanding
Specifically, using that food to feed a vulnerable orphan who is also a close relative.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Islam radically prioritizes the structure of charity. It is a tragedy when people donate thousands to distant international charities for the ego boost, while ignoring their own orphaned nephews and nieces struggling in silence. Fix your immediate radius first.
CharitySocial EthicsCommands
Verse 16
أَوْ مِسْكِينًۭا ذَا مَتْرَبَةٍۢ
"Or a needy person in misery"
Plain Understanding
Or lifting up a destitute, homeless person whose extreme poverty has literally left them covered in the dirt of the street.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Dha Matrabah" means "clinging to the dust." The elite boast about their gold (v. 6), refusing to look at the man in the dust. The irony of the arrogant ego is that it refuses to touch the man in the dust, completely forgetting that its own origin (v. 3-4) and its own grave are made of that exact same dust.

Verse 17
ثُمَّ كَانَ مِنَ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَتَوَاصَوْا۟ بِٱلصَّبْرِ وَتَوَاصَوْا۟ بِٱلْمَرْحَمَةِ
"And then being among those who believed and advised one another to patience and advised one another to compassion."
Plain Understanding
And alongside all these social actions, he must have pure faith in God, and constantly advise his community to hold on to patience and spread deep mercy.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Action without faith is empty humanitarianism; faith without action is religious hypocrisy. You must have both. Furthermore, you cannot survive the "Steep Path" alone. "Tawasaw" means a mutual, continuous exchange of encouragement. You must build a community that injects patience into your spine and mercy into your heart when the climb exhausts you.
Spiritual PracticeSocial EthicsSteadfastness
Verse 18
أُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلْمَيْمَنَةِ
"Those are the companions of the right."
Plain Understanding
The people who embrace the struggle, free the slaves, and feed the poor will be the Companions of the Right Hand (the Blessed).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
They gave up the "comfort" of their wealth in the Dunya, so God grants them the ultimate, eternal comfort of Paradise. The right hand signifies success, honor, and receiving the book of deeds with joy.
HopeJannahAccountability
Verse 19
وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ بِـَٔايَـٰتِنَا هُمْ أَصْحَـٰبُ ٱلْمَشْـَٔمَةِ
"But they who disbelieved in Our signs - those are the companions of the left."
Plain Understanding
Conversely, those who arrogantly rejected Our signs and avoided the steep path of charity are the wretched Companions of the Left Hand.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Mash'amah" is associated with the left side, which in ancient Arab culture symbolized disaster, bad omens, and utter ruin. They chose the "easy" path of greed, which inevitably terminates at the cliff of disaster.

Verse 20
عَلَيْهِمْ نَارٌۭ مُّؤْصَدَةٌۢ
"Over them will be fire closed in."
Plain Understanding
Upon them will be a blazing Fire, locked tight and sealed shut over them like a vault.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
They "sealed" their wallets (v. 6) and closed the doors of their hearts to the starving orphan and the man in the dust (v. 14-16). Divine Justice is a perfect mirror: because they sealed off their mercy in the Dunya, God seals the doors of the Fire over them, cutting them off from His Mercy in the Akhirah.

Mushaf: #103 | Chronological: #13

Surah Al-'Asr

The Declining Time • Makkah • 3 Verses
The Formula for Salvation. Imam Al-Shafi'i famously said: "If people only considered this Surah, it would be sufficient for them." It uses the language of commerce and the unstoppable melting of time to prove that all of humanity is bankrupt—except those who fulfill four precise conditions.
Verse 1
وَٱلْعَصْرِ
"By time,"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by Time, specifically the declining time of the late afternoon, or the span of human history itself.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The pagan Arabs used to curse time when calamities hit them, saying "Time has ruined us." Allah swears by Time to elevate its status. Time is not evil; it is a neutral, created masterpiece—a container. What you fill it with determines your fate.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Time is your only true capital. Imagine a man selling ice in the summer market. He cries out, "Have mercy on the one whose capital is melting away!" Every breath you take is a piece of ice melting. Wasting time is the ultimate spiritual suicide.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"He has given you a time that is short, so do not shorten it further by your heedlessness." Do not mourn the loss of wealth, for it can be replaced. Mourn the loss of a moment that passed without the remembrance of God, for it will never return.

Verse 2
إِنَّ ٱلْإِنسَـٰنَ لَفِى خُسْرٍ
"Indeed, mankind is in loss,"
Plain Understanding
Every single human being, without exception, is actively drowning in a state of absolute loss and ruin.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Quraish prided themselves on their legendary winter and summer trade caravans. They considered themselves masters of profit. Allah uses the language of the marketplace (Khusr = bankruptcy/severe loss) to strike at the heart of their worldview: Your trades are making you rich in the Dunya, but leaving you totally bankrupt in the Akhirah.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The ego creates the illusion of "winning." You get a promotion, you buy a house, you feel victorious. But the soul knows that if your capital (Time) is being exchanged for things that will turn to dust, you are suffering an unimaginable, catastrophic loss.

Verse 3
إِلَّا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّـٰلِحَـٰتِ وَتَوَاصَوْا۟ بِٱلْحَقِّ وَتَوَاصَوْا۟ بِٱلصَّبْرِ
"Except for those who have believed and done righteous deeds and advised each other to truth and advised each other to patience."
Plain Understanding
The only way out of bankruptcy requires four conditions: 1) True inner faith, 2) Outer good actions, 3) Actively advising others toward the truth, and 4) Actively advising others to endure with patience.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
This verse shifts salvation from an individual pursuit to a collective one. You cannot just be a "good person" in a cave. "Tawasaw" means mutual, reciprocal advising. The early Muslim community survived the tortures of Makkah precisely because they constantly held each other up with reminders of Truth and Patience.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Faith (Iman) is the root, and righteous deeds (Amal) are the trunk. But a tree must bear fruit for others. Advising society to Truth and Patience is the fruit of a purified heart overflowing with mercy. A true believer cannot bear to see others drowning in "loss" without throwing them a lifeline.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not keep company with anyone whose state does not elevate you, and whose speech does not direct you to God." Tawasaw (mutual advising) requires true spiritual companionship (Suhbah). If your friends do not pull you toward Truth and Patience, they are pulling you toward Loss.

Surah An-Naba

The Great News • Makkah • 40 Verses
An intensely powerful Makkan Surah that confronts the skepticism of the Quraysh by stacking nine undeniable physical realities of the universe to logically prove the absolute certainty of the Resurrection.
Verse 1
عَمَّ يَتَسَآءَلُونَ
"About what are they asking one another?"
Plain Understanding
God opens the Surah with a powerful, rhetorical question directed at the polytheists. He addresses the intense, mocking conversations they are having among themselves regarding the Prophet's message.
Historical Context
When the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) first began preaching openly in Mecca, his core message centered on the Day of Judgment (Resurrection). The concept of humans being rebuilt from dust was completely foreign to the materialistic Quraysh. They would gather in circles around the Kaaba, intensely questioning each other, arguing, and scoffing at this "bizarre" claim. The Qur'an captures and immortalizes their very conversations.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi notes the linguistic tone of "Amma" (About what?). It expresses divine astonishment and severe reprimand. The reality of God's power is so blatantly obvious in the physical universe that their decision to debate and question the Resurrection is treated as a profound intellectual embarrassment.

Verse 2
عَنِ ٱلنَّبَإِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ
"About the great news -"
Plain Understanding
God answers His own question. The subject of their mockery is not a trivial matter; it is "An-Naba' Al-'Azeem"—the monumental, earth-shattering news of the Hereafter, the Resurrection, and the final judgment of all human beings.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali highlights the spiritual priority established here. It is called the "great news" because it is the *only* news that actually matters. Worldly news—stock markets, politics, wars, and even death—is inherently temporary. The news of the Hereafter dictates eternity, yet the tragedy of the human ego is that it treats the ultimate reality as a casual debate topic.

Verse 5
ثُمَّ كَلَّا سَيَعْلَمُونَ
"Then, no! They will come to know."
Plain Understanding
The threat is emphatically repeated to maximize psychological impact and to establish the absolute, unshakeable certainty of the impending event.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah explains that the repetition signifies two distinct, sequential stages of terrifying realization. "They will come to know" first occurs at the exact millisecond of physical death, when the veil is lifted and the soul sees the angels. "Then, they will come to know" occurs on the Day of Resurrection, when the physical punishment officially begins.

Verse 6
أَلَمْ نَجْعَلِ ٱلْأَرْضَ مِهَـٰدًۭا
"Have We not made the earth a resting phase?"
Plain Understanding
God immediately pivots from the unseen reality of the Hereafter to nine massive, visible proofs in the physical world. He begins with the earth, engineered as a "Mihad" (a smoothed-out, comfortable resting place or cradle) specifically designed to sustain human life and civilization.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi notes the profound metaphor in the word "Mihad" (cradle). A cradle is inherently a temporary resting place for an infant; a child eventually grows up and leaves the cradle. By defining the earth as a cradle, God subtly, yet powerfully, reminds humanity that the Dunya is merely a temporary nursery before the true, mature life of the Akhirah begins.

Verse 7
وَٱلْجِبَالَ أَوْتَادًۭا
"And the mountains as stakes?"
Plain Understanding
The second proof focuses on geology. God highlights the mountains, revealing that they function as "Awtad" (deeply driven stakes or pegs). Just as a peg stabilizes a tent in the desert, the deep subterranean roots of mountains stabilize the earth's crust, preventing it from shaking violently under human feet.
Purification of the Self (Al-Gilani)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani draws a spiritual parallel. Just as God created the physical mountains to act as pegs to stabilize the earth's crust, He places Prophets and the sincerely righteous (Awliya) on the earth to act as spiritual pegs. Their presence, prayers, and guidance stabilize society, preventing it from collapsing into absolute moral and ethical chaos.

Verse 8

"And We created you in pairs"
Plain Understanding
The third proof shifts to human biology. God points out that He did not create humanity as an isolated, self-replicating species. He engineered them deliberately in pairs (male and female) to ensure biological reproduction, psychological comfort, and mutual completion.

Verse 9
وَجَعَلْنَا نَوْمَكُمْ سُبَاتًۭا
"And made your sleep [a means for] rest"
Plain Understanding
The fourth proof addresses daily biology. God designed sleep as "Subat" (a complete cutting off). It is an involuntary biological mechanism that forces the human body and mind to completely disconnect from labor, allowing for essential physical repair and psychological rest.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali views sleep as the ultimate daily proof of the Resurrection. Sleep is the "minor death." Every single night, human consciousness is stripped away, the body is paralyzed, and the person loses all control over their existence. Every morning, consciousness is miraculously returned. How can a human experience this literal daily resurrection and still intellectually deny the Great Resurrection?

Just as clothing wraps around a body to conceal and protect it, the darkness of night wraps around the earth, providing privacy, reducing temperature, and triggering the biological signals for rest.

Verse 16

"And gardens of entwined growth."
Plain Understanding
The final result of the water is the production of dense, lush, and thickly entwined gardens and orchards ("Alfafa"), showcasing the magnificent, life-giving capability of the Creator.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi concludes this section by explaining the master strategy of the Qur'an's logic. God stacked these nine massive, undeniable physical realities—the earth, the mountains, human reproduction, sleep, night, day, the strong heavens, the blazing sun, and the resurrection of dead crops via rain—as premises for one inescapable logical conclusion: The Creator who effortlessly manages and synchronizes all of these massive systems is undeniably capable of orchestrating the Day of Judgment and resurrecting a human from dust. Denying the Hereafter requires denying basic logic.

Verse 17
إِنَّ يَوْمَ ٱلْفَصْلِ كَانَ مِيقَـٰتًۭا
"Indeed, the Day of Judgement is an appointed time -"
Plain Understanding
God transitions directly from the flawless physical engineering of the universe to its ultimate conclusion. The Day of Resurrection is referred to as "Yawm al-Fasl" (The Day of Decision/Separation). It is not a random occurrence; it is a "Miqat"—a strictly scheduled, mathematically precise deadline decreed by the Creator.
Historical Context
The elite of Quraysh would frequently mock the Prophet ﷺ by demanding, "If this Day is real, exactly when is it?" This verse responds to their mockery by affirming that the date is meticulously set and scheduled by God, even if the exact hour remains hidden from human knowledge to preserve the integrity of the spiritual test.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Imam Al-Qurtubi notes the profound theological weight of the name "Yawm al-Fasl" (The Day of Separation). It is the absolute final day where God permanently severs truth from falsehood, the oppressed from the oppressor, and the believer from the disbeliever. The temporary mixing and moral ambiguities of the worldly life are decisively terminated.

Verse 18
يَوْمَ يُنفَخُ فِى ٱلصُّورِ فَتَأْتُونَ أَفْوَاجًۭا
"The Day the Horn is blown and you will come forth in multitudes"
Plain Understanding
The physical mechanism that triggers this Day is revealed. A single, catastrophic blast from the Horn (blown by the Angel Israfil) will compel every deceased human being to resurrect from the dust and gather before God in massive, distinct crowds ("Afwaja").
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Imam al-Ghazali reflects on the concept of these "multitudes." In the Dunya (worldly life), people group themselves based on tribal lineage, corporate wealth, or political affiliation. On the Day of Judgment, these earthly groups dissolve completely. People will be gathered in "Afwaja" strictly according to their spiritual state and the leaders they followed—the people of truth grouped with the Prophets, and the oppressors grouped with the tyrants.

Verse 20
وَسُيِّرَتِ ٱلْجِبَالُ فَكَانَتْ سَرَابًۭا
"And the mountains are removed and will be [but] a mirage."
Plain Understanding
The geological anchors of the earth are destroyed. The massive mountains, which God previously described in Verse 7 as stabilizing "stakes" or pegs, will be entirely uprooted, pulverized into dust, and blown away. To an observer, their scattered remains will look weightless, appearing as nothing more than an optical illusion or a mirage.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Ibn Ata'illah extracts a vital spiritual lesson: "If the greatest, heaviest, and most permanent physical structures of the earth—the mountains—are reduced to a mere mirage before the majesty of God's decree, how can your fragile human ego believe it possesses any permanent weight, power, or stability? Anchor yourself in the Creator, not in the creation."

Verse 21
إِنَّ جَهَنَّمَ كَانَتْ مِرْصَادًۭا
"Indeed, Hell has been lying in wait"
Plain Understanding
God defines the terrifying nature of Hellfire. It is not a passive location or an empty prison cell. It is described as a "Mirsad"—an active, highly alert place of ambush. It is a trap meticulously prepared, continuously watching, and eagerly waiting to capture its intended targets.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi explains the theological reality of "Mirsad" (an ambush point). According to Islamic eschatology, the bridge of Sirat is suspended directly over the roaring fires of Hell, and every single human being must attempt to cross it. Because everyone must pass directly through its airspace, Hell is literally lying in wait for every soul, actively ambushing and pulling down those whose earthly sins cause them to slip.

Verse 23
لَّـٰبِثِينَ فِيهَآ أَحْقَابًۭا
"In which they will remain for ages [unending]."
Plain Understanding
The duration of their torment is established. They will not be punished and then released. They will remain trapped inside for "Ahqab"—massive, successive, and unending eras or epochs of time.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Qurtubi)
Al-Qurtubi clarifies the classical definition of "Ahqab." A single "Huqb" is a vast, nearly immeasurable period of time (often calculated by early scholars as 80 or more years according to the time scale of the Hereafter, where one day equals 1,000 earthly years). The use of the plural indicates an infinite loop: whenever one agonizing epoch finally concludes, another entirely new epoch of punishment immediately begins.

Despite the agonizing heat, they will be completely denied the two most basic physical comforts required for survival: they will not feel a single cool breeze to relieve their burning skin, nor will they receive a single drop of refreshing liquid to quench their severe dehydration.

"Except scalding water and [foul] purulence -"
Plain Understanding
The only liquids available to them are horrific instruments of torture. When their thirst becomes unbearable, they are given "Hameem" (water boiling so fiercely that it destroys the internal organs upon consumption) and "Ghassaq" (the freezing, foul-smelling discharge, sweat, and pus weeping from the wounds of the inhabitants of Hell).

Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani observes the profound spiritual symmetry of this punishment. In the Dunya, these oppressors eagerly drank deeply from the toxic, foul desires of the ego and the world, violently rejecting the pure, refreshing water of divine revelation. Therefore, in the Akhirah, their eternal provision is transformed into the literal, physical manifestation of the toxicity they chose to consume.

"Indeed, they were not expecting an account"

The expectation of the "Hisab" (Divine Account) is the primary spiritual bridle that restrains the ego from crossing into tyranny and oppression. When accountability is removed, the human descends into pure monstrosity.

Verse 30
فَذُوقُوا۟ فَلَن نَّزِيدَكُمْ إِلَّا عَذَابًۭا
"So taste [the penalty], and never will We increase you except in torment."
Plain Understanding
The section concludes with the ultimate, terrifying declaration of divine despair. The angels (or God Himself) order the inhabitants of Hell to taste the agonizing fruit of their actions. They are given a permanent, unbreakable guarantee: as eternity passes, the only thing that will ever increase is the severity of their punishment.
Historical Context
It is widely recorded from classical scholars like Hasan al-Basri, and companions like Abdullah ibn Amr, that this specific verse is universally considered the most severe, frightening, and absolute verse in the entire Qur'an regarding the inhabitants of Hell. It completely and permanently slams the door shut on any shred of hope for relief, a pause in punishment, or divine mercy.

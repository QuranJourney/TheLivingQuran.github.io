Surah 'Abasa

He Frowned • Makkah • 42 Verses
The Divine Reprimand. Revealed when the Prophet ﷺ frowned at a blind man while preaching to the elite. It establishes a radical restructuring of human value: sincerity over status, ending with the terrifying reality of the Day of Judgment where all worldly ties shatter.
Verse 1
عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّىٰٓ
"He frowned and turned away"
Plain Understanding
He [the Prophet] contracted his face into a frown and physically turned his body away.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Notice Allah uses the third person ("He frowned") instead of the second person ("You frowned"). This is out of profound Divine courtesy and love for the Prophet ﷺ, sparing him the immediate harshness of a direct address in the moment of reprimand.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
A frown is a physical manifestation of an internal contraction (Qabd). Even the greatest of creation was corrected for a micro-expression, teaching us that spiritual etiquette (Adab) extends to the very muscles of our face.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
God corrected His most beloved not out of anger, but to perfect his character. The reprimand of the Beloved is a sign of His extreme care for your spiritual rank.

Verse 2
أَن جَآءَهُ ٱلْأَعْمَىٰ
"Because there came to him the blind man,"
Plain Understanding
He frowned because a blind man approached him and interrupted him.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The blind man was Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum. Because he was blind, he could not see that the Prophet ﷺ was deeply engaged in a critical conversation with the tribal chiefs of Quraish (like Utbah bin Rabi'ah and Abu Jahl). He kept excitedly interrupting, saying, "O Messenger of Allah, teach me from what Allah has taught you!"
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
Physical blindness is a veil over the eyes, but spiritual blindness is a veil over the heart. The blind man had seeing faith, while the seeing chiefs had blind hearts. The ego judges by the eyes; the soul judges by the heart.

"But what would make you perceive, [O Muhammad], that perhaps he might be purified,"
Plain Understanding
How do you know? Perhaps this blind man is the one whose soul will actually be cleansed by your teachings.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Allah shifts to the second person ("you"), addressing the Prophet ﷺ directly now. The Prophet's intention was pure: he wanted the chiefs to convert to save the Muslims from torture. But Allah teaches him that human strategy must never override immediate spiritual care.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"Do not look down upon the seeker, for you do not know the hidden treasures God has placed in his heart." The true investment is in the soil that is ready to absorb the water, not the hard rock of the elite.

Plain Understanding

Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The source of every disobedience... is satisfaction with the self." When a person feels "free of need," their spiritual cup is full of themselves; there is no room left to pour in the Divine Light.

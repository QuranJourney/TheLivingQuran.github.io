Surah 'Abasa

He Frowned • Makkah • 42 Verses
The Priority of the Broken Heart. Revealed when the Prophet ﷺ frowned at a blind man while preaching to the elite, delivering a gentle but firm divine correction to the Prophet ﷺ. It establishes a radical restructuring of human value: that in the scales of heaven, a single sincere, broken heart outweighs the wealth and power of the entire world.
Verse 1
عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّىٰٓ
"He frowned and turned ˹his attention˺ away,"
Plain Understanding
The revelation opens gently in the third person, describing a brief moment The Prophet ﷺ frowned and turned his face away in frustration during an interaction.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
The Prophet ﷺ was urgently preaching to the chieftains of Quraysh (like Utbah ibn Rabi'ah and Abu Jahl). His intention was purely selfless; he believed that if these power-brokers embraced Islam, the violent persecution of the weak Muslims in Makkah would finally end.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata'illah)
"Your desire that people know your spiritual rank is a proof of your lack of sincerity." The elite demanded exclusive attention, refusing to sit with the poor. God intervenes immediately to destroy this classist arrogance, refusing to let the pure message be tailored to the egos of the wealthy.

Verse 2
أَن جَآءَهُ ٱلْأَعْمَىٰ
"˹simply˺ because the blind man came to him ˹interrupting˺."
Plain Understanding
This slight shift in attention occurred because a blind man, unable to see the critical meeting taking place, approached to ask a question.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The blind man was Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum (RA). Because he could not see who the Prophet ﷺ was speaking to, he interrupted repeatedly, saying, "O Messenger of Allah, teach me from what Allah has taught you." The Prophet ﷺ frowned purely out of the anxiety of losing the chieftains' attention.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Notice the tender irony: Abdullah lacked physical sight, but his heart was overflowing with spiritual vision. The chieftains had perfect physical eyesight, but their souls were completely blind. The ego prioritizes the outward form; God prioritizes the inward reality.

"You never know ˹O Prophet˺, perhaps he may be purified,"
Plain Understanding
A loving correction: the true potential of a human being is hidden. The one you turned away from was actively seeking the purification of his own soul.
Divine Wisdom (Al-Jilani)
"Do not look down upon anyone, for the secret of God’s friendship (Wilayah) is hidden in His creation." You cannot weigh a person's worth by their societal influence. A single tear of sincerity from a beggar is heavier in the heavens than the gold of kings.

Verse 4
أَوْ يَذَّكَّرُ فَتَنفَعَهُ ٱلذِّكْرَىٰ
"or he may be mindful, benefitting from the reminder."
Plain Understanding
Allah continues to emphasize the missed opportunity. If the Prophet ﷺ had turned his attention to the blind man, the man would have internalized the divine reminder (Thikra), and that reminder would have yielded immediate, practical, and eternal benefits for his soul.
Historical Context (Ibn Kathir)
After this revelation, whenever the Prophet ﷺ saw Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum, he would spread his own cloak out for him to sit on and say, "Welcome to the one for whom my Lord corrected me!" This beautiful humility is the ultimate proof of the Prophet's ﷺ magnificent character.
PURIFICATION OF THE SELF (AL-GILANI)
Sheikh Abdul Qadir al-Gilani reflects on the duty of the spiritual guide (Murshid). The purpose of revelation is transformation, not mere intellectual transmission. The poor man came seeking medicine for a bleeding soul, while the rich men were only interested in debating the ingredients. The true physician must always attend to the dying patient, not the arrogant spectator.

Verse 5

"As for the one who was indifferent,"
Plain Understanding
Regarding the arrogant elite who act as though they are completely self-sufficient and feel no need for divine guidance...
Historical Context (Al-Tabari)
The word 'Istaghna' means to consider oneself free of need. The Makkan chieftains believed their wealth and tribal armies made them invincible. They listened to the Prophet ﷺ not out of a desire to learn, but to debate, mock, and negotiate power.
Purification of the Self (Ibn Ata'illah)
"The source of every disobedience, indifference, and passion is satisfaction with one's self." When a cup is completely full of ego, it is impossible to pour the water of divine grace into it. Their "indifference" was the very disease that made them spiritually unreachable.

Historical Context

Divine Wisdom (Al-Ghazali)
"Do not waste the medicine of the soul on those who refuse to admit they are sick." The Prophet's ﷺ overwhelming empathy made him grieve for those destroying themselves, but God is teaching him to direct his healing light to the soil that is actually ready to grow.

"even though you are not to blame if he would not be purified."
Plain Understanding
You are only the messenger; it is not your fault, nor are you held accountable, if an arrogant person actively chooses to remain spiritually filthy.
Historical Context
The Prophet ﷺ was desperate for their conversion, fearing he would be held responsible if society perished. God clarifies the absolute legal boundary of prophethood: The Prophet is not accountable for the results. If the elite choose to remain spiritually filthy, no blame falls on the Messenger. His duty is solely transmission, not forced conversion.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
We often carry the crushing weight of trying to 'fix' people who do not want to be fixed. God relieves the Prophet ﷺ of this heavy burden. You can present the truth beautifully, but only the Owner of Hearts can turn the key. Surrender the outcome to Him.

For the rest of his life, whenever Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum entered the room, the Prophet ﷺ would literally spread his own cloak on the ground for the blind man to sit on, smiling and saying: "Welcome to the one for whom my Lord reprimanded me!" The Prophet later honored him by leaving him in charge of the entire city of Medina on multiple occasions when the army marched out.

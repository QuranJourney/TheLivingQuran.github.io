Surah Al-Qari'ah

The Striking Calamity • Makkah • 11 Verses
The Cosmic Scales. A deeply terrifying depiction of the exact moment the Day of Judgment strikes, shattering the physical world and reducing humanity to a state of blind panic. It ends with the absolute finality of the Divine Weighing: only spiritual mass can save you from the Abyss.
Verse 1
ٱلْقَارِعَةُ
"The Striking Calamity -"
Plain Understanding
The sudden, universally devastating, and loudly striking disaster.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Al-Qari'ah" comes from the root *qar'a*, meaning to strike something violently so that it makes a horrific, terrifying noise (like a hammer striking an anvil). It refers to the exact moment the Trumpet is blown, shattering the eardrums and striking terror directly into the hearts of all creation.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
There is a cosmic Qari'ah that destroys the world, and a personal Qari'ah that destroys your ego. The spiritual striking of the heart happens when the reality of death and absolute accountability finally pierces the heedless illusions of the Dunya.

Verse 2
مَا ٱلْقَارِعَةُ
"What is the Striking Calamity?"
Plain Understanding
What exactly is this massive, world-ending disaster?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The repetition is designed to induce absolute awe and fear. You think you know what disaster is because you have seen worldly earthquakes and wars, but this question shatters your assumptions. This is an event completely outside of human reference.

Verse 3
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا ٱلْقَارِعَةُ
"And what can make you know what is the Striking Calamity?"
Plain Understanding
And what could possibly make you comprehend the true, unimaginable magnitude of this striking event?
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Just like in Surah Al-Qadr, whenever Allah says "Wa ma adraka" (And what can make you know?), He is declaring that the reality of the subject is so colossal that it fundamentally breaks the limits of human cognition and language.

Verse 4
يَوْمَ يَكُونُ ٱلنَّاسُ كَٱلْفَرَاشِ ٱلْمَبْثُوثِ
"It is the Day when people will be like moths, dispersed,"
Plain Understanding
On that Day, all human beings will be scattered in mass confusion, blindly bumping into each other like a swarm of panicked moths.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Moths are weak, chaotic insects that blindly fly directly into the fire that kills them. Similarly, in the terror of the Resurrection, humans will run aimlessly, entirely stripped of their worldly coordination, intellect, and ranks.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
In the Dunya, human beings arrogantly organize themselves into strict hierarchies: CEOs, kings, and elites. On this Day, all artificial structures collapse instantly, reducing the most powerful tyrant into a fragile, panicked insect swarming in the dust.

Verse 5
وَتَكُونُ ٱلْجِبَالُ كَٱلْعِهْنِ ٱلْمَنفُوشِ
"And the mountains will be like wool, fluffed up."
Plain Understanding
And the massive, unbreakable mountains will be blown apart, floating around like brightly colored, weightless tufts of wool in the wind.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The mountains are the anchors of the earth—the ultimate physical symbols of permanence, majesty, and unshakeable strength. If the solid rock is reduced to weightless, floating fluff by the roar of the Calamity, what do you think will happen to your fragile flesh and bone?

Verse 6
فَأَمَّا مَن ثَقُلَتْ مَوَٰزِينُهُۥ
"Then as for one whose scales are heavy [with good deeds],"
Plain Understanding
Then, out of that chaos, the person whose scales of righteous deeds weigh heavily and pull down the balance.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Mawazin" represents the literal Divine Scales established to weigh human actions. God does not just *count* deeds; He *weighs* them. A million prayers done for showing off weigh less than a feather. A single date given in secret out of pure love for God weighs heavier than Mount Uhud.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
What gives a deed "mass" on the Divine Scale? It is the sheer presence of the heart and the absolute sincerity of Tawheed (La ilaha illallah) attached to the action. A heart heavily anchored in the Creator will anchor the scales.

Verse 7
فَهُوَ فِى عِيشَةٍۢ رَّاضِيَةٍۢ
"He will be in a pleasant life."
Plain Understanding
That person will live an eternally happy, pleasing, and deeply satisfying life in Paradise.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
The Arabic word "Radiyah" operates on two levels: the life itself is deeply pleasing, and the person living it is entirely pleased with God. True wealth is achieving an eternal state of Rida (contentment) where you are permanently safe from the fear of loss.

Verse 8
وَأَمَّا مَنْ خَفَّتْ مَوَٰزِينُهُۥ
"But as for one whose scales are light,"
Plain Understanding
But as for the person whose scale of good deeds pops up because it is terribly light and empty.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Their scale is light not necessarily because they didn't do anything, but because they brought nothing of *spiritual* value. Worldly wealth, status, political power, and corporate fame have absolutely zero mass on the scales of the Hereafter.

Verse 9
فَأُمُّهُۥ هَاوِيَةٌۭ
"His refuge will be an abyss."
Plain Understanding
His ultimate home, tightly embracing him, will be the deep, plunging, bottomless pit of Hell.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
"Umm" literally translates to "Mother," and "Hawiyah" means a steep, plunging pit. It is a terrifying linguistic irony: just as a mother tightly embraces her child and gives it a home, the roaring pit of Hellfire will become the only "mother" that holds the sinner.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
A child runs to its mother for safety when it is scared. Because this soul ran away from God and sought "safety" in the Dunya, its ego, and its sins, its "mother" in the Hereafter becomes the pit of fire. You are ultimately embraced by exactly what you cultivated in your heart.

Verse 10
وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا هِيَهْ
"And what can make you know what that is?"
Plain Understanding
And what could possibly make you comprehend the sheer horror of what this abyss actually is?
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Once again, human imagination totally fails. You have seen worldly fire, you have felt a burn on your finger, but you have absolutely no reference point for the intensity, depth, and psychological terror of the Divine punishment.

Verse 11
نَارٌ حَامِيَةٌۢ
"It is a Fire, intensely hot."
Plain Understanding
It is a violently blazing, intensely scorching, roaring Fire.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Prophet ﷺ said that the regular fire of this physical world is merely one-seventieth (1/70th) of the heat of the fire of Hell. "Hamiyah" means it has been heated to the absolute maximum extreme limit. It is a fire fueled by the unrepentant arrogance of humanity.

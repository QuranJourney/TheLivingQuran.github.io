Surah Al-Qalam

The Pen • Makkah • 52 Verses
Revealed when the elite of Makkah launched a psychological war against the Prophet ﷺ, calling him a madman. This Surah is Allah’s direct defense of His Beloved, defining true sanity, great character, and the anatomy of arrogance.
Verse 1
ن ۚ وَٱلْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ
"Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe,"
Plain Understanding
The Surah begins with the disjointed letter Nun, followed by a Divine Oath sworn by the "Pen" and everything that is recorded.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Pen refers to Al-Qalam Al-A'la (The Supreme Pen), the first creation commanded to write destiny. It establishes the sanctity of literacy immediately after the command to "Read" in Surah Al-Alaq.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The spiritual aspirant must "record" their spiritual states (ahwal) with gratitude; otherwise, they vanish. The heart must have no will but Allah's, just as the pen has no will of its own.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
"The hearts of free men are the tombs of secrets." The letter Nun is a secret. We respect the Unseen (Ghayb) just as we respect the Manifest (Zahir).
Knowledge & SignsThe Unseen
Verse 2
مَآ أَنتَ بِنِعْمَةِ رَبِّكَ بِمَجْنُونٍ
"You are not, by the favor of your Lord, a madman."
Plain Understanding
Because of the blessing (Revelation) bestowed upon you, you are absolutely not insane.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The Quraish launched a psychological war, calling the Prophet ﷺ "Majnun" (possessed). Allah negates this, linking his perfect sanity to the Ni'mah (Favor) of Allah.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
When you detach from the world, the world calls you crazy. Focus on the "Favor of your Lord," not people's insults.

"And indeed, for you is a reward uninterrupted."
Plain Understanding
You will receive a reward that never ends and is never cut off.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
This teaches Steadfastness (Istiqama). The reward is "uninterrupted" because the Prophet’s patience was uninterrupted. Consistency is key.

Verse 4
وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ
"And indeed, you are of a great moral character."
Plain Understanding
You possess the highest, most tremendous standard of character and ethics.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Aisha (RA) said: "His character was the Quran." He was the living embodiment of the Divine Will.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The goal of Sufism is Takhalluq (adopting the traits of Allah). Great Character means giving to those who deprive you, and forgiving those who wrong you.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
Good character is the vessel that allows you to carry His Light. You cannot carry heavy revelation with weak character.

Verse 7

Part 2: The Parable of the Garden (Verses 17-33)
Verse 17
إِنَّا بَلَوْنَـٰهُمْ كَمَا بَلَوْنَآ أَصْحَـٰبَ ٱلْجَنَّةِ إِذْ أَقْسَمُوا۟ لَيَصْرِمُنَّهَا مُصْبِحِينَ
"Indeed, We have tested them as We tested the companions of the garden, when they swore to cut its fruit in the morning."
Plain Understanding
Allah compares the test of Makkah to farmers who swore to harvest entirely in secret.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
A father in Yemen used to give a portion to the poor. When he died, his sons decided to harvest early to bypass the poor.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
Highlights the danger of Greed (Shuhh). Viewing the poor as a liability instead of a means of purification invites destruction.

"Without making exception."
Plain Understanding
They did not say "Insha'Allah" (If Allah wills).
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
When you plan without Him, you are implicitly saying you don't need Him. Certainty in your own power is a delusion.

"So there visited it a visitation from your Lord while they were asleep."

Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)

Part 3: The True Judgment (Verses 34-52)
Verses 34-35
إِنَّ لِلْمُتَّقِينَ عِندَ رَبِّهِمْ جَنَّـٰتِ ٱلنَّعِيمِ • أَفَنَجْعَلُ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ كَٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ
"Indeed, for the righteous with their Lord are the Gardens of Pleasure. Then will We treat the Muslims like the criminals?"
Plain Understanding
It is illogical for God to treat those who submit (Muslims) the same as those who rebel (Criminals).
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The elites of Makkah claimed: "Even if there is an afterlife, we will be richer than you there too!" Allah destroys this arrogant assumption.

"What is [the matter] with you? How do you judge? Or do you have a scripture... Or do you have covenants with Us...?"
Plain Understanding
Allah rhetorically dismantles their arguments. They have no scripture, no divine promise, and no partner-gods to save them.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)

Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Because they refused to prostrate voluntarily in this world when they were healthy, they will be physically unable to do so when they finally want to.

Verses 51-52
وَإِن يَكَادُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَيُزْلِقُونَكَ بِأَبْصَـٰرِهِمْ لَمَّا سَمِعُوا۟ ٱلذِّكْرَ وَيَقُولُونَ إِنَّهُۥ لَمَجْنُونٌ • وَمَا هُوَ إِلَّا ذِكْرٌ لِّلْعَٰلَمِينَ
"And indeed, those who disbelieve would almost make you slip with their eyes when they hear the message, and they say, 'Indeed, he is mad.' But it is not except a reminder to the worlds."
Plain Understanding
The enemies look at you with such intense hatred and envy they almost strike you down with their eyes (Evil Eye). But this Quran is a global reminder.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
They tried to use the "Evil Eye" against the Prophet ﷺ to harm him physically when psychological warfare failed. Allah protected him.
Purification of the Self (Al-Jilani)
The Surah ends exactly how it began: negating the charge of madness. The world will always call the Truth "madness," but to the awakened soul, it is the only true "Reminder."

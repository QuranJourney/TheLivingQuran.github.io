Surah Al-Mursalat

The Emissaries • Makkah • 50 Verses (Part 1: 1-24)
The Unstoppable Decree. Opening with oaths upon the violent forces of nature and the angels, this Surah drives home a terrifying, ten-fold refrain warning the deniers of the inescapable reality of Divine Judgment.
Verse 1
وَٱلْمُرْسَلَـٰتِ عُرْفًۭا
"By those [winds] sent forth in gusts"
Plain Understanding
An oath sworn by the winds that are unleashed in continuous, successive waves.
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
Scholars have two main interpretations for these opening verses: they either refer to the physical winds of nature, or the spiritual "winds"—the angels descending successively with revelation. Both are forces sent by God that cannot be stopped by human technology.
Purification of the Self (Al-Ghazali)
The "winds" represent the continuous, flowing waves of Divine inspiration and mercy that sweep over the awakened heart, washing away the stagnant air of heedlessness.

Verse 2
فَٱلْعَـٰصِفَـٰتِ عَصْفًۭا
"And the winds that blow violently"
Plain Understanding
And by the hurricane-force winds that roar and destroy everything in their path.
Divine Wisdom (Ibn Ata’illah)
God is the Lord of the gentle breeze (v. 1) and the violent hurricane (v. 2). He controls both His Beauty (Jamal) and His Majesty/Wrath (Jalal). Do not be so comforted by the breeze that you forget He can unleash the hurricane.

Verse 49
وَيْلٌۭ يَوْمَئِذٍۢ لِّلْمُكَذِّبِينَ
"Woe, that Day, to the deniers."
Plain Understanding
Absolute destruction and misery on that Day to those who called the truth a lie!
Historical Context (Al-Qurtubi)
The **Tenth** and final refrain. Woe to the one who thought bowing in prayer was a humiliation, completely unaware that refusing to bow guarantees the ultimate humiliation of Hell.

Verse 50
فَبِأَىِّ حَدِيثٍۭ بَعْدَهُۥ يُؤْمِنُونَ
"Then in what statement after the Qur'an will they believe?"
Plain Understanding
If they refuse to believe in this incredibly clear, overwhelming, and majestic Quran, what message could possibly ever convince them?

The Surah ends on a profound note of finality. The Quran is the ultimate intervention. If the "winds sent forth," the destruction of past nations, the miracle of embryology, and the terrifying warnings of Judgment do not wake the heart, the heart is legally dead. There is no other speech coming to save them.
